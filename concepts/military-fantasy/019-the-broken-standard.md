# The Broken Standard

## Genre
Military Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A deserter who fled a massacre must return to the army that branded him traitor—because they're the only ones who can stop what's coming.

## Expanded Premise
Sergeant Elric Thane ran from the Battle of Ashenmoor when his commanding officer ordered him to slaughter surrendered prisoners. The army branded him coward; his family disowned him; he survived as a caravan guard, telling himself honor mattered more than reputation. Now something has crawled from the Ashenmoor—a horde of creatures born from the blood of those murdered prisoners, seeking vengeance on the army that created them. Elric is the only survivor who knows what happened that day, the only one who can explain what the creatures want. He must return to the army that wants him hanged and convince them to follow a deserter against an enemy they created—or watch everyone die for a crime they won't admit.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Elric Thane
**Age:** 34
**Occupation / Background:** Former sergeant of the Bronze Lions, now caravan guard, once considered a rising star in the kingdom's military
**Core skill:** Tactical intuition combined with moral clarity—he knows not just how to win fights but which fights are worth winning
**Fatal flaw:** His conviction in his own righteousness prevents him from acknowledging when others might have perspectives worth considering
**Character arc:** Believes he was entirely right to desert; learns that being right doesn't mean he made no mistakes; becomes someone who can hold complexity without losing conviction
**Voice and register:** Bitter and defensive, waiting to be attacked. When he forgets to guard himself, a passionate idealism shows through.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Bloodborn, creatures spawned from the massacre at Ashenmoor—not quite undead, not quite spirits, fueled by vengeance
**Goal:** To destroy everyone connected to the massacre—officers, soldiers, their families, anyone who benefited from the war
**Method:** Overwhelming numbers, immunity to conventional weapons, and the psychological horror of facing victims of your own crimes
**Connection to protagonist:** Elric's desertion technically saved him from creating any Bloodborn—but his unit killed dozens; he carries their guilt by association

## Supporting Characters

**General Marcus Vorn** — The commander who ordered the massacre, now leading the defense, forced to work with the man who exposed his crime
**Corporal Sera Mist** — A young soldier who wasn't at Ashenmoor but believes in the army; she represents what Elric lost
**The Broken Man** — A prisoner who survived the massacre, now half-Bloodborn himself, who holds the key to stopping them

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Kingdom of Valdris, a Germanic-inspired realm with a proud military tradition
**Time period:** High medieval equivalent, with knight orders and professional armies
**Atmosphere:** Forests of black pine under gray skies. Fortresses that were proud now feel like tombs. The rain never stops. Old battlefields whisper.
**Key locations:** The caravan routes where Elric hides; Ironhold fortress where the army is based; Ashenmoor battlefield, now a blighted wasteland; the Command Tent where politics are as deadly as battle; the Last Bridge where the final stand occurs

## Central Conflict
Elric must convince an army to trust a deserter while working alongside the war criminal he despises, fighting an enemy that embodies the guilt everyone refuses to acknowledge.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The Bloodborn are laid to rest, the truth about Ashenmoor comes out, and the army is forced to reckon with its crimes
**If the protagonist fails:** The Bloodborn destroy the kingdom's military, spread to civilian populations, and demonstrate that some sins cannot be escaped
**Personal stakes:** Elric gets vindication or learns that vindication was never the point

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Elric's caravan is attacked by something inhuman; he recognizes the unit insignia—these were the prisoners at Ashenmoor
- Ch 2 — The Pull: A royal messenger finds him with a pardon and a summons; the army is being destroyed; they need him
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Elric arrives at Ironhold as the Bloodborn assault begins; facing General Vorn is almost as hard as facing the monsters

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Elric's tactical knowledge saves a battalion; the soldiers start listening; Vorn seethes but needs him
- Ch 5 — The Push: They discover that Bloodborn can be destroyed with weapons forged from Ashenmoor iron; hope rises
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The Bloodborn focus on Elric specifically—they want him to acknowledge what his unit did; his righteousness cracks
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Elric meets the Broken Man, learns that some victims became monsters by choice; innocence and guilt blur
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Elric confronts Vorn privately; Vorn confesses—not guilt but reasoning; Elric realizes evil is rarely simple

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The army makes its stand at the Last Bridge; Elric and Vorn fight side by side; the Broken Man sacrifices himself to call the Bloodborn home
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The Bloodborn fade; Vorn is quietly arrested; Elric is offered his rank back but chooses something else—he'll tell the true story

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Facing consequences of past atrocities
**Deep theme:** Being right about something doesn't make you right about everything, and righteousness can become its own form of blindness
**Thematic question:** How do you work with people whose crimes you know, when the alternative is letting worse crimes happen?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Ashenmoor Vision**: Elric experiences the massacre from the victims' perspective, feeling what his unit did
2. **The Ironhold Siege**: Bloodborn climb the walls like a tide, soldiers recognizing victims they personally killed
3. **The Bridge of the Fallen**: Armies face off across a narrow bridge while Elric negotiates with the dead

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Revenant (2015), Apocalypse Now (1979)
**Book comparables:** The First Law trilogy by Joe Abercrombie, Best Served Cold by Joe Abercrombie

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Grim and morally complex, with no easy heroes. The pacing alternates between military action and political/psychological drama. The horror of the Bloodborn should be sad as much as scary—these were people. The relationship between Elric and Vorn is the emotional core—mutual loathing forced into cooperation.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Elric's voice is tense and guarded, loosening only when discussing tactics. Germanic-inspired names: Elric (EL-rik), Vorn (VORN), Valdris (VAL-dris). The Bloodborn should sound layered—many voices at once, all in pain. Battle scenes need confusion and horror. Vorn should sound reasonable, educated—evil should not sound evil.
