# The Blood Feather War

## Genre
Military Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
An Aztec battlefield healer must lead the last warriors when the death god escapes and turns all the empire's armies into his own.

## Expanded Premise
Itzali Teyacapan is a Flower Healer, one of the sacred medics who retrieve the wounded from battlefield sacrifices. She has never held a weapon. When the priest-king's ritual goes wrong, the death god Mictlantecuhtli breaks free from the underworld—and every warrior killed in the god's name across five centuries rises as his army. Within days, the empire's greatest forces become its greatest threat. Itzali survives only because healers are not sacrificed. Now she must gather the unlikely survivors—farmers, artisans, the cowardly, the disgraced—and find a way to fight an army of the dead led by a god. The living have never faced odds like this. The dead have forever.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Itzali Teyacapan
**Age:** 26
**Occupation / Background:** Flower Healer of the Eagle Order, trained in battlefield medicine and sacred retrieval of the sacrificed
**Core skill:** Understanding of human anatomy and medicinal magic—she knows exactly where bodies break and how to break them
**Fatal flaw:** She took vows never to cause harm; violence makes her physically ill even when necessary
**Character arc:** Believes healing is the highest calling and violence corrupts the soul; learns that sometimes the healer must become the sword; becomes someone who can kill while remaining who she was
**Voice and register:** Gentle and precise, the voice of someone who talks people through pain. Under pressure, the gentleness calcifies into terrible focus.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Mictlantecuhtli, Lord of the Dead, the god who rules the underworld, now walking the mortal realm
**Goal:** To end the cycle of death and rebirth by making all living things dead—in his view, this is mercy, ending suffering forever
**Method:** Commands the risen dead of five centuries, including the greatest warriors in history; corruption spreads from his presence
**Connection to protagonist:** Itzali's brother was sacrificed last year; he now serves in the god's army; she will have to face him

## Supporting Characters

**Tezoc the Craven** — A warrior who fled from his first battle and was shamed; his cowardice kept him alive; he becomes Itzali's war-chief
**Citlali** — A young obsidian-crafter who can make weapons that harm the dead, the empire's most valuable artisan
**The Obsidian Mirror** — A priest who survived the botched ritual, half-mad, who knows how to trap the god again

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Triple Alliance Empire, an Aztec-inspired realm of floating gardens, pyramid temples, and constant sacred warfare
**Time period:** Height of the Mesoamerican classical period, with blood magic and living gods
**Atmosphere:** The air smells of marigolds and copal incense, now mingling with decay. Pyramids loom over canals filled with floating corpses. Drums that once called warriors to glory now beat from skeletal hands.
**Key locations:** The Temple of the Sun where the ritual failed; the floating city of Texcoco where survivors gather; the Obsidian Quarry where weapons are forged; the Gate of Bones where Mictlantecuhtli entered; the Temple of the Morning Star where he can be bound

## Central Conflict
Itzali must lead the unwarlike against an infinite army of dead warriors and the god who commands them—finding a way to win without becoming a monster, when every instinct says healing cannot survive contact with war.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The god is re-bound, the dead return to rest, and the empire can rebuild with a new understanding of sacrifice
**If the protagonist fails:** All life in the known world ends, joining the dead god's eternal kingdom
**Personal stakes:** Itzali must face her dead brother in battle; killing him may be the only way to free him

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Itzali retrieves wounded from a battle as usual—then the dead rise and slaughter the living; she barely escapes
- Ch 2 — The Pull: The capital falls; Itzali flees with refugees to Texcoco; she realizes the survivors look to her because everyone else is dead
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Dead warriors assault Texcoco; Itzali uses her medical knowledge to direct defense; they survive but she must kill for the first time

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Citlali creates obsidian weapons that harm the dead; Tezoc begins training civilians; Itzali learns war
- Ch 5 — The Push: A successful raid on the Obsidian Quarry provides weapons; the survivors become an army; hope rises
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Itzali's brother leads the dead against Texcoco; she cannot fight him; the city is sacked
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Survivors scatter; Itzali is alone, wounded, cursing the gods; she nearly gives up
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: The Obsidian Mirror reveals the binding ritual requires a sacrifice—someone must choose to die and hold the god in the underworld

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The assault on the Temple of the Morning Star; Itzali leads the last survivors against the god himself
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The ritual succeeds; Itzali's brother chooses to be the sacrifice, finding peace; Itzali survives to heal a broken world

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Fighting an impossible enemy
**Deep theme:** Sometimes the healer's path requires passing through the valley of death
**Thematic question:** Can someone remain good while doing terrible things, and where is the line?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Capital Falls**: The moment the dead rise during the festival, pyramids running with blood, the god walking through screaming crowds
2. **The Siege of Texcoco**: Civilian defenders holding canals and causeways against an endless tide of dead warriors
3. **The Brother's Choice**: Itzali fights her risen brother while the ritual burns around them, his death becoming sacrifice

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Apocalypto (2006), Army of Darkness (1992)
**Book comparables:** Gods of Jade and Shadow by Silvia Moreno-Garcia, The Fifth Season by N.K. Jemisin

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Combines Mesoamerican mythology with horror-action momentum. The first third is survival horror—running, hiding, witnessing atrocity. The middle becomes military organization and grim preparation. The final act is mythological warfare at full scale. The tone treats the culture with respect while embracing the supernatural chaos. Violence is ritualistic and meaningful.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Itzali's voice should be warm but becomes harder through the story. Nahuatl-inspired names: Itzali (eet-ZAH-lee), Mictlantecuhtli (meek-tlahn-teh-KOOT-lee), Texcoco (tesh-KOH-koh). The death god should be voiced slowly, with eternal patience. Ritual terminology should be delivered with reverence. Battle sounds include drums and conch shells.
