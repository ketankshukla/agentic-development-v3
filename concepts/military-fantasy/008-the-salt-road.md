# The Salt Road

## Genre
Military Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A quartermaster must keep an army alive through an impossible desert crossing while saboteurs within destroy supplies each night.

## Expanded Premise
Kofi Mensah built the greatest supply system in the Ashanti Empire's history—ten thousand soldiers could march anywhere with full bellies. When General Yaa commands a surprise invasion across the Burning Sea desert, Kofi must move two months of food and water through an environment that kills within days. The mathematics barely work. Then supplies start disappearing: poisoned wells, burnt wagons, scattered livestock. Someone in the army wants this campaign to fail—but accusation without proof would shatter morale. Kofi must find the traitor while keeping the army alive, knowing that if he fails, twenty thousand soldiers die of thirst before ever seeing battle.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Kofi Mensah
**Age:** 45
**Occupation / Background:** Chief Quartermaster of the Golden Army, former merchant turned military logistician
**Core skill:** Mathematical genius for supply logistics—he can calculate consumption rates, spoilage, and optimal distribution faster than scribes can write
**Fatal flaw:** He sees people as numbers; individual suffering doesn't register until it accumulates into statistics
**Character arc:** Believes efficiency is the highest virtue; learns that logistics is ultimately about keeping specific people alive; becomes someone who counts individuals, not units
**Voice and register:** Precise and numerical, thinks in tallies and percentages. Speaks rapidly when engaged with a problem, barely acknowledges people when calculating.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Prince Kwadwo Asante, the Emperor's nephew, who commands the cavalry and is secretly negotiating with the enemy
**Goal:** To ensure the campaign fails and General Yaa dies in disgrace, clearing his path to power through a peace negotiated at the empire's expense
**Method:** Agents among the porters sabotage supplies while Kwadwo publicly supports the campaign and gains glory in minor engagements
**Connection to protagonist:** Kwadwo dismissed Kofi as "a shopkeeper in soldier's clothes" at a war council—he never considered the quartermaster worth watching

## Supporting Characters

**General Yaa Abrafi** — The empire's greatest warrior, a legend who understands battlefields but treats logistics as beneath her attention
**Sergeant Efua Darko** — Kofi's most trusted subordinate, a former thief who now guards supplies, fiercely loyal
**The Sand Speaker** — A desert hermit who knows the old water sources, whose help comes with strange prices

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Burning Sea, a vast salt desert separating the Ashanti Empire from its rival, the Oyo Confederacy
**Time period:** Early modern West African-inspired era, with bronze weapons and elaborate kingdoms
**Atmosphere:** White salt flats stretch to infinity, shimmering in heat. The sun murders without mercy. Mirages promise water that doesn't exist. At night, the desert freezes. Vultures follow the army, patient.
**Key locations:** Fort Kwame (the staging base); the First Oasis (sabotaged); the Bone Road (ancient path marked by skeletons); the Salt Citadel (enemy fortress); the Hidden Spring (where everything changes)

## Central Conflict
Kofi must solve a mathematical impossibility—feeding an army through death itself—while hunting a traitor he cannot openly accuse and keeping a proud general from discovering how close to death her army truly is.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The army reaches its target, wins the war, and Kofi transforms military logistics forever
**If the protagonist fails:** Twenty thousand die in the desert, the empire loses a generation of soldiers, and the enemy conquers for a decade
**Personal stakes:** Kofi must prove that "shopkeepers" matter more than warriors—or die as a footnote in someone else's defeat

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Kofi presents his supply plan to the war council; General Yaa approves the desert crossing; Kofi begins preparations knowing the margin is razor-thin
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Three days into the march, the First Oasis is found poisoned; Kofi realizes someone is actively sabotaging the campaign
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Kofi quietly investigates and finds a dead saboteur—but the man was killed before he could talk; the conspiracy is larger than one traitor

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Rationing begins; Kofi discovers Prince Kwadwo's supply wagons are mysteriously untouched; suspicion grows but proof remains absent
- Ch 5 — The Push: The Sand Speaker guides them to a hidden water source; Kofi's strict rationing keeps the army alive; morale rises
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Half the remaining supply train burns in a single night; Kofi's calculations now show only sixty percent will survive to reach the enemy
- Ch 7 — The Descent: General Yaa discovers the true numbers and nearly collapses; soldiers begin dying of thirst; Kofi faces the reality that some must die for others to live
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Sergeant Efua captures a saboteur who names Prince Kwadwo; Kofi must choose between exposing treason and causing the army to splinter mid-desert

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Kofi exposes Kwadwo as the army reaches the Salt Citadel; Kwadwo's cavalry deserts; the remaining forces must take an impregnable fortress with half their strength
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Kofi finds a way to use the citadel's own water supply against it; the fortress falls; he returns home as a hero but haunted by those he couldn't save

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The logistics of desert warfare
**Deep theme:** Invisible work sustains everything—glory is built on the backs of those who count beans
**Thematic question:** Is there heroism in mathematics, and can numbers ever justify sacrifice?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Burning Wagons**: A ring of supply wagons catches fire at night while Kofi organizes bucket chains, flames visible for miles
2. **The Bone Road March**: The army walks past ten thousand skeletons from a failed expedition, a visceral reminder of what failure looks like
3. **The Water Siege**: Kofi uses the citadel's aqueducts to flood the enemy's own chambers, water becoming a weapon

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Lawrence of Arabia (1962), Dunkirk (2017)
**Book comparables:** The Fifth Season by N.K. Jemisin, Black Leopard, Red Wolf by Marlon James

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Tense and claustrophobic despite the vast setting—every scene should convey the mathematics of death closing in. The pacing accelerates as resources dwindle, each chapter representing shrinking margins. The emotional climax is not the battle but the moment Kofi must decide who lives and who dies. The tone respects the horror of preventable death.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Kofi's internal monologue should be rapid and numerical, almost like counting. Akan-inspired names: Kofi (KOH-fee), Yaa (YAH), Kwadwo (KWAH-jo). The desert itself should be narrated as an enemy—constant, patient, deadly. Distinguish the Sand Speaker with an old, rhythmic voice. Soldiers' dying should be handled with dignity, not spectacle.
