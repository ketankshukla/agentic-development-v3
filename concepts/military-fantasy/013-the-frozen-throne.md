# The Frozen Throne

## Genre
Military Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A disgraced prince must unite the feuding mountain clans before an avalanche of ice demons buries them all.

## Expanded Premise
Prince Dorje Namgyal was exiled from the Himalayan kingdom of Narkang for refusing to execute prisoners. For three years he survived as a caravan guard, while at home his brother ruled with increasing cruelty. Now the Ice Demons have awakened—ancient beings of cold and hunger that pour from the glaciers every thousand years. The king is dead, killed in the first assault, and his brother's army is scattered. Dorje must return to a kingdom that hates him, unite clans that have feuded for centuries, and hold the mountain passes against an enemy that brings winter itself as a weapon. He has twenty thousand refugees behind him and an army of maybe three thousand. The demons have never been stopped—only survived.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Dorje Namgyal
**Age:** 31
**Occupation / Background:** Exiled prince, former military commander, now caravan guard and occasional bandit
**Core skill:** Mountain warfare mastery—he knows every pass, every weather pattern, every way the terrain can kill you
**Fatal flaw:** He refuses to sacrifice anyone, even when the mathematics of war demands it
**Character arc:** Believes that a ruler who sacrifices his people doesn't deserve rule; learns that sometimes sacrifice is necessary but the ruler must bear the weight; becomes a king who spends lives carefully rather than refusing to spend them
**Voice and register:** Quiet and observant, speaks with the economy of someone who survived by not being noticed. His royal training emerges in moments of crisis—suddenly commanding.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Frost Lords, a council of five ancient ice demons, each commanding a different aspect of cold—wind, snow, ice, void, and hunger
**Goal:** To reclaim the mountains as their eternal kingdom, driving all warm-blooded life below the tree line
**Method:** Supernatural storms, ice creatures, animated avalanches, and the simple reality that humans cannot survive extreme cold
**Connection to protagonist:** The last time the Frost Lords rose, Dorje's ancestor made a bargain that bound them—a bargain Dorje's brother broke with his cruelty, releasing them

## Supporting Characters

**Tashi Dolma** — A warrior-nun from the high monastery, deadly with blade and prayer, who becomes Dorje's war leader
**Lord Karma Sherpa** — Head of a rival clan that murdered Dorje's grandfather; they must now fight as allies
**The Oracle of the Snows** — A child who speaks for the mountain itself, whose guidance is essential but whose visions are maddening

## Setting

**Primary location:** Narkang, a kingdom based in the Himalayan high plateaus
**Time period:** Medieval Tibetan-inspired era, with Buddhist and Bon magical traditions
**Atmosphere:** Prayer flags snap in howling wind. Snow blankets everything, deeper each day. The mountains that were home become a frozen hell. Silence is broken only by distant avalanches and closer screams.
**Key locations:** The exile's camp at the Low Pass; the fortress-monastery of Tengri Dzong; the Sacred Lake now frozen solid; the Ancestor's Cave where the original bargain was struck; the High Throne Pass where the final stand must be made

## Central Conflict
Dorje must unite clans that would rather kill each other than cooperate, while leading them against an enemy that commands the weather itself and cannot be defeated—only outlasted.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The clans survive, a new kingdom emerges from the disaster, and the Frost Lords are bound for another millennium
**If the protagonist fails:** Everyone above the tree line dies, the kingdom becomes a frozen wasteland, and the demons expand their domain
**Personal stakes:** Dorje can redeem his exile and prove his way was always right—or die confirming his brother's assessment that he was too weak to rule

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Dorje sees refugees streaming down the passes; a survivor tells him the king is dead and the demons have risen
- Ch 2 — The Pull: A delegation from the clans finds him—not to honor him, but because no one else is left; Dorje accepts leadership
- Ch 3 — First Contact: First engagement with ice creatures; Dorje's mountain knowledge saves them, but the cold itself is the enemy

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: The clans gather at Tengri Dzong; old feuds flare; Lord Karma Sherpa nearly kills Dorje in a duel of honor
- Ch 5 — The Push: Dorje's strategy of fighting retreat and controlled engagement works; they buy time; the monastery holds
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The Frost Lords freeze the Sacred Lake and march across; the monastery's outer walls fall; thousands die
- Ch 7 — The Descent: The Oracle reveals that rebinding requires a royal sacrifice; Dorje's surviving brother (crippled and penitent) offers himself
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Dorje refuses to let his brother die alone; they reconcile and plan together; the final stand is chosen

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The Battle of High Throne Pass—humans against elemental cold, while Dorje and the Oracle perform the binding ritual
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The brother completes the sacrifice; the demons are bound; Dorje is king of a diminished but united kingdom

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Survival against nature's fury
**Deep theme:** True leadership means being willing to sacrifice yourself, not just others
**Thematic question:** What makes someone fit to rule—the willingness to make hard choices, or the refusal to accept easy ones?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Frozen Monastery**: Ice creatures assault the ancient walls while monks chant and warriors fight in howling snow
2. **The Sacred Lake Crossing**: The Frost Lords walk across the frozen surface, frost spreading from their feet, an army of cold behind them
3. **The Royal Sacrifice**: Dorje's brother walks into the binding circle as the ritual completes, chosen rather than discarded

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Revenant (2015), The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers (Helm's Deep)
**Book comparables:** Empire of Sand by Tasha Suri, The Wolf of Oren-Yaro by K.S. Villoso

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Builds from desperate survival to organized resistance to mythological confrontation. The cold itself should be a constant presence—breath frosting, fingers numbing, the simple act of staying warm requiring constant effort. Pacing alternates between slow frozen tension and explosive action. The tone is somber and spiritual, with the mountains treated as sacred spaces.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Dorje's voice is quiet and measured, warming when he speaks to his people. Tibetan-inspired names: Dorje (DOR-jay), Namgyal (NAM-gyal), Tengri (TENG-ree). The Oracle speaks in a child's voice with ancient weight. Frost Lords should sound like wind through cracks—inhuman but not monstrous. Describe cold's physical effects—it should feel uncomfortable to listen to.
