# Storm Surge

## Genre
Action-Adventure

## One-Line Premise
A former Coast Guard rescue swimmer must save her father from a Caribbean island prison as a Category 5 hurricane makes direct impact—with twelve hours before the storm surge drowns everyone inside.

## Expanded Premise
Lieutenant Commander Maya Baptiste gave up her rescue swimmer career after a failed mission killed three colleagues. Now she's a civilian helicopter pilot in Puerto Rico, flying supplies to remote islands. When a Category 5 hurricane changes course toward the private island of El Refugio—home to an illegal black-site prison where her father has been held without trial for three years—Maya realizes no one is coming to evacuate the prisoners. The corrupt warden is leaving them to drown. Maya must fly into the teeth of the worst storm in a decade, breach a prison designed to prevent escape, and get her father and the other prisoners out before the storm surge floods the facility to the ceiling.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Lieutenant Commander Maya Baptiste (ret.)
**Age:** 35
**Occupation / Background:** Former Coast Guard rescue swimmer and helicopter pilot, now flying civilian supply runs across the Caribbean. Born in Haiti, raised in Miami, trained at Aviation Training Center Mobile.
**Core skill:** Elite water rescue and helicopter operation in extreme conditions—she's flown through hurricanes before, and she's pulled people from drowning when others said it was impossible.
**Fatal flaw:** Guilt that manifests as risk aversion. After the mission that killed her colleagues, she stopped taking any risk that might harm others—which has kept her from acting when action was needed.
**Character arc:** Believes that her failure killed people, so she's never allowed to fail again—which means never trying. Discovers that refusing to act is its own kind of failure. Ends willing to risk again.
**Voice and register:** Precise and professional, Creole phrases emerging under stress. She talks through procedures out loud—a habit from training that helps her focus.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Hurricane Maria Dos, a Category 5 storm with sustained winds of 180 mph, storm surge projected at 20 feet, making direct impact on El Refugio.
**Goal:** The storm has no goal—it's pure physics. But Warden Carlos Estrada, who runs El Refugio for unnamed government interests, plans to evacuate his staff and leave the prisoners to die, destroying evidence of the black site's existence.
**Method:** The hurricane provides perfect cover. No witnesses, no survivors, no evidence. Natural disaster as mass murder.
**Connection to protagonist:** Maya's father, a journalist investigating black sites, was disappeared three years ago. She's known he was alive but couldn't prove where—until a dying prisoner's confession revealed El Refugio.

## Supporting Characters

**Henri Baptiste** — Maya's father, a Haitian-American investigative journalist whose reporting on Caribbean black sites made him a target. He's survived three years of isolation through sheer will.
**Warden Carlos Estrada** — Former Cuban intelligence, now private contractor running the black site. He's not sadistic—just absolutely amoral about his work.
**Dr. Rosa Méndez** — Prison doctor who's been secretly documenting abuses. She's Maya's inside contact and the reason Maya knows the evacuation is happening.
**Tomás Guerrero** — Mexican prisoner, former pilot, whose flight skills make him essential if Maya's helicopter is damaged.
**Lieutenant Javier Santos** — Coast Guard officer who was Maya's closest friend. He still believes in her skills and provides unofficial support from the mainland.

## Setting

**Primary location:** El Refugio, a private island prison off the coast of Puerto Rico, twenty miles from any other land, directly in the path of a Category 5 hurricane.
**Time period:** Present day, hurricane season—the specific date chosen because a storm this powerful creates the perfect cover for what Estrada has planned.
**Atmosphere:** The building pressure before a major hurricane—sky yellowing, birds gone silent, sea withdrawing before the surge. Then the impact: wind that screams, rain that hammers horizontal, darkness pierced only by lightning. The prison itself: concrete and steel, designed to contain, filling slowly with water.
**Key locations:** Maya's helicopter base in San Juan (staging), the flight path through the storm's outer bands (approach), El Refugio prison complex (primary action), the flooding cell blocks (rescue), the helicopter pad (escape).

## Central Conflict
Maya must fly into a Category 5 hurricane, land on an island that's being evacuated by everyone except the prisoners, breach a facility designed to prevent escape, free her father and as many prisoners as possible, and fly back out through the storm—all before the surge drowns everyone and wipes away the evidence.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Her father and other prisoners survive to testify about the black site. The program is exposed. Those responsible face accountability.
**If the protagonist fails:** Forty prisoners drown, including her father. The black site is never acknowledged. She adds forty more deaths to her conscience.
**Personal stakes:** Maya either proves that her skills weren't the problem—that the mission that killed her colleagues was impossible, not her failure—or she dies believing she was always the one who couldn't get it done.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Maya monitors Maria Dos's track change and realizes El Refugio is in the direct path. She contacts Dr. Méndez, who confirms: Estrada is evacuating staff only. Prisoners will die. Maya's father will die.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Maya tries official channels—Coast Guard, authorities, media. No one can reach the island in time, and no one with authority believes her about the black site. Javier tells her it's not possible. She tells him possible isn't the point.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Maya takes off into the storm's outer bands, flying alone in a helicopter rated for conditions half this severe. The approach nearly kills her twice. She lands on El Refugio as the storm's eye approaches.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: The prison is chaos—staff evacuating, prisoners locked in cells, water already rising in the lower levels. Maya finds Dr. Méndez and together they locate Henri. He's alive but weak. The reunion is thirty seconds because the surge is coming.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Maya and Henri work through the cell blocks, freeing prisoners who can help free others. Power fails. The eye passes over—terrifying calm, stars visible, then the back wall hits. Water rises faster.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Estrada discovers Maya's helicopter on the pad. He tries to take it—she's trapped him on the island, and now he'll use her escape route. Fight on the rain-swept landing pad. Maya wins but the helicopter is damaged.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Water chest-high in the cell blocks. Not everyone can be saved. Maya must choose who gets out and who drowns. Her father tells her to leave him—save the younger prisoners. She refuses. She's not leaving anyone.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Tomás reveals his pilot skills and repairs the helicopter enough to fly. Maya coordinates the impossible: loading an overweight helicopter in hurricane conditions. The storm is weakening but still deadly. This is it.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Takeoff in 100 mph winds with a damaged helicopter carrying twice the safe load. Maya flies by feel, by training, by refusing to die. The helicopter fails. They crash in shallow water a mile from Puerto Rico's coast. Maya leads a swim to shore, saving those who can't make it alone—rescue swimming one last time.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The survivors reach shore as dawn breaks. Coast Guard cutters—sent by Javier—recover them. Henri's testimony, combined with Dr. Méndez's documentation, exposes the black site program. Estrada dies on the island, drowned in his own prison. Maya is offered her commission back. She accepts, but requests duty in Puerto Rico—where hurricanes happen.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Rescue against impossible natural forces.
**Deep theme:** The difference between risk and recklessness—and how guilt can make us abandon the responsibility to act.
**Thematic question:** When saving everyone is impossible, is saving anyone still worth the risk?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Flight In**: Single-pilot helicopter navigation through a Category 5 hurricane's outer bands, instruments failing, flying by instinct and desperation.
2. **The Flooded Cells**: Swimming through prison corridors with water at ceiling height, finding air pockets, freeing prisoners who've already given up.
3. **The Crash Landing**: The helicopter failing over open water, Maya bringing it down without killing everyone, then leading a mile-long swim to shore in storm conditions.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *The Guardian* meets *The Perfect Storm*
**Book comparables:** *Into the Storm* by Reed Timmer, *Zeitoun* by Dave Eggers

## Tone and Pacing Notes
The hurricane is a constant presence—building, hitting, and finally passing. Weather conditions should be visceral: wind that steals breath, rain that blinds, water that rises with inexorable patience. The rescue sequences are technical and desperate. The moral choice about who to save is given weight; Maya can't be a superhero who saves everyone.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Haitian names use standard English pronunciation with French influence (Baptiste = bap-TEEST, Henri = on-REE). Spanish names for Puerto Rican characters (Méndez, Santos). Hurricane aviation terminology should sound authoritative. The storm's presence should be audible through description—wind howl, water roar, the terrible quiet of the eye.
