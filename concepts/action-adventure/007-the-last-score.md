# The Last Score

## Genre
Action-Adventure

## One-Line Premise
An aging master thief must reunite her scattered crew for one final heist—stealing evidence from a Paris vault to save her imprisoned daughter—while evading the cop who's hunted her for twenty years.

## Expanded Premise
Margaux Devereaux was Europe's greatest thief until she retired twenty years ago to raise her daughter Claire. Now Claire is in a French prison, framed for a crime orchestrated by Jean-Paul Mercier, a corrupt industrialist who needed a scapegoat. The only evidence that could free Claire is locked in Mercier's private vault beneath his Paris mansion—a vault designed by the same architect who built the Banque de France. Margaux must reassemble her legendary crew, now scattered across three continents and variously aged, alcoholic, or dead, and pull off a heist that younger thieves call impossible. All while Inspector Gilles Romain, the detective who's spent his career almost catching her, finally has a chance to close the case.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Margaux Devereaux
**Age:** 62
**Occupation / Background:** Retired thief, former art restorer (her cover), now living quietly in Lyon as a grandmother. Born into a family of thieves in Marseille, trained by her father, exceeded everyone's expectations.
**Core skill:** Planning and adaptation—she sees systems the way musicians see notes. She can walk through a building once and know every weakness. Her reflexes aren't what they were, but her mind is faster than ever.
**Fatal flaw:** Pride that refused to let her age gracefully out of the game. She stayed too long, took too many risks, and now those risks have finally caught up through her daughter.
**Character arc:** Believes she can fix everything if she plans well enough. Discovers that some problems require trusting others and accepting that control is an illusion. Ends learning that legacy isn't what you steal—it's what you leave behind.
**Voice and register:** Elegant, precise, with undercurrents of Marseille profanity when stressed. She treats planning like poetry and executes like a machine.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Jean-Paul Mercier, industrialist, arms dealer, and owner of half the politicians in France, who framed Claire to cover his illegal weapons exports.
**Goal:** Keep his empire intact. Claire's imprisonment is just housekeeping; she stumbled onto his operation while auditing one of his companies. He didn't know who her mother was, but now that he does, eliminating Margaux becomes priority one.
**Method:** Owns private security, bought judges, and has no compunction about violence. His vault is the most secure private installation in Europe. He's offered a bounty to anyone who delivers Margaux.
**Connection to protagonist:** Mercier's father was a fence who betrayed Margaux's father to police in 1978, resulting in his death in prison. This heist is personal across generations.

## Supporting Characters

**Claire Devereaux** — Margaux's daughter, an auditor who never knew about her mother's past until it destroyed her life. Brilliant, bitter, and losing hope.
**Hugo Sanz** — Spanish, the crew's wheelman, now seventy and officially blind in one eye. His hands still know every road in Europe.
**Nadira Benali** — Algerian-French, the crew's hacker before hacking existed—she was a lockpick and social engineer. Now runs a legitimate security firm and has the most to lose.
**Tomas Richter** — German, the crew's muscle and demolitions expert. Currently drinking himself to death in Prague, haunted by a job gone wrong.
**Inspector Gilles Romain** — The cop who's pursued Margaux for twenty years, now six months from retirement. He respects her. He may even like her. But he will not let her go.

## Setting

**Primary location:** Paris and its environs—from the working-class suburbs to the gilded arrondissements, from Belle Époque architecture to Mercier's fortress-mansion in Neuilly-sur-Seine.
**Time period:** Present day, autumn
**Atmosphere:** Gray light and golden stone, café terraces and cobblestone, the Paris of postcards existing alongside high-tech security systems. Old Europe confronting new surveillance. The romance of crime meeting the reality of age.
**Key locations:** Margaux's Lyon apartment (planning headquarters), the Belleville safehouse (crew staging), Mercier's Neuilly mansion (target), the Paris catacombs (infiltration route), the Seine (escape route).

## Central Conflict
Margaux must pull off a heist that requires skills she no longer possesses, using a crew who may not survive the attempt, against a target who's already killed to protect his secrets—all while Inspector Romain closes in, and her daughter's trial date approaches. She has six weeks.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Claire goes free, Mercier faces justice for arms trafficking, and Margaux proves that the old ways still matter—that skill and planning beat technology and money.
**If the protagonist fails:** Claire serves twenty years for a crime she didn't commit. Margaux dies or goes to prison herself. Her crew—her family—is destroyed. Mercier wins.
**Personal stakes:** Margaux either saves her daughter and finds a way to finally stop running, or she loses everything she retired to protect, proving that the life she chose always costs more than it pays.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Margaux visits Claire in prison, sees what incarceration is doing to her, and decides to act. She reviews what she knows about Mercier, recognizes the name, and understands this is about more than her daughter.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Margaux tracks down her old crew. Hugo is driving tourists in Seville. Nadira refuses three times before Margaux threatens to expose her past. Tomas has to be dragged out of a Prague bar.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: The crew conducts initial reconnaissance of Mercier's mansion and discovers the vault is military-grade. Mercier's security chief spots them. Romain receives word that Margaux has resurfaced. The clock starts.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: The crew develops the plan: enter through Paris's catacombs, breach the vault during Mercier's annual gala, and escape via the Seine. Each element requires skills they may no longer have.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Training and preparation. Hugo relearns the streets. Nadira cracks Mercier's digital security. Tomas gets sober enough to handle explosives. Margaux practices the vault sequence until she can do it blind. They're ready.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Night of the gala. Everything goes wrong. Mercier has changed the vault codes. Romain appears at the party, tracking a separate lead. Tomas is recognized by a former associate and barely escapes. The crew aborts.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: In the aftermath, they realize Mercier knows they're coming—he's doubled security and moved the evidence to a secondary location. Nadira wants out. Hugo's heart condition worsens. Margaux faces the possibility that she's led her friends to die for nothing.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Margaux visits Romain, unofficially. They share wine and history. She doesn't confess, but she makes him understand what's at stake. He can't help her, but he tells her about the secondary location. She returns to the crew with a new plan—simpler, more dangerous, but possible.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The crew hits Mercier's backup vault—a bank deposit box in an old Belle Époque vault that Margaux's father robbed fifty years ago. Full circle. Romain arrives with backup but Margaux has anticipated him—the crew escapes through the same tunnel her father used. Final confrontation with Mercier in the catacombs; Tomas dies saving Margaux.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The evidence reaches prosecutors through an anonymous channel. Claire is released. Mercier flees to a country without extradition but is dead within a month—old enemies. Romain retires without his arrest, finally at peace. Margaux attends Tomas's funeral, then disappears. The final scene: Margaux teaching her teenage granddaughter to pick a lock.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** One last job before it's too late.
**Deep theme:** What we pass on to our children—skills, values, sins—and whether we can ever stop running from who we were.
**Thematic question:** When your past catches up with your family, do you owe them your truth or your protection?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Catacomb Descent**: Navigating miles of bone-lined tunnels beneath Paris, racing against a security response, the claustrophobic dark punctuated by Hugo's failing night vision.
2. **The Gala Infiltration**: Margaux in disguise among Paris's elite, playing a role she perfected fifty years ago, while her crew moves through the building's arteries.
3. **The Belle Époque Vault**: Breaking into a nineteenth-century vault using nineteenth-century techniques, the new plan requiring skills that feel like muscle memory—like coming home.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *Ocean's Eleven* meets *The Town*
**Book comparables:** *The Lock Artist* by Steve Hamilton, *Firewatch* by Connie Willis

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Elegance with undertones of melancholy. The heist structure provides momentum, but the emotional core is aging, regret, and redemption. The crew's banter carries decades of shared history. Tension builds through accumulating complications rather than constant action. Paris is a character—its beauty and history woven through every scene.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
French names use proper pronunciation (Devereaux = dev-uh-ROH, Mercier = mair-see-AY). Parisian locations should sound authentic without being performative. Margaux's voice should convey elegance and competence even in casual dialogue. Tomas's German-accented French adds texture. Romain's weary professionalism contrasts with the crew's theatrical flair.
