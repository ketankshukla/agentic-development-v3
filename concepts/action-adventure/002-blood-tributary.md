# Blood Tributary

## Genre
Action-Adventure

## One-Line Premise
A indigenous rights lawyer must guide a group of survivors through unmapped Amazon jungle while hunted by the cartel soldiers who murdered their colleagues.

## Expanded Premise
Sofia Reyes arrived at a remote research station on Brazil's Rio Negro to document illegal mining operations for an international tribunal. Within hours of her arrival, cartel gunmen attacked, slaughtering the researchers and burning their evidence. Now the sole survivor with legal training, Sofia must lead five traumatized civilians—including a wounded photojournalist and a twelve-year-old witness—two hundred kilometers through roadless jungle to reach the nearest town before the cartel's tracker unit catches them. The jungle wants them dead. The cartel wants them silent. Sofia has never spent a night outdoors in her life.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Sofia Reyes
**Age:** 34
**Occupation / Background:** Environmental human rights attorney based in São Paulo, daughter of Peruvian immigrants, educated at Columbia Law. Her career has been courtrooms and conferences, never fieldwork.
**Core skill:** Persuasion and improvisation under pressure—she can read people, build coalitions, and talk her way through impossible situations. She learns fast and never stops thinking.
**Fatal flaw:** Perfectionism that borders on control addiction. When she can't control outcomes, she freezes instead of adapting.
**Character arc:** Believes that justice comes from systems and institutions. Discovers that when systems fail, justice becomes personal—and sometimes survival is the only verdict that matters. Becomes someone who acts rather than argues.
**Voice and register:** Precise, educated, with a lawyer's instinct for leverage. Under stress, her composure cracks to reveal genuine warmth and unexpected steel.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Comandante Rivas, head of the cartel's enforcement arm, former Brazilian special forces turned mercenary, now the cartel's solution to problems that require disappearing.
**Goal:** Eliminate all witnesses to the massacre before evidence reaches international authorities. If the tribunal proceeds, the cartel's mining operation—worth $800 million annually—collapses, and Rivas's employers will kill him for failing.
**Method:** Commands a twelve-man tracker unit with military training and jungle experience. Uses drones, satellite phones, and local informants. Burns every village that might offer shelter.
**Connection to protagonist:** Rivas's unit killed Sofia's mentor, Dr. Elena Castillo, in the initial attack. He's heard Sofia's voice on the radio and knows she's the threat—the one who can make the evidence matter.

## Supporting Characters

**Tomás Aguirre** — Mexican photojournalist, wounded in the attack, carrying the only surviving camera with massacre footage. Cynical, experienced in conflict zones, quietly terrified of dying in the jungle.
**Yara** — Twelve-year-old daughter of a murdered researcher, the only witness to where the cartel buried the bodies. Silent since the attack, but watches everything.
**Father Miguel Soares** — Elderly liberation theology priest who ran the research station's medical clinic. Knows the jungle trails but is physically failing.
**Kael Mwangi** — Kenyan marine biologist studying river dolphins, the group's only member with actual survival skills. Steady, practical, refuses to be anyone's leader.
**Renata Vidal** — Brazilian government liaison who may or may not have warned the cartel about the research team's location. Her loyalty remains unclear until the final act.

## Setting

**Primary location:** The upper Rio Negro basin in Brazil's Amazon, from the burned research station at Tapuruquara through unmapped jungle to the town of Santa Isabel do Rio Negro.
**Time period:** Present day, wet season
**Atmosphere:** Green darkness pressing from all sides, the canopy blocking the sun so completely that noon feels like twilight. Every surface crawls with insects. The air is so humid that breathing feels like drowning. Rivers appear without warning, brown and fast and full of things that bite.
**Key locations:** The Tapuruquara Research Station (starting point, burned), the Flooded Forest (várzea that must be navigated by improvised raft), the Abandoned Rubber Camp (colonial-era ruins offering shelter), the Yanomami Contact Zone (where outside help might exist), Santa Isabel do Rio Negro (destination).

## Central Conflict
Sofia must keep five civilians alive across two hundred kilometers of jungle that offers countless ways to die—disease, predators, rivers, exposure—while evading a professional military unit that's hunting them with superior firepower, local knowledge, and no witnesses to restrain their methods. The evidence they carry could save thousands of indigenous lives, but only if any of them survive to deliver it.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The tribunal receives testimony and evidence proving cartel complicity in the massacre. The mining operation is shut down, cartel leadership is indicted, and indigenous land rights in the region are legally reinforced for a generation.
**If the protagonist fails:** Six more bodies disappear into the jungle. The massacre is blamed on indigenous violence. The cartel expands operations into protected territory. Dr. Castillo's life work dies with her.
**Personal stakes:** Sofia either proves that her skills matter outside a courtroom—that she can be more than words—or she dies knowing she led five people into the jungle and couldn't save any of them.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Sofia arrives at the research station, meets the team, and reviews evidence that's even more damning than expected. That night, the cartel attacks. Sofia survives by hiding in the river.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Dawn reveals the massacre. Sofia finds the five other survivors and realizes they have no radio, no weapons, and two hundred kilometers of jungle between them and safety. They have to move before the cartel returns to burn the bodies.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: The group's first day in the jungle nearly kills them—heat exhaustion, a snake bite scare, a near-drowning. They make camp only to hear Rivas's unit arriving at the station behind them. The hunt has begun.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Rivas's drones find them. The group flees into the flooded forest, building rafts to navigate waters too deep to walk. Tomás's wound becomes infected. Rivas radios a message: surrender the evidence and he'll let them live.
- Ch 5 — The Push: They reach the abandoned rubber camp and find supplies left by illegal miners. Father Miguel's knowledge of old trails gives them a shortcut. For one night, survival seems possible.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The cartel has burned the Yanomami village that was their hope for help. Rivas's unit catches up—firefight in the jungle. Father Miguel is killed covering the group's escape. Renata's sat phone is discovered, proving she's been updating someone on their position.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: The group fractures. Tomás wants to kill Renata. Kael wants to scatter and run. Yara speaks for the first time since the massacre—to say they should surrender. Sofia realizes her leadership has been about control, not care, and they're following a dead woman walking.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Sofia confronts Renata, who reveals she was informing the Brazilian government, not the cartel—but her reports were intercepted. Together they devise a plan to use Renata's compromised channel to feed Rivas false information about their route.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The group sets an ambush at a river crossing, using the terrain Sofia has learned to read over nine brutal days. Rivas's unit walks into a flooded ravine trap. Hand-to-hand combat in chest-deep water. Sofia kills Rivas with his own knife as he's strangling Kael.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The survivors reach Santa Isabel, more dead than alive. Sofia delivers testimony to the waiting tribunal representatives. Yara is placed with relatives. Tomás's photos run on the front page of six international papers. Sofia returns to São Paulo but can no longer practice law the same way—she now runs a foundation training indigenous communities in legal self-defense.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Survival against nature and human predators.
**Deep theme:** The difference between arguing for justice and fighting for it—and whether the institutions we believe in can survive contact with raw power.
**Thematic question:** When civilization's protections are stripped away, what remains of who you thought you were?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Flooded Forest Crossing**: The group navigates improvised rafts through miles of submerged jungle, avoiding caimans and cartel drones while Tomás slips in and out of fever. The trees rise from black water like drowned giants.
2. **The Rubber Camp Siege**: Pinned down in colonial ruins as Rivas's men approach from three sides. Father Miguel's last stand holding a doorway with a machete and faith.
3. **The River Crossing Ambush**: Neck-deep in fast-moving water, the survivors turn the terrain against their hunters. Sofia goes underwater with Rivas, fighting blind by touch in the silted brown current.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *The Revenant* meets *Clear and Present Danger*
**Book comparables:** *The River* by Peter Heller, *Annihilation* by Jeff VanderMeer

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Suffocating tension that rarely releases. The jungle is an omnipresent antagonist—every chapter includes some new way it tries to kill them. Pacing alternates between desperate movement and terrifying stillness when the cartel is close. The group dynamics shift constantly as stress reveals character. Violence is sudden, ugly, and has consequences—wounds don't heal, death is permanent, and killing changes the survivor.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Portuguese and Spanish names use standard pronunciation (Reyes = RAY-ess, Soares = SWAH-resh). The jungle soundscape should be constantly implied—insects, water, things moving in the undergrowth. Yara's silence and then first words need careful pacing for emotional impact. Father Miguel's death should land with weight, not action-movie casualness. Sofia's internal voice grows rougher as the journey progresses.
