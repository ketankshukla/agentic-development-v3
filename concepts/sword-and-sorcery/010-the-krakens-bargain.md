# The Kraken's Bargain

## Genre
Sword-and-Sorcery

## One-Line Premise
A maroon pirate queen must command a drowned god's navy of the dead to stop a colonial fleet carrying an artifact that will enslave the sea itself.

## Expanded Premise
Adaora escaped slavery at fourteen, stole a ship at sixteen, and became the most feared pirate in the Sapphire Sea by twenty-five. Now she faces an enemy she cannot outrun: Admiral Blackwood's colonial fleet carries the Trident of Dominion, a stolen artifact that can command any vessel that touches salt water. When Adaora's ship is sunk, she drowns—and wakes in the court of Mami Wata, goddess of the deep, who offers a bargain: lead my navy of drowned sailors, stop Blackwood, destroy the Trident, and I will give you back your life. The navy of the dead answers to Adaora—but so does the madness of the deep.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Adaora Stormborn (birth name lost to slavery; she chose this one)
**Age:** 28
**Occupation / Background:** Escaped slave, pirate captain, legendary liberator of slave ships
**Core skill:** Unmatched naval tactician—she can read currents, winds, and enemy formations like others read books
**Fatal flaw:** She cannot trust any authority, even benevolent ones; she's been betrayed too many times
**Character arc:** Believes freedom means answering to no one; discovers that some powers are worth serving if they serve the right cause; becomes not just a pirate but a protector of the sea itself
**Voice and register:** Speaks in command voice—short, declarative sentences that expect obedience. Internally, she's constantly calculating odds and escape routes.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Admiral Lord Blackwood, commander of the Colonial Reclamation Fleet
**Goal:** To use the Trident to seize control of every ship in the Sapphire Sea, ending piracy and securing colonial trade routes—enslaving any who resist
**Method:** The Trident, when planted at the sea's magical heart, will spread its control like infection through every hull that touches water
**Connection to protagonist:** Blackwood was the captain of the slave ship young Adaora was transported on; she killed his son during her escape

## Supporting Characters

**Juma** — Adaora's first mate, still alive and searching for her; he becomes the land-side ally coordinating with the maroon communities
**The Drowned Chorus** — The crew of dead sailors who follow Adaora; they retain their personalities but are bound to her will; their leader was once a rival pirate Adaora defeated
**Mami Wata** — The goddess of the deep, beautiful and terrifying; she genuinely wants to stop Blackwood but has her own reasons for choosing Adaora

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Sapphire Sea, a Caribbean-inspired archipelago of sugar islands, maroon settlements, and colonial ports
**Time period:** Secondary world equivalent of early 18th century
**Atmosphere:** Turquoise water and white sand. The crack of sails and the creak of timber. Rum and gunpowder. Bodies hanging at harbor entrances. Hidden coves where the escaped build new lives.
**Key locations:** The Wreck of the Storm Queen (Adaora's sunken ship), The Court of the Deep (Mami Wata's underwater palace), Port Sovereign (Blackwood's fortress harbor), The Heart of the Sea (where the Trident must be planted), The Maroon Island (free settlement)

## Central Conflict
Adaora must command dead sailors who remember resenting her, evade a colonial fleet with overwhelming firepower, and reach the Heart of the Sea before Blackwood—all while the deep god's power slowly transforms her into something that may not want to return to the living world.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The Trident is destroyed, Blackwood dies, and the free settlements gain permanent independence
**If the protagonist fails:** Every ship becomes colonial property, the maroons are recaptured, and the sea itself becomes a weapon against freedom
**Personal stakes:** Each day in the deep, Adaora becomes less human; if she stays too long, she can never return to life—and part of her is beginning not to want to

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Last Battle: Adaora faces Blackwood's fleet with her pirate armada; she's outgunned and her ship is sunk; she drowns
- Ch 2 — The Deep Court: Adaora wakes in Mami Wata's realm; the goddess offers the bargain; Adaora accepts because she will not let Blackwood win
- Ch 3 — The Drowned Crew: Adaora takes command of the dead sailors; many hate her; she must prove her authority in the strange physics of the underwater world

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The First Strike: The dead navy attacks a colonial patrol; Adaora's tactics work but the transformation begins—she can breathe water now, but air burns
- Ch 5 — The Maroon Alliance: Juma contacts her through coastal magic; the maroon settlements will fight but need time to prepare
- Ch 6 — The Heart's Location: A drowned sailor reveals the Heart of the Sea's location—it's beneath Port Sovereign, Blackwood's own fortress
- Ch 7 — The Goddess's Lie: Mami Wata's true plan emerges: she wants not just to stop Blackwood but to sink every colonial ship, drowning thousands; Adaora refuses to become a weapon of mass murder
- Ch 8 — The Renegade Dead: Adaora convinces her crew they can win without genocide; Mami Wata withdraws her favor but not the bargain—Adaora is on her own, dead and living

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Battle of Port Sovereign: The drowned navy attacks from below while Juma's forces strike from land; Adaora fights through sunken tunnels to reach the Heart
- Ch 10 — The Trident's End: Adaora destroys the Trident but must sacrifice her connection to the deep to do it; she chooses life, emerging from the sea fully human again; the dead sailors are freed to rest; Adaora returns to the surface as someone new

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Freedom at any cost
**Deep theme:** Liberation cannot become tyranny, or it betrays its own meaning
**Thematic question:** If you've become a monster to fight monsters, how do you know when to stop—and can you ever become human again?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Sinking**: Adaora's ship going down around her, underwater combat as she fights even while drowning, the moment she inhales water and doesn't die
2. **The Underwater Armada**: Ships that sail beneath the waves, crewed by the phosphorescent dead, attacking a colonial fleet from below
3. **The Heart Chamber**: A cavern beneath the sea, glowing with primal power, Adaora and Blackwood fighting on a platform suspended above the ocean's soul

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest* meets *Black Panther* (Wakanda's isolationism vs. engagement parallel)
**Book comparables:** Robert Jackson Bennett's *Foundryside*; Tochi Onyebuchi's *War Girls*

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Propulsive and angry, with moments of strange beauty in the deep. The colonial violence should feel raw, the freedom fighting should feel earned. Let the underwater scenes be alien and dreamlike while the surface scenes are visceral and immediate. Adaora's voice is the anchor—always tactical, always planning, but increasingly uncertain about what winning means.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Adaora's voice should have West African Caribbean inflection, commanding and clipped. Blackwood should sound aristocratic and cold. The Drowned Chorus should have watery, echoing voices. Yoruba-derived names: Adaora (a-da-OH-ra), Juma (JOO-ma). Ocean ambience should be constant. Action scenes need faster pacing with underwater acoustic effects.
