# The Woad King's Heir

## Genre
Sword-and-Sorcery

## One-Line Premise
A Roman-trained druid must unite feuding Celtic tribes against a possessed legion that turns everything it touches to living stone.

## Expanded Premise
Brennus was taken from his burning village at seven and raised in Rome as a curiosity—a druid trained in philosophy and war. Now twenty-nine, he's returned to Britannia as a translator for the invasion force, a collaborator his people would kill on sight. But the Ninth Legion he marches with has been corrupted: the general made a pact with an entity from beyond the stone circles, and every soldier is slowly becoming granite. When the Legion begins petrifying villages and enslaving souls in stone shells, Brennus realizes only he can stop it—a druid who knows Roman tactics, leading Celts who hate him, against an army that cannot die.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Brennus ap Owain (Roman name: Brennus Rufus)
**Age:** 29
**Occupation / Background:** Roman-trained druid and translator, property of General Varro, cultural exile between two worlds
**Core skill:** He can speak to the land spirits and has the Roman education to understand military strategy—the bridge between worlds
**Fatal flaw:** He doesn't know who he is; every action is a performance of either "Roman" or "Celt," neither of which feels real
**Character arc:** Believes he is either traitor or liberator; discovers he can be both and neither—he must forge a third identity; becomes the bridge who saves his people by being something new
**Voice and register:** Speaks Latin with precision and Celtic with rust. His internal voice switches between languages depending on emotion.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** General Marcus Varro, commander of the Ninth Legion, now vessel for the Stone-That-Hungers
**Goal:** To petrify the entire island, creating an eternal monument to Rome that requires no food, no rebellion, no maintenance
**Method:** The Stone-That-Hungers feeds on living will, converting flesh to granite while trapping the soul inside
**Connection to protagonist:** Varro bought Brennus from slave traders, educated him, and genuinely cared for him in a twisted paternal way; the entity now wearing Varro's face still calls Brennus "son"

## Supporting Characters

**Boudicca of the Iceni** — A queen who lost her daughters to the Legion; she leads the tribal alliance and distrusts Brennus completely until he proves himself
**Cassius** — A Roman centurion who realizes his Legion is damned and defects; he provides tactical knowledge but faces hatred from both sides
**Old Myrddin** — An ancient druid who remembers what the stone circles were built to contain; he provides cryptic guidance and magical knowledge

## Setting

**Primary location:** Britannia, a Celtic-inspired island of fog-shrouded forests, sacred groves, and hillforts
**Time period:** Secondary world equivalent of 1st century CE
**Atmosphere:** Mist in the valleys. The smell of peat and rain. Blue woad on scarred faces. Standing stones humming with old power. The clash of bronze against iron.
**Key locations:** The Petrified Village (first horror), Boudicca's Hillfort (tribal gathering), The Stone Circle of Carnac (site of the entity's emergence), The Roman Camp (enemy base), The Grove of the Old Gods (where the final ritual must occur)

## Central Conflict
Brennus must convince tribes who see him as a traitor to follow him, master druidic powers he barely understands, and lead an outnumbered force against immortal stone soldiers—all while the entity keeps offering him reconciliation and calling him by his slave name.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The Stone-That-Hungers is resealed, the Legion is destroyed, and the tribes have time to prepare for the next invasion
**If the protagonist fails:** Every living thing on the island becomes eternal granite, conscious and imprisoned forever
**Personal stakes:** Brennus must choose who he is—Roman collaborator, Celtic liberator, or something unprecedented; the wrong choice loses him everyone's trust

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Stone Village: Brennus scouts ahead for the Legion and finds an entire village frozen in granite; he witnesses a stone soldier converting a survivor and barely escapes
- Ch 2 — The Desertion: Brennus flees the Legion as Varro reveals his transformation; Cassius joins him; they run toward the nearest tribal holding
- Ch 3 — The Queen's Judgment: Boudicca captures them; Brennus must prove his value by offering Roman intelligence; she spares him but keeps him prisoner

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The Druids' Test: Old Myrddin demands Brennus prove he remembers the old ways; a vision-quest in the grove reveals his power and the entity's nature
- Ch 5 — The Alliance Forged: Brennus uses Roman tactical knowledge to help Boudicca defeat a stone patrol; the tribes begin to trust him
- Ch 6 — The Father's Voice: The Varro-entity captures Brennus and offers him a place in the eternal kingdom; Brennus nearly accepts before rejecting his "father"
- Ch 7 — The Stone Tide: The Legion advances on Boudicca's hillfort; the defenders cannot hold; hundreds are petrified
- Ch 8 — The Old Magic: Myrddin reveals the sealing ritual requires a sacrifice of identity—someone must give up who they are to power the binding

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Circle War: Brennus leads the tribal army in a fighting retreat to the Stone Circle; the climactic battle unfolds across the standing stones
- Ch 10 — The Third Way: Brennus sacrifices his Roman identity, his Celtic identity, everything he was—and becomes the seal itself, binding the entity; he survives, but has no name, no past, no nation; he walks away to become something new

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The conflict between empire and indigenous resistance
**Deep theme:** Identity is not inherited or imposed—it must be forged through choice
**Thematic question:** If you belong to both sides and neither, can you save a people by becoming something none of them can recognize?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Stone Conversion**: A single survivor being slowly transformed, pleading, while Brennus watches from hiding, unable to help
2. **The Hillfort Assault**: Stone soldiers climbing walls, swords shattering against granite, defenders setting themselves on fire rather than being converted
3. **The Circle Binding**: Brennus standing at the center of the ring, Varro's entity attacking, the standing stones glowing as he sacrifices his names

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *Centurion* meets *The Green Knight*
**Book comparables:** Bernard Cornwell's *The Winter King*; Guy Gavriel Kay's *The Fionavar Tapestry*

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Grim and atmospheric, with moments of savage beauty. The pacing should feel like a forced march—relentless forward pressure with desperate skirmishes. Let Brennus's internal conflict be loud; his identity crisis is not subtext but text. The horror elements should be body horror: the conversion process should disturb.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Brennus switches between Latin precision and Celtic earthiness; both need to be distinct in narration. Brythonic/Celtic names: Boudicca (boo-DIK-a), Myrddin (MUR-thin), Owain (OH-wine). Roman Latin pronunciation for Roman names. Stone soldiers should have grinding, mineral voices. Standing stone scenes need low, resonant humming.
