# The Djinn's Ransom

## Genre
Sword-and-Sorcery

## One-Line Premise
A disgraced prince must steal three impossible artifacts from rival kingdoms to pay a djinn's ransom and save his dying city from eternal sandstorm.

## Expanded Premise
Prince Cyrus of Sarvestan watched his father sell their kingdom's protective djinn to pay war debts. Now that djinn, Zepharak, has returned—not as a servant but as a conqueror, summoning an endless sandstorm that buries Sarvestan inch by inch. Zepharak offers one chance: bring him the Peacock Crown of the Maharajah, the Lion Seal of the Sultan, and the Dragon Pearl of the Emperor within sixty days, or everyone Cyrus loves suffocates beneath the sand. Cyrus must become the thief his enemies always accused him of being.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Cyrus Sarvan
**Age:** 29
**Occupation / Background:** Exiled prince, now a caravan guard on the Silk Road, hiding his identity
**Core skill:** Polyglot and master of court intrigue—he speaks seven languages and knows the weaknesses of every noble house from Constantinople to Beijing
**Fatal flaw:** Pathological pride; he cannot admit failure or ask for help, believing a prince should need no one
**Character arc:** Believes royalty means never showing weakness; learns that true leadership is knowing when to trust others; becomes a king worth following instead of one worth fearing
**Voice and register:** Speaks with aristocratic precision even when filthy and desperate. His thoughts are full of bitter self-mockery.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Zepharak, the Djinn of the Screaming Winds
**Goal:** To humiliate the Sarvan dynasty by making their heir prove their corruption, then destroy them anyway
**Method:** The sandstorm kills slowly, giving Cyrus hope while ensuring he knows every death is his family's fault
**Connection to protagonist:** Cyrus's father sold Zepharak into a copper lamp for forty years; the djinn spent that time dreaming of revenge against the bloodline

## Supporting Characters

**Nasreen** — A Zoroastrian fire-priestess who can sense lies; she joins Cyrus because the artifacts he seeks are sacred relics that shouldn't belong to any throne
**Kang Wei** — A Chinese ex-soldier turned smuggler who knows every secret passage across the Silk Road; he's dying of a curse and needs Cyrus to steal one of the artifacts for his own reasons
**Farrokh** — Cyrus's former bodyguard, now hunting him as a traitor; he doesn't know the truth about the sandstorm

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Silk Road network from Persia to China, passing through Central Asian kingdoms
**Time period:** Secondary world equivalent of 13th century
**Atmosphere:** Caravansaries at sunset, the smell of saffron and horse sweat, silk and steel changing hands in firelit markets. Dust in everything. The stars overhead so bright they seem close enough to cut.
**Key locations:** Sarvestan (the buried city), The Palace of a Thousand Mirrors (Indian location), The Sultan's Maze (Ottoman fortress), The Jade Forbidden City (Chinese imperial seat), The Oasis of Whispers (neutral ground where thieves meet)

## Central Conflict
Cyrus must infiltrate three of the most heavily guarded treasuries in the known world, each protected by magic and armies, while being hunted by his own kingdom's assassins who believe he abandoned his people—all within sixty days before the sand swallows everyone he's trying to save.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Zepharak gets his artifacts and must lift the storm, per the djinn laws of bargaining
**If the protagonist fails:** Sarvestan is buried forever, two hundred thousand people die, and Cyrus's bloodline ends in disgrace
**Personal stakes:** Even if Cyrus wins, he'll be a thief who robbed three empires—he can never go home as anything but a criminal

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Sand That Swallows: Cyrus returns to Sarvestan to find it half-buried; Zepharak appears and makes the bargain
- Ch 2 — The Caravan West: Cyrus recruits Nasreen and Kang Wei with half-truths; they set out for the first artifact in Hindustan
- Ch 3 — The Palace of Mirrors: They infiltrate the Maharajah's court during a festival; Cyrus steals the Peacock Crown but Nasreen is captured

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The Cost of Crowns: Cyrus must break Nasreen out, revealing himself to the Maharajah's spies and starting a chase across continents
- Ch 5 — The Sultan's Maze: They reach the Ottoman fortress; Cyrus uses his court training to manipulate his way inside and steals the Lion Seal
- Ch 6 — The Brother's Blade: Farrokh catches up to them; he reveals Cyrus's father is dead and his brother has surrendered to Zepharak, offering Cyrus as a traitor
- Ch 7 — The Desert Crossing: Betrayed and wounded, the group barely survives the wasteland; Kang Wei's curse worsens and he reveals why he really needs the Dragon Pearl
- Ch 8 — The Third Crown: In the Chinese capital, Cyrus realizes the only way in is to tell the truth—he throws himself on the Emperor's mercy and asks for help

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Djinn's Game: Cyrus delivers all three artifacts, but Zepharak reveals the final twist—he wants Cyrus to choose which kingdom dies in Sarvestan's place
- Ch 10 — The Wind's End: Cyrus refuses to choose, instead offering himself as the djinn's new slave for a thousand years; Zepharak, impressed by the sacrifice, lifts the curse and vanishes; Cyrus returns home a hero but no longer a prince

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The price of redemption
**Deep theme:** Legacy is not what your ancestors did—it's what you do with the debt they leave
**Thematic question:** Can you save your people by becoming everything they fear, and still be worthy of leading them?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Mirrored Heist**: A theft through a palace where every surface reflects and guards use the mirrors to see around corners—Cyrus must move without casting a reflection
2. **The Sandstorm Wall**: A sequence where the group rides through the edge of Zepharak's storm, seeing the ghostly shapes of drowning buildings
3. **The Emperor's Judgment**: Cyrus throws away his pride and kneels before an enemy court, gambling everything on an honest plea

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *Aladdin* meets *The Mummy* (1999)
**Book comparables:** S.A. Chakraborty's *The City of Brass*; Scott Lynch's *The Lies of Locke Lamora*

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Propulsive heist energy with romantic adventure undertones. This is a ticking-clock story that never lets the reader forget the deadline. Moments of wonder and beauty punctuate the tension—the Silk Road should feel magical even when dangerous. Let Cyrus be witty even when desperate; his voice carries the book.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Cyrus should have an elegant Persian-accented English, never rushed. Zepharak's voice should be inhuman—echoing, with multiple tones layered. Names require careful attention: Sarvestan (sar-veh-STAHN), Nasreen (nas-REEN), Farrokh (fa-ROKH). Chinese names should follow Mandarin pronunciation.
