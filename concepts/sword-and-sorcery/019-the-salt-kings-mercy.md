# The Salt King's Mercy

## Genre
Sword-and-Sorcery

## One-Line Premise
A blind desert guide who sees through sand must lead a caravan of the damned across the Sahara to buy back his daughter's soul from the djinn who stole it.

## Expanded Premise
Idir lost his eyes to a sandstorm at fifteen but gained something in return—the sand speaks to him now, showing him paths no sighted man could follow. He has guided caravans across the Endless Sands for twenty years, saving hundreds of lives. When the Sand Djinn Taziri steals his daughter's soul, leaving her body alive but empty, Idir must cross the most forbidden stretch of desert—the Kingdom of Salt—to reach Taziri's court and bargain for her return. The price: guide a caravan of condemned souls, each owing a debt to the djinn, across terrain that kills everything. If any die along the way, his daughter is lost.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Idir ag Amellal ("he lives" of the Tuareg)
**Age:** 47
**Occupation / Background:** Master desert guide, the blind man who sees the sand, trusted by every caravan master in the trade routes
**Core skill:** Sand-sight—he perceives the desert through the movement and memory of sand grains, seeing paths, dangers, and water that no eyes could find
**Fatal flaw:** He believes his sand-sight is a bargain paid in his daughter's soul; he never forgave himself for taking the gift that may have marked her
**Character arc:** Believes he caused his daughter's curse by accepting the sand's gift; discovers the curse was laid regardless, as bait; learns to wield his power without guilt
**Voice and register:** Speaks in the measured cadences of one who conserves water even in words. Internal voice is navigational—everything measured in distance, direction, danger.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Taziri, the Sand Djinn, Queen of the Kingdom of Salt
**Goal:** To collect souls for her court, building an army for the coming war between djinn factions
**Method:** She offers desperate humans bargains they cannot refuse, then arranges circumstances that force them to fail
**Connection to protagonist:** Taziri has been watching Idir since the sandstorm; she gave him the gift, planning to use him as the ultimate collector of desperate souls

## Supporting Characters

**Amara** — Idir's daughter, 23, her body walking but her eyes empty; she follows him on the journey, a living reminder of the stakes
**The Condemned**: Khadija (a merchant who sold poisoned goods), Driss (a soldier who deserted), Tamazight (a mother who drowned her own child in madness), Yusuf (a thief who stole from temples)
**Moussa** — Idir's old assistant, a young man who can see but not as Idir sees; he provides eyes when sand-sight fails

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Endless Sands, a Saharan-inspired expanse of dunes, salt flats, and hidden oases
**Time period:** Secondary world equivalent of 14th century
**Atmosphere:** Heat that presses down like hands. The hiss of sand against sand. Mirages that might be real. Nights cold enough to crack stone. The endless patience of a land that wants you dead.
**Key locations:** Idir's Village (starting point), The Sand Sea (first crossing), The Bones of Empires (ruined desert city), The Kingdom of Salt (Taziri's domain), The Court of Whispers (djinn stronghold)

## Central Conflict
Idir must guide five people who have committed terrible sins across the most lethal desert in the world, keeping all of them alive despite the desert's attempts to kill them and their own guilt driving them toward death—all to save a daughter who may not remember him when she wakes.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** His daughter's soul returns, the condemned are freed of their debts, and Taziri's collection is diminished
**If the protagonist fails:** His daughter remains a living corpse, and every soul on the caravan joins Taziri's army
**Personal stakes:** Idir must confront the truth about his gift—was it freely given or was he marked as a tool from the beginning?

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Empty Eyes: Idir returns from a journey to find his daughter soulless; Taziri's voice offers the bargain; he accepts
- Ch 2 — The Condemned Gather: Idir meets the five souls he must transport; each hates themselves; each believes they deserve to die in the desert
- Ch 3 — The Sand Sea: The journey begins; the first crisis—a sand kraken attacks; Idir's sight saves them, but Driss the deserter nearly runs

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The Confession Walk: The desert forces truth; each condemned person reveals their crime during a sandstorm; Idir judges none of them
- Ch 5 — The Bones of Empires: They shelter in ruins; Khadija recognizes them—she sold the poison that killed this city; she nearly sacrifices herself in guilt
- Ch 6 — The Water Choice: Only enough water for some; Idir's sand-sight finds a hidden source; Tamazight, the infanticidal mother, nearly gives her share to the others to die
- Ch 7 — The Salt Threshold: They enter the Kingdom of Salt; Taziri's minions test them; Yusuf the thief steals something sacred and nearly dooms everyone
- Ch 8 — The Father's Truth: At the edge of Taziri's court, Idir realizes the djinn planned everything—his gift, his daughter's soul, this caravan; he was always meant to deliver these souls

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Court of Whispers: Idir enters Taziri's palace; he offers himself instead of the condemned; Taziri refuses—she wants them all; Idir uses his sand-sight to collapse her salt throne, revealing the weakness beneath her kingdom
- Ch 10 — The Mercy of Monsters: Taziri, impressed by Idir's defiance, offers a new bargain—take your daughter and go, but the condemned must choose for themselves; each chooses freedom over safety; they walk out together as the Kingdom of Salt begins to crack

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** A father's love transcends any desert
**Deep theme:** Mercy is not earned; it is given, and in giving it, we become more than our sins
**Thematic question:** If the guilty are beyond saving, what does that say about those who refuse to save them—and what does it mean when we choose to try anyway?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Sand Kraken**: A massive creature of living desert attacking the caravan, Idir "seeing" its movements through ripples in the sand, directing defense blindly
2. **The Confession Storm**: Each condemned person's crime visualized in the sandstorm—ghostly images of their victims, their choices, their moments of damnation
3. **The Collapsing Kingdom**: Taziri's court of salt cracking and crumbling, djinn servants screaming, the condemned running for the desert they once feared

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *Lawrence of Arabia* meets *The Road*
**Book comparables:** Paolo Bacigalupi's *The Water Knife*; Frank Herbert's *Dune*

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Relentless and parched—the prose should feel dry, every word conserved like water. The pacing is a long march punctuated by sudden crisis. The condemned should become sympathetic through their suffering, not their justifications. Idir's blindness should never be a limitation; his sand-sight makes him more capable, not less.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Berber/Tuareg-derived names need correct pronunciation: Idir (ee-DEER), Taziri (ta-ZEE-ree), Tamazight (ta-ma-ZIGHT). Idir's voice should be low, measured, conserving energy. Desert ambience throughout—wind, sand hiss, the creak of leather and rope. Djinn voices should echo, multiple tones layered. The sandstorm scenes need overwhelming audio presence.
