# The Tiger's Daughter

## Genre
Sword-and-Sorcery

## One-Line Premise
A shapeshifting princess must embrace the beast within to stop a demon-king from sacrificing a million souls to open a door between worlds.

## Expanded Premise
Padmavati was born during a tiger attack—her mother died, but the tiger's spirit entered the infant. Now she transforms into a white tigress under emotional stress, a secret her father has hidden for nineteen years by keeping her isolated in the women's quarters. When the demon-king Ravaka invades, conquering kingdom after kingdom and binding their populations for mass sacrifice, Padmavati's father is killed and her brothers captured. She escapes into the jungle with one mission: reach the Temple of the First Dawn before the new moon and perform the ritual that will seal the door between worlds forever. The journey will force her to become the beast she fears.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Padmavati Rajadevi
**Age:** 19
**Occupation / Background:** Hidden princess, raised in isolation to prevent her transformations from being discovered
**Core skill:** As the tiger, she has strength, speed, senses, and killer instinct; as herself, she has the education of a royal daughter and an eidetic memory for texts
**Fatal flaw:** She hates the tiger inside her and tries to suppress it, which only makes it more dangerous when it emerges
**Character arc:** Believes the beast is a curse to be controlled; discovers that the tiger is not separate from her—it is her, and wholeness means integration; becomes a warrior-queen who rules with both human wisdom and animal power
**Voice and register:** Speaks with formal royal precision, carefully controlled. Her internal voice is wilder, constantly battling the tiger's urges.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Ravaka, the Door-Keeper, demon-king of the conquered north
**Goal:** To sacrifice enough souls simultaneously to tear open the barrier between the demon realm and the mortal world, allowing his true masters to feast on humanity
**Method:** Conquering kingdoms not for territory but for population; keeping prisoners alive for the mass sacrifice at the new moon
**Connection to protagonist:** Ravaka was summoned nineteen years ago by Padmavati's own grandfather, who wanted to use demon magic to heal his dying daughter-in-law; the summoning succeeded but created Ravaka as a consequence

## Supporting Characters

**Ashoka** — A former palace guard, now a one-eyed survivor; he knows the jungle and was secretly in love with Padmavati from watching her in the gardens
**The Rishi** — An ancient sorcerer living as a hermit who knows the sealing ritual; he tests Padmavati by refusing to help until she demonstrates she can control her transformation
**Meera** — Padmavati's handmaiden who escaped with her; she's terrified of the tiger but loves Padmavati too much to abandon her

## Setting

**Primary location:** Bhoomi, an Indian subcontinent-inspired realm of jungle kingdoms, river civilizations, and sacred mountains
**Time period:** Secondary world equivalent of 5th century
**Atmosphere:** Monsoon rains and temple bells. Incense and elephant musk. Saris the color of fire and precious stone. Tigers in the garden shadows, cobras in the courtyard wells.
**Key locations:** The Zenana Palace (women's quarters where Padmavati was hidden), The Jungle of Whispers (enchanted forest), The Rishi's Mountain (hermit's cave), The Temple of the First Dawn (ancient ritual site), Ravaka's Prison-City (where a million captives await sacrifice)

## Central Conflict
Padmavati must cross a demon-infested continent, master a transformation she's feared her entire life, and perform a ritual that requires absolute inner peace—all while racing against the new moon and the terror of becoming something inhuman.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The door remains sealed, Ravaka is destroyed, and her brothers and people are freed
**If the protagonist fails:** A million souls are consumed, the demon realm floods into the world, and humanity becomes prey
**Personal stakes:** Each transformation makes the tiger stronger and Padmavati weaker; if she transforms too many times without integration, the tiger will take over permanently

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Tiger Wakes: Ravaka's forces attack the palace; Padmavati's father dies protecting her; she transforms for the first time in years, slaughtering soldiers, then flees in horror
- Ch 2 — The Jungle's Heart: Padmavati, Meera, and Ashoka flee into the jungle; demon scouts pursue them; Padmavati transforms again to save her companions and barely returns to human form
- Ch 3 — The Hermit's Test: They reach the Rishi, who refuses to teach the ritual until Padmavati can transform and return by choice, not terror

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The Tiger's Teacher: The Rishi forces Padmavati to confront why she fears the transformation; she learns her grandfather summoned the original demon
- Ch 5 — The Voluntary Hunt: Padmavati successfully transforms by choice for the first time, hunting alongside a wild tiger pack; she begins to understand the beast is not evil
- Ch 6 — The Prison City: They scout Ravaka's stronghold; Padmavati is captured trying to reach her brothers; Ravaka reveals he needs her specifically—her tiger-soul can power the door alone
- Ch 7 — The Cage of Souls: Imprisoned, awaiting sacrifice, Padmavati despairs; the tiger screams to be released; she finally speaks to it, not as master to beast but as self to self
- Ch 8 — The Integration: In the cell, Padmavati merges fully with the tiger—not transformation but unification; she breaks free with a new kind of power

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The New Moon Battle: Padmavati reaches the Temple of the First Dawn as Ravaka begins the sacrifice; she performs the sealing ritual while fighting his demon guard, simultaneously beast and woman
- Ch 10 — The Whole Queen: The door seals, Ravaka is pulled into it, the captives are freed; Padmavati's brothers acknowledge her as rightful queen; she rules with the tiger beside her throne—not separate, but within

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Accepting the monster inside
**Deep theme:** We become whole not by defeating our dark aspects but by integrating them
**Thematic question:** If you contain something dangerous, is suppression strength—or is true strength making peace with your own nature?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The First Transformation**: The palace attack, seen from Padmavati's fragmenting perspective as human consciousness gives way to tiger fury, soldiers becoming prey
2. **The Pack Hunt**: Padmavati running with wild tigers through the jungle at night, learning their language of growls and glances, taking down a deer as one of them
3. **The Dual Combat**: The final battle, Padmavati shifting fluidly between forms mid-fight—tiger claws, human sword, tiger leap, human strategy

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *RRR* meets *Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon*
**Book comparables:** Samantha Shannon's *Priory of the Orange Tree*; Tasha Suri's *The Jasmine Throne*

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Lush and sensory, with a wild undercurrent. The prose should be rich and colorful in human scenes, stripped to pure action in tiger scenes. The internal conflict is as important as the external—let Padmavati's self-talk feel like a debate between two intelligent beings. The transformation scenes should be visceral and uncomfortable until they become beautiful.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Sanskrit-derived names require proper pronunciation: Padmavati (pad-ma-VA-ti), Ravaka (RA-va-ka), Ashoka (a-SHO-ka). Tiger scenes should have growling undertones. The transition between human and tiger voice is crucial—same actor, but shifted register. Demon voices should be multiple, layered.
