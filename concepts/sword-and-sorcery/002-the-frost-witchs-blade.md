# The Frost Witch's Blade

## Genre
Sword-and-Sorcery

## One-Line Premise
A shieldmaiden must wield her dead mother's cursed sword to stop a necromancer from waking the god frozen beneath the ice.

## Expanded Premise
Sigrid Kolsdottir swore she would never touch the black iron blade that killed her mother—a sword that drinks the warmth from whoever holds it. But when the sorcerer Halvard the Pale begins sacrificing entire villages to crack the glacier imprisoning the Dead God Fimbul, Sigrid is the only warrior who can reach his fortress before the spring thaw completes the ritual. Each time she draws the blade, she loses more of herself to the cold. She must kill Halvard before she becomes as frozen and merciless as the weapon she carries.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Sigrid Kolsdottir
**Age:** 27
**Occupation / Background:** Shieldmaiden and village protector of the remote fishing settlement of Kaldheim
**Core skill:** Unbreakable endurance—she can fight longer, march farther, and survive conditions that would kill anyone else
**Fatal flaw:** She buries her emotions so deep that she's forgotten how to feel anything, including the connections that make life worth living
**Character arc:** Believes strength means feeling nothing; learns that true strength is fighting for people you allow yourself to love; must choose between becoming an invincible weapon or remaining human
**Voice and register:** Speaks rarely and only when necessary, in blunt declarations. Her internal voice is richer, full of the poetry she'll never say aloud.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Halvard the Pale, last priest of the Dead God Fimbul
**Goal:** To awaken Fimbul and bring an eternal winter that will "purify" the world of the weak and unworthy, leaving only the strong
**Method:** Blood sacrifices that crack the divine ice; he's collecting "warm lives" to fuel the final shattering
**Connection to protagonist:** Halvard was once Sigrid's mother's lover; he gave her the cursed blade knowing it would eventually consume her, testing whether she was "worthy" of the new world

## Supporting Characters

**Bjorn One-Hand** — A berserker who lost his arm to frostbite; he follows Sigrid because he believes she's the prophesied "Cold-Born" and serves as her connection to warmth and humanity
**Yrsa the Red** — A völva (seeress) who can read the future in spilled blood; she knows how Sigrid's journey ends but refuses to tell her
**The Hollow Men** — Halvard's frozen soldiers, corpses animated by splinters of Fimbul's ice; they retain fragments of who they were, making them tragic enemies

## Setting

**Primary location:** Vinterheim, a Norse-inspired frozen archipelago at the top of the world
**Time period:** Secondary world equivalent of 9th century Viking age
**Atmosphere:** Endless white broken by black rock. The aurora burns green and violet overhead. Sound carries impossibly far across the ice. Everything smells of salt, seal fat, and coming snow.
**Key locations:** Kaldheim Village (Sigrid's home), The Glacier of Fimbul (prison of the dead god), Halvard's Bone Fortress (built from whale skeletons), The Drowned Longship (frozen shipwreck containing ancient weapons), The Screaming Crevasse (a chasm that leads to the god's heart)

## Central Conflict
Sigrid must cross three hundred miles of lethal frozen wilderness, defeat an army of the undead, and kill a sorcerer protected by divine ice—all while the sword she carries slowly steals her ability to feel warmth, emotion, and eventually her own heartbeat.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Fimbul remains imprisoned, Halvard dies, and the surviving villages can rebuild in spring
**If the protagonist fails:** The Dead God wakes, eternal winter spreads across the world, and all warm-blooded life slowly freezes
**Personal stakes:** Every use of the sword costs Sigrid more of her humanity—if she uses it too much, she'll survive but become as cold and empty as the blade itself

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Last Warm Night: Sigrid's village is attacked by Hollow Men; she's forced to draw her mother's blade for the first time to save the children, and feels her heart slow
- Ch 2 — The Cold Path: Sigrid sets out with Bjorn and Yrsa; she learns the scope of Halvard's plan from a dying messenger
- Ch 3 — The Drowned Longship: They shelter in the frozen wreck and are ambushed by Hollow Men; Sigrid kills them but realizes they were once her kinsmen

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The Screaming Wind: A blizzard traps them; Yrsa reveals the blade's full curse and Halvard's connection to Sigrid's mother
- Ch 5 — The Bone Fortress Siege: They ally with surviving warriors from other villages and assault Halvard's outer defenses
- Ch 6 — The Betrayal Cold: Halvard captures Bjorn and offers a trade—the sword for his life; Sigrid refuses and Bjorn is transformed into a Hollow Man before her eyes
- Ch 7 — The Frozen Heart: Sigrid can no longer feel her own heartbeat; she considers letting the blade consume her entirely to gain the power to kill Halvard
- Ch 8 — The Aurora's Voice: Yrsa's sacrifice reveals Fimbul's weakness; Sigrid chooses to fight as a human, not a weapon

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The God's Eye: Sigrid descends into the glacier, fights through Halvard's elite guard, and confronts him at the god's frozen face—he offers her a place at his side
- Ch 10 — The Thaw: Sigrid kills Halvard by driving the blade into Fimbul's eye, freezing them both forever; she survives but must live knowing she'll never feel true warmth again

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The cost of necessary weapons
**Deep theme:** We become what we use to survive unless we choose otherwise
**Thematic question:** How much of your humanity can you sacrifice before the victory isn't worth winning?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Village Burning Cold**: Hollow Men freeze the hearth-fires while villagers scream, forcing Sigrid to fight by moonlight on ice slicked with frozen blood
2. **The Blizzard Battle**: A three-way fight between Sigrid's band, Hollow Men, and wolves during a whiteout—all navigation by sound
3. **The God's Face**: The climax inside the glacier, fighting on the actual frozen features of a mile-wide divine face, Halvard wielding shards of the god's broken teeth as weapons

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *The Northman* meets *Conan the Barbarian*
**Book comparables:** Joe Abercrombie's *The Blade Itself*; Michael Moorcock's Elric saga

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Cold, beautiful, and brutal. The pacing alternates between long atmospheric marches across frozen landscapes and sudden explosive violence. Sentences should feel sharp, like ice cracking. Let the quiet moments breathe so the action hits harder. This book should make readers feel cold.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Sigrid's voice should be low and controlled, with Norse-influenced cadence—clipped consonants, minimal emotional inflection. Halvard speaks with seductive softness, almost a whisper. Old Norse-style names require consistent pronunciation. The Hollow Men should sound hollow—echoey, distant.
