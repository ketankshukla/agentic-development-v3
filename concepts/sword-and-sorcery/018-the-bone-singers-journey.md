# The Bone Singer's Journey

## Genre
Sword-and-Sorcery

## One-Line Premise
An outcast shaman who speaks to the bones of the dead must journey to the bottom of the world to rescue the Sea Mother before the ice swallows all life.

## Expanded Premise
Siku was born wrong—seeing through the eyes of the dead when she should have seen through the eyes of spirits. Her village tolerated her because her bone-songs kept the dead from rising, but when the great ice begins spreading unnaturally, killing all it touches, they blame her. Cast out to die, Siku discovers the truth: the Sea Mother Sedna has been kidnapped by Amarok, the Wolf-That-Eats-The-Sun, dragged to the bottom of the world. Without Sedna's blessing, the sea gives no food, and the ice advances. Siku must journey to the Frozen Abyss, speak to every dead thing she passes, and sing the song that even bones fear—the one that might free Sedna or doom them all.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Siku Qamaniq ("ice light")
**Age:** 22
**Occupation / Background:** Outcast bone-singer, able to speak to the dead through their remains and sing them to rest
**Core skill:** She can hear the memories in bones, command the dead, and sing songs of power that affect both living and dead
**Fatal flaw:** She believes she is fundamentally broken; she saves others not out of love but to prove she deserves to exist
**Character arc:** Believes she is wrong, cursed, a mistake of the spirits; discovers her power is not a flaw but a gift the world desperately needs; becomes the bridge between living, dead, and divine
**Voice and register:** Speaks rarely, in quiet observations. Her songs are powerful, but her conversation is hesitant. Internal voice is surprisingly analytical—she studies everything.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Amarok, the Wolf-That-Eats-The-Sun, a primal spirit of hunger and cold
**Goal:** To drag all warmth into the abyss, creating a world where only cold things survive—and he is the coldest
**Method:** By capturing Sedna, he cuts off the sea's bounty; the people starve; the ice expands; warmth itself dies
**Connection to protagonist:** Amarok killed Siku's mother years ago, swallowing her soul; Siku has heard her mother's bones singing in the wolf's belly

## Supporting Characters

**Tulugaq** — A raven-spirit bound to Siku's grandmother's skull; he provides guidance, sarcasm, and aerial reconnaissance
**Nanuk** — A polar bear who owes Siku a life-debt; he provides transportation, protection, and a warm body to sleep against
**The Drowned Fisherman** — A dead hunter who joins Siku's journey; he knows the underwater paths but fears what waits at the bottom

## Setting

**Primary location:** Silam, an Inuit-inspired realm of endless ice, dark seas, and spirits that walk openly
**Time period:** Secondary world equivalent of pre-contact Arctic
**Atmosphere:** The crack and groan of ice. Wind that cuts through fur. The aurora rippling overhead like curtains to another world. Silence so absolute that thoughts seem loud.
**Key locations:** The Exile Wastes (where Siku is cast out), The Bone Fields (whale graveyard), The Spirit Road (aurora pathway), The Drowned Village (underwater settlement), The Frozen Abyss (Amarok's lair)

## Central Conflict
Siku must cross the deadliest terrain in the world, navigate spirit roads that exist between states of being, and confront a primal wolf-god that has already swallowed her mother—all while proving to herself that an outcast can be a hero.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Sedna is freed, the sea provides again, and the great ice retreats
**If the protagonist fails:** All warmth dies, every village starves, and Amarok's eternal winter covers the world
**Personal stakes:** Siku's mother's soul is trapped inside Amarok; freeing Sedna might mean freeing her mother—or might require destroying the wolf with her mother still inside

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Casting Out: Siku is blamed for the ice and exiled; alone on the frozen waste, she hears the dead whispering the truth about Sedna
- Ch 2 — The Raven's Bargain: Tulugaq finds her; he was bound to her grandmother and offers guidance in exchange for eventual freedom; he reveals Amarok's lair location
- Ch 3 — The Bear's Debt: Siku saves Nanuk from hunters; the polar bear pledges himself to her journey; they begin crossing the Bone Fields

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The Whale's Memory: In the Bone Fields, Siku sings to the whale skeletons, learning the underwater paths from their ancient memories
- Ch 5 — The Spirit Road: They walk the aurora to bypass the worst of the ice; Siku sees her mother's spirit briefly, calling from inside Amarok
- Ch 6 — The Drowned Village: Siku dives to a village killed by Amarok's ice; the Drowned Fisherman joins her, knowing the way deeper
- Ch 7 — The Mother's Voice: At the edge of the Frozen Abyss, Siku hears her mother's soul clearly—warning her away; Siku descends anyway
- Ch 8 — The Wolf's Throat: Siku enters Amarok's territory; the cold is intelligent, hunting her; she sings a fire-song from bones of creatures that remember warmth

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Abyss Battle: Siku confronts Amarok; she cannot defeat him physically; instead, she sings to every soul he's swallowed—including her mother—and they rise against him from within
- Ch 10 — The Mother's Rest: Amarok is destroyed; Sedna is freed; Siku's mother is released but chooses to pass on, finally at peace; Siku returns as a hero, welcomed where she was once cursed

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** An outcast saves the world
**Deep theme:** What the world calls wrong may be the exact medicine it needs; difference is not deficiency
**Thematic question:** If everyone tells you that you're broken, how do you learn to see yourself as whole—and can you save a world that rejected you?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Bone Field Symphony**: Siku walking among thousand-year-old whale skeletons, singing them awake, their memories rising like visible ghosts around her
2. **The Aurora Walk**: Traveling on the northern lights themselves, transparent pathways through the sky, with spirits walking parallel roads
3. **The Internal Rebellion**: Inside Amarok's belly, a thousand swallowed souls rising against their devourer, Siku's mother leading the charge

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *Frozen* (the fairy tale, not Disney) meets *The Revenant*
**Book comparables:** Nghi Vo's *The Empress of Salt and Fortune*; Ursula K. Le Guin's *The Left Hand of Darkness*

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Quiet and relentless, like Arctic survival itself. Long stretches of endurance punctuated by sudden, desperate action. The cold should be a character—intelligent, patient, hungry. Siku's songs should be rendered as prose-poems, rhythmic and strange. The emotional climax is reunion and release, not just victory.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Inuit-inspired names need consistent pronunciation: Siku (SEE-koo), Tulugaq (too-LOO-gak), Nanuk (NA-nook), Sedna (SED-na), Amarok (AH-ma-rok). The bone-songs require special attention—slower, rhythmic, almost chanted. Arctic ambience: wind, ice cracking, deep silence. Wolf sounds should be terrifying—not animal but primal.
