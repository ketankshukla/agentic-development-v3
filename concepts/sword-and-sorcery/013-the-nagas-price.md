# The Naga's Price

## Genre
Sword-and-Sorcery

## One-Line Premise
A temple thief discovers the golden statue he stole is a living god, and he must return it to its throne before the monsoon drowns a kingdom.

## Expanded Premise
Arjun has stolen from every temple in the Khmer Empire and never been caught—until he steals a golden naga statue from the most sacred vault in Angkor. The moment he touches it, he discovers the statue is no statue: it's Muchilinda, the Serpent King, trapped in gold by an ancient curse. Muchilinda once protected the kingdom from the Monsoon Demon Varuna; without his power on the throne, Varuna will unleash a flood to drown the entire empire. Arjun has seven days to return Muchilinda to his temple, break the curse, and restore the god to his throne—while every priest, soldier, and sorcerer in the kingdom hunts the thief who stole their protector.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Arjun Devakula
**Age:** 33
**Occupation / Background:** Master temple thief, former acolyte who lost his faith and found his fingers
**Core skill:** He can break any lock, bypass any ward, and escape any pursuit; he knows temple architecture and sacred geography like no one else
**Fatal flaw:** He trusts no one because he believes everyone is exactly as selfish as he is
**Character arc:** Believes the gods are lies and faith is a tool of control; discovers through Muchilinda that some powers are real and some causes are worth dying for; becomes a guardian of the faith he once robbed
**Voice and register:** Speaks in quick, deflecting humor, always ready with a lie. Internal voice is more honest—calculating but increasingly conscience-bound.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Varuna the Drowner, demon lord of the monsoon
**Goal:** To reclaim dominion over the wet season, drowning anyone who defies him and demanding blood sacrifice for rain
**Method:** With Muchilinda trapped, Varuna is flooding the kingdom incrementally—rivers rising, storms worsening—until the final monsoon surge destroys everything
**Connection to protagonist:** Arjun's grandfather made a pact with Varuna to escape a flood; that inherited debt is why Arjun could touch the cursed statue without dying

## Supporting Characters

**Muchilinda** — The Serpent King, trapped in gold, speaking only in Arjun's mind; he's ancient, wise, and increasingly fond of the thief who must save him
**Sita** — A temple dancer who helps Arjun because Muchilinda appeared in her dreams; she provides cover, guidance, and eventually love
**The High Priest Jayavarman** — The man who cursed Muchilinda to steal his power; he appears to be hunting Arjun but actually wants to ensure the curse is never broken

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Naga Empire, a Khmer-inspired civilization of temple mountains, jungle cities, and sacred waterways
**Time period:** Secondary world equivalent of 12th century
**Atmosphere:** Steam rising from temple stones. Lotus ponds and crocodile-haunted moats. The smell of incense and monsoon rain. Gold everywhere—on towers, on statues, on the faces of gods.
**Key locations:** Angkor Thom (capital city), The Temple of the Serpent Throne (Muchilinda's home), The Drowned Village (first warning), The Underground River (secret passage), The Storm Palace (Varuna's manifestation point)

## Central Conflict
Arjun must navigate a kingdom hunting him, decode the curse binding Muchilinda, and reach the Serpent Throne before the final monsoon—all while the god in his bag grows heavier, the water rises higher, and his own skepticism wars with what he's witnessing.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Muchilinda is restored, Varuna is bound again, and the monsoon returns to its natural rhythm
**If the protagonist fails:** The kingdom drowns, a million people die, and Varuna reclaims his ancient dominion over rain and flood
**Personal stakes:** Arjun's grandfather's debt means he can serve as the vessel to break the curse—but only if he willingly gives himself to the god; he must choose faith or freedom

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Golden Theft: Arjun steals the naga statue; Muchilinda awakens in his mind; the temple alarms reveal the theft and the hunt begins
- Ch 2 — The Drowned Warning: Arjun witnesses a village destroyed by unnatural flooding; Muchilinda explains the stakes; Arjun agrees to help for payment
- Ch 3 — The Temple Dancer: Sita finds Arjun; she was sent by dream-visions; she offers to guide him through the sacred passages if he promises to save her people

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The Underground River: They travel the hidden waterways beneath Angkor; Varuna's water-demons attack; Arjun kills them but realizes they're just scouts
- Ch 5 — The Priest's Truth: Jayavarman captures them; Arjun learns the High Priest cursed Muchilinda to gain personal power—and is terrified the thief might succeed
- Ch 6 — The Broken Faith: Arjun escapes but Sita is taken hostage; Muchilinda urges him to abandon her; Arjun refuses and mounts a rescue that costs him his escape route
- Ch 7 — The Rising Waters: The penultimate monsoon hits; Angkor begins to flood; Arjun must climb the temple mountain as water rises around him
- Ch 8 — The Vessel's Choice: At the Serpent Throne, Arjun realizes only his willing sacrifice—giving up his freedom to become Muchilinda's host—can break the curse

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Storm Palace: Varuna manifests physically to stop the restoration; Arjun fights a demon made of flood water while Sita performs the restoration ritual
- Ch 10 — The Serpent Reborn: Arjun accepts the bond; Muchilinda rises restored; Varuna is cast down; Arjun survives but is forever changed—he sees with the serpent's eyes and must guard the temple he once robbed

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** A thief becomes a guardian
**Deep theme:** Faith is not belief in stories—it's willingness to sacrifice for something larger than yourself
**Thematic question:** If the gods are real, does that vindicate the institutions that serve them—or only prove that power exists beyond human understanding?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Angkor Heist**: A theft sequence through the most elaborate temple complex in the world, dodging priests, guards, and magical wards
2. **The Underground River Chase**: A boat pursuit through flooded temple basements, crocodile attacks, water-demons rising from the deep
3. **The Flood Battle**: Fighting Varuna's avatar on the steps of the Serpent Throne as water explodes upward and lightning splits the sky

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *Indiana Jones: Raiders of the Lost Ark* meets *The Raid*
**Book comparables:** Fonda Lee's *Jade City*; Michael Moorcock's *Elric of Melniboné*

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Heist energy meets religious epic. The pacing should be relentless—Arjun never stops moving, never stops scheming, never stops running. The relationship between Arjun and Muchilinda is the emotional heart: a cynic and a god learning to trust each other. Let the water be omnipresent; every scene should feel humid, damp, drowning.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Sanskrit and Khmer-derived names need careful pronunciation: Arjun (AR-jun), Muchilinda (moo-chi-LIN-da), Jayavarman (ja-ya-VAR-man), Varuna (va-ROO-na). Muchilinda's voice should be resonant, ancient, serpentine. Water sounds should underscore most scenes. The monsoon should build—light rain to heavy rain to storm to flood.
