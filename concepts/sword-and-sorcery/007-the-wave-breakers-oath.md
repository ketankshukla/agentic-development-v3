# The Wave Breaker's Oath

## Genre
Sword-and-Sorcery

## One-Line Premise
A banished navigator must sail into the throat of a volcanic god to rescue his people from an island that's sinking into the sea.

## Expanded Premise
Kainoa broke the sacred law: he looked upon the face of the Sleeping Fire, the volcano god who protects his island home. Banished to die on the endless ocean, he survived by making a pact with a sea-spirit—a pact that now binds his life to the waters. Years later, drifting past his home island, Kainoa sees the impossible: the volcano is awakening, and the Sleeping Fire is dragging the entire island down into the magma chamber as punishment for his transgression. Only Kainoa, who has looked upon the god's face and lived, can enter the volcano's heart, find the god's anger-stone, and shatter it before everyone he loves drowns in fire.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Kainoa Moana ("the sea")
**Age:** 32
**Occupation / Background:** Disgraced master navigator, now a wanderer bound to a sea-spirit's canoe
**Core skill:** He can read the ocean like a language—currents, star paths, wave patterns tell him exactly where he is and what approaches
**Fatal flaw:** He refuses to ask others to bear burdens for him, even when sharing the weight would save everyone
**Character arc:** Believes his crime makes him unworthy of his people; discovers that transgression and redemption are part of the same wave; becomes a navigator who leads not by being pure but by being willing to sin for his people
**Voice and register:** Speaks in the rhythms of ocean voyaging, everything measured against water and stars. Internally, he still talks to the sea-spirit who saved him.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Sleeping Fire, a volcano god waking in anger
**Goal:** To punish the transgression by drowning the island that failed to enforce the sacred law
**Method:** Slowly drawing the island down into its caldera through divine will; no amount of sacrifice can appease a god that feels betrayed
**Connection to protagonist:** Kainoa looked upon the god's face and saw weakness—a moment of loneliness in eternity; the god is punishing everyone because it cannot bear that a mortal knows its secret

## Supporting Characters

**Mara** — Kainoa's former betrothed, now the island's high priestess; she's the one who enforced his banishment, and she's been sacrificing herself to slow the god's rage
**Tangaroa** — The sea-spirit who saved Kainoa; he's ancient, capricious, and claims to be an enemy of the Sleeping Fire—but his true motives are unclear
**Kai** — Kainoa's younger brother, who became a warrior because Kainoa became an exile; he hates Kainoa but must guide him through the island's changed landscape

## Setting

**Primary location:** Te Moana Nui, a Polynesian-inspired chain of volcanic islands in an endless tropical ocean
**Time period:** Secondary world equivalent of pre-European contact Pacific
**Atmosphere:** Salt spray and coconut smoke. The roar of surf against black rock. Tattoos that tell lineage stories. Nights so full of stars they cast shadows on the water.
**Key locations:** The Banished Shoals (reef where exiles are left to die), Makana Island (home), The Sleeping Fire Caldera (volcanic god's heart), The Sea Spirit's Court (underwater realm), The Star Stones (navigation markers on the ocean floor)

## Central Conflict
Kainoa must return to a home that hates him, navigate through a volcano that is actively trying to kill him, and destroy a god's heart-stone without becoming trapped in the caldera forever—all while his sea-spirit patron may be manipulating him for ancient revenge.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The island stabilizes, his people survive, and the Sleeping Fire returns to true sleep
**If the protagonist fails:** Eight thousand people drown in fire, and the Sleeping Fire's rage spreads to the entire island chain
**Personal stakes:** Kainoa's pact with the sea-spirit has a price: if he sets foot on land without the spirit's permission, he dies; entering the volcano means walking the line between shore and deep

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Sinking Home: Kainoa sees his island descending into the sea; Tangaroa reveals the only way to stop it and grants limited permission to walk the shore
- Ch 2 — The Exile Returns: Kainoa lands on Makana for the first time in nine years; Kai and a warrior band attack him before Mara intervenes
- Ch 3 — The Priestess's Price: Mara reveals what she's been sacrificing to slow the god—her own life force; Kainoa realizes she has days left, not weeks

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The Star Stone Path: To reach the caldera's secret entrance, Kainoa must dive to the ocean floor and follow the ancient navigation markers; he nearly dies from depth sickness
- Ch 5 — The Warrior's Grudge: Kai joins the expedition out of duty, not love; during a lava-tube ambush, Kai saves Kainoa's life and begins to understand why his brother broke the law
- Ch 6 — The Spirit's Truth: Tangaroa reveals his real plan—he wants the Sleeping Fire destroyed, not pacified; Kainoa realizes the sea-spirit is using him for ancient vengeance
- Ch 7 — The Caldera's Throat: They enter the volcano; the heat kills two of their warriors, and Kai is trapped under fallen rock; Kainoa must choose between saving his brother and reaching the anger-stone
- Ch 8 — The God's Face Again: Kainoa sees the Sleeping Fire's face a second time—and speaks to it, learning why it's truly angry: not at him, but at its own loneliness; he offers a different solution

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Sea Inside Fire: Kainoa makes a new pact—he will carry a fragment of the Sleeping Fire with him across the endless ocean, giving the god experiences beyond its caldera; Tangaroa attacks to prevent the bargain
- Ch 10 — The Twin Spirits: Kainoa defeats Tangaroa by binding him to the fire-fragment, forcing the two ancient rivals to coexist within him; the island rises, Mara survives, and Kainoa sails again—but now he carries two gods

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Redemption for transgression
**Deep theme:** Sometimes breaking the law is the only way to save what the law was meant to protect
**Thematic question:** If you've been cast out for seeing too much, do you owe the community that rejected you—or is surviving its own kind of truth?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Star Stone Dive**: Kainoa descending through luminescent ocean depths, following glowing markers while giant shadows move in the darkness
2. **The Lava Walk**: A battle through rivers of flowing magma, warriors leaping between stone islands as the rock melts beneath them
3. **The Spirit War**: Tangaroa's ocean-manifestation attacking Kainoa inside the volcano, water and fire weapons clashing

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *Moana* meets *Apocalypse Now* (the river journey structure)
**Book comparables:** N.K. Jemisin's *The Broken Earth* trilogy; Ursula K. Le Guin's *A Wizard of Earthsea*

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Mythic and oceanic—the prose should roll like waves, building to crests and subsiding to troughs. The vast emptiness of the Pacific should feel present even in enclosed spaces. This is a story about scale: tiny humans against infinite gods and endless ocean. Let the spirituality be practical, not mystical.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Hawaiian/Polynesian pronunciation for names: Kainoa (kai-NO-ah), Tangaroa (tan-ga-RO-ah), Mara (MAH-rah). The ocean ambient sound should underlie most narration. Chanting and drum rhythms for ritual scenes. The volcano scenes need crackling, rumbling bass. Sea-spirit speech should have aquatic resonance.
