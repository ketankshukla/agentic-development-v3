# The Thousand-Year Debt

## Genre
Epic Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A street thief discovers he owes a debt to the Fae Courts that spans a thousand years—and the only way to pay it is to steal the sun.

## Expanded Premise
Tomás Cuervo has survived the slums of Città Nera by being the fastest thief in the city and never taking a job he couldn't escape. When he picks the wrong pocket, he discovers a contract binding his bloodline to the Fae—his ancestor stole something from them a millennium ago, and the debt compounds with interest. Now Tomás owes the Summer Court nothing less than the sun itself: the literal orb that hangs above the Fae realm. If he fails to deliver within one year, the Fae will claim the souls of every living member of his bloodline—including his pregnant sister. To steal the sun, Tomás must navigate two rival Fae Courts, recruit a crew of the most dangerous specialists in either realm, and pull off the greatest heist in the history of both worlds.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Tomás Cuervo
**Age:** 26
**Occupation / Background:** Master thief from the criminal underworld of Città Nera, raised in an orphanage, reunited with his sister only three years ago
**Core skill:** Preternatural spatial awareness—he can map any building, vault, or fortress by walking through it once; in the Fae realm, this extends to sensing magical wards
**Fatal flaw:** He's spent his whole life trusting no one; building a crew means learning to rely on people, which terrifies him
**Character arc:** Begins as a loner who sees people as tools or threats; ends understanding that some heists—some lives—require trust, even when trust can break you
**Voice and register:** Sardonic, quick, deflecting with humor. He narrates like he's casing the room, noticing exits and weaknesses. His internal monologue is lonelier than his bravado suggests.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Lord of the Summer Court, Obereth the Radiant
**Goal:** To recover what was stolen—an artifact that could end the eternal balance between Summer and Winter; he's bound by Fae law to pursue the debt but would rather torture Tomás than see him succeed
**Method:** Fae hunters, impossible contracts with hidden clauses, manipulation of other Court nobles, bending the rules of the deal without breaking them
**Connection to protagonist:** Obereth was personally humiliated by Tomás's ancestor; this is vendetta wrapped in legality

## Supporting Characters

**Sienna, the Breaker** — A former knight turned safecracker; she can shatter any physical barrier but not the emotional walls she's built; Tomás's eventual right hand
**Whisper** — A changeling spy who serves neither Court; their loyalties are genuinely unclear until the final act
**The Countess of Frozen Tears** — A Winter Court noble who offers Tomás a deal: steal the sun and give it to Winter instead; enemy of Summer, unreliable ally at best

## Setting

**Primary location:** The mortal city of Città Nera (inspired by Renaissance Venice) and the endless Fae realms beyond
**Time period:** A secondary-world equivalent of 16th century, with canal cities, masked nobility, and thieves' guilds
**Atmosphere:** Città Nera is shadow and candlelight, water and whispers. The Fae realm is too beautiful, too sharp, colors that hurt and music that enslaves. The contrast between mortal grit and Fae glamour creates constant tension.
**Key locations:** The Thieves' Market beneath Città Nera, the Gate of Thorns (passage to Fae), the Summer Palace (target location), the Winter Court's Obsidian Maze (where Tomás must negotiate), the Vault of the Sun (final heist location)

## Central Conflict
Tomás must assemble a crew capable of operating in a realm where the rules of reality are negotiable, navigate the politics of immortal beings who view mortals as entertainment, and steal an object that may be more than physical—while the Summer Lord deploys every hunter and trap to stop him.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** His family is free; the balance of the Fae Courts shifts dramatically (for better or worse); mortals gain a foothold against Fae predation
**If the protagonist fails:** His sister, her unborn child, and every Cuervo descendant are claimed by Summer forever, their souls woven into eternal servitude
**Personal stakes:** Tomás must choose whether to betray crew members who trust him when the price of success becomes clear

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Tomás's latest heist goes wrong; the contract is revealed; his sister is marked; he has one year
- Ch 2 — The Pull: He enters the Fae realm to negotiate terms, discovers the sun is a real object that can be stolen, and barely escapes the Summer Palace alive
- Ch 3 — First Contact: He begins recruiting a crew; Sienna signs on; Whisper approaches with information; the Countess makes her offer

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Full crew assembled; they attempt to case the Vault of the Sun; realize it exists in a space that changes constantly
- Ch 5 — The Push: A successful theft of a minor Summer artifact proves the concept; the crew bonds; the plan crystallizes
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Whisper is revealed as a Summer agent—or so it seems; half the crew is captured; Tomás must choose between the mission and rescuing them
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Rescues the crew but loses three months; his sister's mark advances; the Winter Countess's price for help becomes clear: she wants Tomás, bound to her Court forever
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Tomás accepts the Countess's deal, knowing he'll lose his freedom; crew discovers this and refuses to let him sacrifice himself alone—they're all in, whatever the cost

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The Heist of the Sun—infiltration, betrayals, impossible escapes, Tomás facing Obereth directly while the crew executes the extraction
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The sun is stolen but cannot be given to either Court without catastrophe; Tomás finds a third option that frees his family and binds him to neither Court—but changes the nature of both realms forever

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The price of freedom
**Deep theme:** The debts we inherit are not our fault, but how we pay them defines who we become
**Thematic question:** What do you owe to family, to friends, and to yourself—and what happens when those debts conflict?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Masked Ball Infiltration**: The crew attends a Summer Court celebration in disguise, gathering intelligence while avoiding recognition, a dance of lies and glamour
2. **The Vault Approach**: Navigating a space that reshapes itself based on intention, requiring the crew to "want" different things to keep the vault stable
3. **The Sun Extraction**: Tomás physically climbing a tower of crystallized light while the Summer Lord's hunters close in from below and his crew holds the escape route open

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Ocean's Eleven meets Pan's Labyrinth
**Book comparables:** Six of Crows by Leigh Bardugo, The Lies of Locke Lamora by Scott Lynch

## Tone and Pacing Notes
This is a heist novel with fantasy elements, not a fantasy novel with heist elements. The pacing follows heist structure—setup, complications, execution—with each phase accelerating. Banter matters. The crew dynamics carry as much weight as the action. The prose should be clever without being cute, atmospheric without being slow.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Tomás's voice is quick and confident with underlying tension. Sienna is gruff and direct. Whisper's voice should be androgynous and slightly unsettling. Fae characters speak with formal precision. Italian-influenced names: "Città Nera" (chee-TAH NEH-ra), "Tomás Cuervo" (toh-MAHS KWER-voh). Heist sequences need crisp, staccato delivery.
