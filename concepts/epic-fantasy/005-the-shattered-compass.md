# The Shattered Compass

## Genre
Epic Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A cartographer who can map the future discovers her gift is slowly erasing the past—and she must chart a course to a place that doesn't exist to save everyone she's ever loved.

## Expanded Premise
Yara Okonkwo draws maps that show not what is, but what will be. For the Merchant Princes of the Sunstone Coast, her gift is priceless—trade routes, storm paths, enemy positions. What no one knows is that every future she maps erases the past that led to it: events cease to have happened, people cease to have existed, and only Yara remembers. When she draws a map showing a cataclysm that will destroy the Coast in three months, she discovers the only salvation is a legendary island that exists outside normal time—a place she cannot map because it has no future. To reach it, she must navigate backward through her own erased timeline, recover people and places that no longer exist, and assemble a crew from throughout history to find what may be a myth.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Yara Okonkwo
**Age:** 29
**Occupation / Background:** Royal Cartographer to the Merchant Princes, daughter of fishermen, trained in secret by the last master of prophetic cartography before his gift consumed him
**Core skill:** Temporal cartography—she can draw maps of futures that then become inevitable; she can also, with great difficulty, map the echoes of erased pasts
**Fatal flaw:** She has erased so many people accidentally that she's stopped letting herself care about anyone, believing attachment makes the loss worse
**Character arc:** Begins isolated by guilt and fear of her own power; ends accepting that love is worth the risk of loss, and that some connections transcend even erased time
**Voice and register:** Precise, observational, with the detachment of someone afraid to feel too much. Her narration is full of detail—she sees the world as a cartographer, noting landmarks, distances, relationships between things. The emotion she suppresses leaks through in what she chooses to describe.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Tide That Was, a sentient temporal phenomenon that feeds on erased time
**Goal:** To consume all erased moments, growing larger until it can erase the present itself—it has been following Yara since her first prophetic map, growing stronger with each erasure
**Method:** It manifests as impossible weather, temporal loops, and twisted versions of erased people who serve its will; it cannot exist where time flows normally but can strike at the edges
**Connection to protagonist:** Yara created it inadvertently—her first map erased her twin brother, and his stolen existence became the seed of the Tide

## Supporting Characters

**Captain Idris Bello** — A navigator who sails the erased seas between timelines; gruff, competent, and secretly terrified of the Tide that took his homeland
**Chioma, the Remembered** — A woman Yara erased years ago who somehow persists in fragmented form; she is anger and grief made manifest, but also the key to reaching the island
**The Prince of Sunstone** — The ruler who employs Yara; he learns the truth about her gift and must decide whether to exploit her or protect her

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Sunstone Coast, a wealthy trading civilization inspired by West African coastal empires, and the Erased Seas beyond conventional reality
**Time period:** A secondary-world equivalent of 15th century, with advanced navigation, complex trade networks, and university cities
**Atmosphere:** Sun-bleached stone and vibrant markets, the smell of salt and spices. The Erased Seas are their opposite—gray, silent, populated by the ghosts of unmade things. The contrast between vibrant present and haunted past drives the book's visual language.
**Key locations:** The Cartographer's Tower in Sunstone City, the Harbor of Lost Ships (entrance to the Erased Seas), the Temporal Maelstrom (where timelines collide), the Island Outside Time (the destination)

## Central Conflict
Yara must travel through seas made of erased time, confront the monster her gift created, and reach an island that has no future—all while maintaining her sanity as she encounters everyone she's accidentally unmade, including the twin brother she can barely remember.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The cataclysm is prevented, the Tide is contained, and Yara gains control of her gift—no longer erasing blindly
**If the protagonist fails:** The Tide consumes the present, erasing the Sunstone Coast and eventually the world; reality collapses into a single eternal moment of unmaking
**Personal stakes:** Yara might be able to restore some of what she erased—including her brother—but the cost may be erasing her own existence instead

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Yara maps the cataclysm, watches someone she spoke to yesterday vanish from everyone's memory but hers; the Tide stirs
- Ch 2 — The Pull: The Prince demands she fix it; Yara reveals her gift's true nature; she must find the Island Outside Time or accept the apocalypse
- Ch 3 — First Contact: She locates Captain Idris, who has been waiting for someone like her; they enter the Erased Seas; Chioma appears, furious and fragmented

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Navigating the Erased Seas reveals the scope of what Yara has unmade—entire cities, wars, peoples; the Tide hunts them
- Ch 5 — The Push: They find a map fragment that can lead to the Island, hidden in an erased library; Chioma begins to stabilize, remembering who she was
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The Tide catches them; Idris's ship is damaged; they crash in the Temporal Maelstrom where Yara confronts visions of everyone she erased, including her brother Kofi
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Yara cannot distinguish real from memory; Chioma must guide her; Kofi's ghost offers a terrible bargain—he'll lead them to the Island if Yara takes his place in erasure
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Yara refuses to erase herself; she realizes she can map the Tide itself, giving it a future—and therefore an ending

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The race to the Island while mapping the Tide's destruction, Chioma fully restored and fighting alongside, Kofi's ghost choosing to help without conditions
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The Island exists; the Tide is contained in a map that gives it an end; the cataclysm is prevented; Yara accepts her gift's responsibility, founding an order of cartographers who map responsibly—and Kofi, impossibly, is restored when the Tide releases what it consumed

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The responsibility of power
**Deep theme:** We cannot undo our mistakes, but we can carry them forward and build something better from the ruins
**Thematic question:** If changing the future erases the past, is any progress worth the cost?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Mapping of the Cataclysm**: Yara in trance, drawing a map that burns with golden light, watching the future unfold in ink and horror across the page
2. **The Voyage Through Erased Seas**: Sailing through gray waters where half-formed cities rise and fall, crewed by ghosts who don't know they're dead
3. **The Confrontation in the Maelstrom**: A fracturing reality where Yara must navigate through versions of herself, past and future, to reach the eye of the storm

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Inception meets Pirates of the Caribbean
**Book comparables:** The Cartographers by Peng Shepherd, Piranesi by Susanna Clarke

## Tone and Pacing Notes
This novel moves between action and introspection, with the navigation sequences providing thriller momentum and the erased-timeline encounters slowing into haunted wonder. The prose should be lush when describing the normal world, spare and unsettling in the erased spaces. Time itself should feel malleable in the reading experience.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Yara's voice is calm and measured, becoming strained in emotional moments. Idris is deep-voiced and weathered. Chioma shifts between fury and confusion, her voice fragmenting. Kofi speaks with echo effects, slightly out of sync. Yoruba-influenced names: "Yara" (YAH-rah), "Okonkwo" (oh-KAWN-kwoh), "Chioma" (chee-OH-mah). Erased Sea sequences need slower, dreamlike pacing.
