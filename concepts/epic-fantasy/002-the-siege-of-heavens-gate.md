# The Siege of Heaven's Gate

## Genre
Epic Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A soldier who died and returned must lead an army to storm the afterlife before the gods can destroy the mortal world.

## Expanded Premise
Sergeant Korvun died at the Battle of Ash Valley, spent three days in the Halls of the Dead, and returned when his body was thrown on a pyre—the fire somehow reversing his death. He returns to find the world changed: the gods have declared humanity a failed experiment, and in six months, they will unmake the mortal realm entirely. The only way to stop them is to reach Heaven's Gate, the physical passage between worlds, and hold it long enough for mortal mages to sever the gods' connection to creation. Korvun, who alone has walked both realms, must lead an army of the desperate and the damned up the Mountain of Ascension to besiege the gods themselves.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Korvun Ashfall
**Age:** 41
**Occupation / Background:** Veteran sergeant of the Valdric infantry, twenty-three years of service, never sought promotion—he leads from the line, not the rear
**Core skill:** He can see the threads that connect souls to both life and death, allowing him to strike where enemies are most vulnerable and lead troops through terrain that would kill others
**Fatal flaw:** He believes he should have stayed dead and unconsciously seeks a worthy death rather than a worthy life
**Character arc:** Begins as a man waiting to die properly; ends as a man who chooses to live for those who depend on him, even knowing his death will come
**Voice and register:** Rough, practical, blackly humorous. Speaks in the short sentences of a man who has given too many orders and buried too many friends. His internal thoughts are more poetic than he'd ever admit.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Divine Consensus—the seven gods who created humanity, led by Aurethon the Lawgiver
**Goal:** To unmake humanity and start over; they believe mortals have become too corrupt, too violent, too broken to be redeemed
**Method:** The Divine Mandate—a ritual that will dissolve the mortal realm back into primordial chaos; requires all seven gods acting in concert at Heaven's Gate
**Connection to protagonist:** Korvun's return was not an accident—one of the seven, Morenna the Merciful, broke divine law to bring him back, believing he could change her siblings' minds

## Supporting Characters

**Morenna the Merciful** — The goddess who resurrected Korvun, now stripped of most power and hunted by her siblings; she serves as guide and fragile hope
**Captain Dessa Ironhand** — A noblewoman-turned-soldier who takes command of the army when the generals die; respects Korvun but struggles with his willingness to sacrifice himself
**The Burned King** — A mad prophet leading a cult of those who want to die in divine fire; obstacle and eventual ally when Korvun shows him a better death

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Kingdom of Valdric and the Mountain of Ascension, a spire that pierces the boundary between mortal and divine realms
**Time period:** A secondary world in post-medieval collapse, empires fallen and city-states rising
**Atmosphere:** A world that knows it's ending—desperate prayers in crowded temples, soldiers volunteering for suicide missions, artists painting their final works. The Mountain itself is beauty and terror, with reality becoming increasingly strange as one ascends.
**Key locations:** The Ruined City of Valdric (base camp), the Pilgrim's Stair (ancient path up the mountain), the Cloud Fortress (divine defensive position), the Bridge of Souls (final approach), Heaven's Gate (the threshold itself)

## Central Conflict
Korvun must lead an army up an impossible mountain, breaking through divine fortifications, while contending with desertion, resource depletion, and the temptation to simply accept the coming end—all while the gods themselves deploy angels, holy warriors, and reality-warping miracles against him.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Humanity survives, the gods are cut off from creation, and mortals must chart their own course for the first time—a terrifying freedom
**If the protagonist fails:** Every human soul is unmade, not even death to escape to—simply cessation, as if humanity never existed
**Personal stakes:** Korvun carries the souls of everyone who died at Ash Valley within him; if he falls before reaching the Gate, they are lost forever

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Korvun wakes on his own funeral pyre, staggers into a world preparing for apocalypse; Morenna appears and explains what he is and what must be done
- Ch 2 — The Pull: He reaches the army camp at Valdric, finds it in chaos—the generals are dead, killed by divine assassins; reluctantly, he rallies the survivors
- Ch 3 — First Contact: First assault on the Pilgrim's Stair, angels descend, hundreds die—but Korvun's sight allows them to break through the first defensive line

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: They discover the full scope of divine defenses; the Burned King's cult blocks a crucial pass; Korvun must convert enemies to allies
- Ch 5 — The Push: Allied with the cult, they storm the Cloud Fortress in a brutal three-day battle; victory seems possible
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Aurethon himself manifests, offers Korvun a deal—his soul and Morenna's in exchange for humanity's survival as diminished, servile beings; Dessa and half the army want to accept
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Army splits, Korvun continues with the true believers, Aurethon begins accelerating the Divine Mandate—they now have days, not weeks
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: In the ruins of the Cloud Fortress, Korvun confronts his death-wish, speaks to the souls he carries, and chooses to fight not for death but for them

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The Battle of Heaven's Gate—Korvun's diminished force against the remaining six gods; Morenna sacrifices herself to open the Gate; Korvun holds the threshold as the mages work
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The gods are severed; Korvun survives, to his own surprise; the world must rebuild without divine guidance; he becomes the keeper of Heaven's Gate, guarding the threshold from both sides

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Humanity's right to exist despite its flaws
**Deep theme:** Hope is not the belief that things will improve—it is the refusal to stop trying even when destruction seems certain
**Thematic question:** If our creators deem us failures, do we have the right to disagree?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Charge up the Pilgrim's Stair**: Ten thousand soldiers ascending a mountain path while angels dive from clouds, swords of light cutting through formations, catapults hurling sacred fire
2. **The Storm of the Cloud Fortress**: A siege in three dimensions, soldiers climbing crystalline walls while gravity shifts, Korvun dueling an archangel on a bridge of frozen lightning
3. **The Holding of the Gate**: Korvun alone in the threshold, reality fracturing around him, fighting manifestations of divine will while mortal mages desperately work behind him

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King meets Gladiator
**Book comparables:** The Blacktongue Thief by Christopher Buehlman, The Rage of Dragons by Evan Winter

## Tone and Pacing Notes
This is a war novel that happens to involve gods. The pace is relentless—logistics, casualties, hard choices. Quiet moments exist only in contrast to what's coming. The prose should be muscular and direct, the battles exhausting to read in the best way. Grief is earned, not manufactured.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Korvun's voice should be gravelly and tired, with unexpected warmth in quiet moments. Morenna speaks with ethereal resonance, slightly echoing. The Burned King's voice is ash-rough and fervent. Military jargon should flow naturally. "Aurethon" (aw-RETH-on), "Morenna" (mor-EN-na), "Valdric" (VAL-drik). Battle scenes need varying tempo—slow during horror, rapid during action.
