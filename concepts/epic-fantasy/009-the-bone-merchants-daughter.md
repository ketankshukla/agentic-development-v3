# The Bone Merchant's Daughter

## Genre
Epic Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A young woman who can hear the dead through their bones must navigate an underworld auction where gods, demons, and mortals bid for the remains of a murdered titan.

## Expanded Premise
Kira Ossene inherited her father's trade: buying and selling bones, each whispering secrets of its owner's life. It's a marginal living in the sprawling undercity of the Immortal Bazaar—until the Collector arrives with something unprecedented. The bones of a titan, a being that predates the gods, murdered ten thousand years ago. Every faction in the Bazaar wants them: death cults seeking resurrection, demons wanting to consume its power, gods who fear what it might reveal. When Kira's father is killed for refusing to sell, she inherits his place in the auction—and the titan's bones begin speaking directly to her, offering her a choice: sell them to the highest bidder, or use them to unmake the gods who killed it.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Kira Ossene
**Age:** 22
**Occupation / Background:** Bone merchant's daughter, raised in the Bazaar's undercity among spirits, charlatans, and immortals passing through; self-taught in bone-reading, streetwise and pragmatic
**Core skill:** Ostomancy—she hears the dead through their remains, conversing with echoes of who they were; with the titan's bones, this extends to memories of creation itself
**Fatal flaw:** She craves power she's never had; the titan offers it, and she wants to believe she deserves it
**Character arc:** Begins as a survivor seeking revenge; discovers that the power she's offered comes with truths she can't ignore; ends choosing justice over vengeance and walking away from godhood
**Voice and register:** Quick, defensive, with the patter of a marketplace dealer. She's always assessing value, even when talking about emotions. Her internal voice is lonelier, hungrier than she'd ever admit aloud.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Collector—an ancient entity who brokers impossible deals; and The Thirteen, the gods who murdered the titan and now want its bones destroyed
**Goal:** The Collector wants chaos, preferring a universe where mortals and gods are at war; The Thirteen want to erase all evidence of their crime
**Method:** The Collector manipulates the auction; The Thirteen deploy divine agents and offer Kira immortality if she surrenders the bones quietly
**Connection to protagonist:** The titan reveals that Kira's bloodline was created by it, millennia ago, as a contingency—she is literally the key it made for its own potential resurrection

## Supporting Characters

**Thrace the Unbound** — A fallen god stripped of divinity, attending the auction to destroy the bones and earn his way back; becomes an unlikely ally when he recognizes what Kira is
**Marrowen** — A demon bidder who genuinely likes Kira and offers protection in exchange for partnership; her deals are fair but her nature is hungry
**Ossander** — Kira's father's ghost, lingering in his own skull; he guides her but his memory is fragmenting, and his final secret might save or doom her

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Immortal Bazaar, a dimensional marketplace where mortals, gods, demons, and stranger things trade; and its undercity, where the desperate live
**Time period:** Outside normal time—entities from all eras of existence mingle here; the Bazaar has always existed
**Atmosphere:** Narrow streets lit by stolen starlight, stalls selling concepts and souls, tea houses where deals reshape reality. The undercity is darker, wetter, louder—bones rattling in every niche, the dead never quite silent. It smells of incense, old blood, and possibility.
**Key locations:** The Ossene Shop (home and business), the Auction Hall of Eternal Contracts, the Temple of the Thirteen (their seat of power), the Titan's Tomb (where the murder happened), the Heart of the Bazaar (where reality is most thin)

## Central Conflict
Kira must survive an auction where every bidder would kill her to possess the bones—while deciding whether to use the titan's offer of power to take revenge on the gods, knowing that resurrection would mean her own transformation into something inhuman.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The truth of the gods' crime is revealed, the balance of power shifts, and Kira determines what "justice" looks like—possibly destroying divine authority forever
**If the protagonist fails:** The bones are destroyed or misused, Kira dies, her father's ghost dissipates, and the Thirteen continue ruling unchallenged
**Personal stakes:** The titan offers Kira what she's always wanted—power, respect, vengeance—but the cost is her humanity, her father's memory, and everything she knows about herself

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Kira's ordinary trade, the arrival of the titan bones, her father's murder
- Ch 2 — The Pull: She inherits the auction seat; the titan speaks; factions begin circling
- Ch 3 — First Contact: An assassination attempt fails; she realizes she can use the bones' power; Thrace appears with a warning

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: The auction's first phase; Kira plays factions against each other; her father's ghost reveals partial truths
- Ch 5 — The Push: Alliance with Thrace and Marrowen; she discovers her bloodline's origin; the titan offers a full bargain
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The Thirteen intervene directly, destroying half the auction hall; Marrowen is injured; Ossander's ghost begins to fragment
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Kira touches the titan's core, sees the full history—the gods' betrayal, the creation of her line, what resurrection would truly mean
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: She refuses the titan's full offer but uses partial power to survive; prepares for final confrontation

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The final auction round, all factions present, Kira manipulating the outcome; confrontation with The Thirteen's avatar
- Ch 10 — Resolution: She chooses neither to resurrect the titan nor destroy it—she scatters the bones across the Bazaar, giving every faction a piece, ensuring no one wins; the power disperses; she walks away human, but changed

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The power of knowing secrets
**Deep theme:** Revenge is not justice, and power given freely always has hidden costs
**Thematic question:** When offered the power to right ancient wrongs, who decides what "right" means?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Shop Destruction**: Kira's home burning as assassins close in, her father's bones clutched in her hands, the first manifestation of the titan's power blasting them away
2. **The Auction Floor**: A vast hall where gods sit beside demons, mortals sweat beside spirits, and Kira must outbid them all with nothing but wit and secrets
3. **The Scattering**: The climax, Kira surrounded by enemies, choosing to break the titan's bones into a thousand pieces, each flying to a different corner of the Bazaar as she walks through the chaos

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** John Wick meets Spirited Away
**Book comparables:** The House in the Cerulean Sea by TJ Klune meets Gideon the Ninth by Tamsyn Muir

## Tone and Pacing Notes
This novel moves between tense negotiation scenes and explosive violence. The Bazaar should feel overwhelming—too much to process—while Kira's thread through it is clear. Pacing accelerates through the auction phases. The prose should be vivid and strange, finding beauty in macabre imagery.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Kira's voice is quick and wary, softening when she speaks to the dead. Thrace speaks with weary nobility. Marrowen is warm but with predatory undertones. The titan's voice is vast, ancient, almost painful to hear. Ossander is fading throughout, becoming harder to understand. Names should be crisp: "Ossene" (oh-SEEN), "Marrowen" (MAHR-oh-wen). Auction scenes need rapid-fire delivery.
