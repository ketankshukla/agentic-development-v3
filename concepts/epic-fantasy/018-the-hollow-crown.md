# The Hollow Crown

## Genre
Epic Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A court jester who secretly controls the kingdom discovers his puppet-king has been poisoned—and the only suspects are the fools he's been playing against each other.

## Expanded Premise
For fifteen years, Oisín the Fool has been the secret power behind the throne of Ériu. King Cormac is a drunk who signs whatever Oisín suggests, while Oisín plays the nobility like a chess board, keeping the realm stable through manipulation and misdirection. Then someone poisons Cormac. As the king slowly dies, Oisín must find the assassin among the nobles he's been deceiving—any of whom might be the killer, and all of whom have reason to hate him once the truth emerges. Worse, without Cormac, Oisín has no legitimacy; if the king dies before he finds the killer, the realm falls to civil war and Oisín becomes the most wanted man in the kingdom.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Oisín mac Faolán (known as "The Fool" or "Bells")
**Age:** 46
**Occupation / Background:** Court jester to the King of Ériu for twenty years; born a peasant with a gift for voices, recruited by the previous spymaster, took over when the old man died
**Core skill:** Mimicry and manipulation—he can imitate any voice, read any room, and play people against each other with surgical precision; he also has near-perfect recall of every secret in court
**Fatal flaw:** He has grown to love the game more than the realm; he cannot trust anyone because he knows everyone's worst secrets
**Character arc:** Begins believing he is the only one who can save the kingdom; learns that his secrecy created the conditions for this crisis; ends by finally trusting someone else, sharing power rather than hoarding it
**Voice and register:** Theatrical, shifting personas constantly, only honest in his own head. His internal voice is tired, cynical, and occasionally surprised by his own remaining idealism.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The poisoner is one of the five Great Nobles, but determining which is the mystery; the true enemy is the system Oisín created—a court where no one trusts anyone because trust has been weaponized
**Goal:** The poisoner wants to claim the throne and free the realm from Cormac's incompetent (or Oisín's hidden) rule
**Method:** Slow-acting poison that gives the poisoner time to position for succession; misdirection that makes every noble look guilty
**Connection to protagonist:** Each of the five nobles has been manipulated by Oisín; each has reason to hate him; the poisoner chose this moment because they discovered what Oisín truly is

## Supporting Characters

**Lady Síomha** — The Queen, Cormac's wife, smarter than anyone credits; she knows Oisín is more than a fool but not how much more; potential ally or enemy
**Lord Brennan** — The Marshal, loyal to the crown if not the king; Oisín's chief rival for influence; he despises the Fool but respects the kingdom
**Nessa** — A servant girl who sees everything and says nothing; Oisín recruited her years ago; she may be his only genuine friend

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Kingdom of Ériu, an island realm inspired by medieval Ireland with High Kings, competing nobles, and a culture of poetry and politics
**Time period:** A secondary-world equivalent of 11th century, with feudal lords, monastery learning, and deeply personal honor systems
**Atmosphere:** Stone castles shrouded in mist, great halls lit by firelight, the smell of peat smoke and whiskey. Politics is poetry; insults are art; murder is considered too crude for anyone with subtlety—which is why the poisoning is such a scandal.
**Key locations:** The Great Hall at Temair (seat of the High King), the Five Lords' Towers (each noble's stronghold within the court complex), the Fool's Quarters (hidden passage network), the Queen's Garden (secret meeting place), the King's Sickroom

## Central Conflict
Oisín must solve a murder mystery while maintaining his cover as a simpleton, navigating nobles who may all be killers, protecting the dying king, and ultimately deciding whether to reveal himself—knowing it will destroy everything he's built.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The poisoner is found, a succession crisis is averted (perhaps with Oisín finally revealing himself to manage it), and the kingdom survives
**If the protagonist fails:** Cormac dies without justice, civil war consumes Ériu, and Oisín is likely executed as the manipulator who failed to protect his king
**Personal stakes:** Oisín must decide if his legacy is the kingdom he secretly saved or the secrets he kept from everyone—including whether he can love someone he's been manipulating

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: The court through Oisín's eyes, his careful manipulations, the discovery of the poisoning
- Ch 2 — The Pull: He begins investigating, wearing his jester's mask, each noble a suspect; Cormac worsens
- Ch 3 — First Contact: First false lead; someone knows what Oisín really is; he receives a warning—solve this or be exposed

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: He investigates each noble, finding motives everywhere; Lady Síomha reveals she knows more than he thought
- Ch 5 — The Push: Evidence points to Lord Brennan; Oisín nearly moves to arrest—but something doesn't fit
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Brennan is framed; the real poisoner strikes again, killing a key witness; Oisín realizes he's being played
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Cormac has days left; the nobles unite against the "incompetent" Fool who hasn't found the killer; Oisín's network of informants is compromised
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Nessa helps him see the truth—the poisoner knows how Oisín thinks because they've been watching him for years; it's someone close

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The revelation, the confrontation, done publicly where Oisín has to reveal himself to prove his case; the truth of what he is shocks the court
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The poisoner is exposed; Cormac survives (barely) with antidote obtained; Oisín must navigate new reality where everyone knows; he makes alliance with Síomha, sharing power properly this time

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The loneliness of power behind the throne
**Deep theme:** Manipulation corrupts the manipulator—you cannot deceive everyone and remain whole
**Thematic question:** If you save a kingdom through lies, have you saved it—or poisoned it differently?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Court Performance**: Oisín juggling and jesting while mentally tracking six different noble conversations, his inner monologue revealing his true analysis
2. **The Confrontation with Síomha**: Two intelligent people finally speaking honestly, sparring verbally, testing whether trust is possible
3. **The Public Revelation**: Oisín dropping his mask in the Great Hall, becoming the schemer for all to see, proving murder while destroying his cover

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Favourite meets Murder on the Orient Express
**Book comparables:** The Lions of Al-Rassan by Guy Gavriel Kay, A Memory of Light by Brandon Sanderson

## Tone and Pacing Notes
This is a mystery wrapped in a political thriller. Pacing should be methodical in investigation, tense in confrontation. The prose should sparkle with Irish wit—humor masking danger. Every chapter should reveal something while deepening the mystery.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Oisín's voice shifts constantly—different personas for different audiences, only his inner voice consistent. He should use light Irish accent. Lady Síomha is cool and measured. Lord Brennan is gruff. Irish-inspired names: "Oisín" (oh-SHEEN), "Ériu" (AY-roo), "Síomha" (SHEE-va). Court scenes need verbal agility; emotional moments need grounding.
