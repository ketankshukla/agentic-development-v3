# The Drowned Kingdom

## Genre
Epic Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A pearl diver discovers her people's sunken homeland is rising from the sea—and the empire that conquered them a thousand years ago has sent an armada to ensure it stays buried.

## Expanded Premise
Moana Tane dives for pearls in the coral lagoons of the scattered islands, the last remnants of her people. According to legend, their ancestors ruled a vast kingdom before the sea swallowed it in divine punishment. Now strange currents have begun pulling toward a fixed point in the empty ocean. Moana follows them and discovers the truth: the kingdom is rising. Palaces of coral and gold breach the surface. Ancient magic awakens. And the Varengian Empire—built on the ruins of what they stole when the kingdom drowned—sends its navy to destroy the evidence of their original crime. Moana must race to claim her people's birthright before the empire buries it again, but the kingdom isn't empty. Something has been living in it for a thousand years.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Moana Tane
**Age:** 23
**Occupation / Background:** Pearl diver and fisher, daughter of the last traditional navigator who keeps the old star-paths; she was raised on stories of the lost kingdom but never believed them
**Core skill:** Divine breath—she can hold her breath for inhuman lengths of time and swim in pressure that would kill others; this proves to be the remnant of her ancestors' sea-magic
**Fatal flaw:** She has internalized her people's shame; she doesn't believe they deserve to reclaim their kingdom
**Character arc:** Begins ashamed of her people's small existence; discovers they fell not from sin but betrayal; ends claiming her heritage without the shame, leading her people home
**Voice and register:** Fluid, direct, sometimes sardonic. She speaks like someone used to working with her hands and body, not her words. Her internal voice is more poetic, carrying the rhythms of the chants she heard as a child.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Admiral Varvara Kron of the Varengian Empire—and The Keeper, the creature dwelling in the drowned kingdom
**Goal:** The Admiral wants to destroy the rising kingdom before it reveals that the Varengians caused the drowning; the Keeper wants worshippers to replace the people who abandoned it
**Method:** Kron deploys an armada of warships; the Keeper offers power to anyone who will pledge to it, trying to seduce Moana
**Connection to protagonist:** Kron's family were the ones who triggered the cataclysm a thousand years ago, stealing the kingdom's sea-controlling artifact; the Keeper was once the kingdom's god, maddened by centuries of isolation

## Supporting Characters

**Tua Tane** — Moana's father, keeper of the old stories; he begins dying once the kingdom rises, his life somehow tied to its slumber
**Commander Yannick** — A Varengian officer who defects when he learns the truth of his empire's founding crime; becomes Moana's reluctant ally and guide to enemy tactics
**Grandmother Wave** — The ghost of the last queen, preserved in the rising palace; she can teach Moana to use her heritage but her memory is fragmented and sometimes hostile

## Setting

**Primary location:** The scattered Coral Islands (Polynesian-inspired) and the Rising Kingdom (a drowned civilization of impossible beauty)
**Time period:** A secondary-world equivalent of 17th century, with island navigation culture and colonial empire fleets
**Atmosphere:** Turquoise waters, white sand, palm shade—and beneath, palaces of pearl and coral, streets where fish swim, treasures of a lost age. The empire's presence is dark ships and cannon smoke, gunpowder and greed. The contrast between island peace and imperial violence is constant.
**Key locations:** Moana's home island of Motunui, the Rising Spire (first structure to surface), the Throne of Tides (seat of ancient power), the Admiral's flagship Subjugator, the Keeper's Grotto (where the creature waits)

## Central Conflict
Moana must dive into her people's past, master powers she never knew she had, unite the scattered island clans, and claim the kingdom before either the empire destroys it or the Keeper corrupts it—all while racing against the empire's approaching armada.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The kingdom rises fully, the islands are united, the empire's crime is exposed, and the Keeper is either redeemed or destroyed
**If the protagonist fails:** The kingdom sinks again (or is bombed to rubble), her people remain scattered and conquered, and the empire's false history continues
**Personal stakes:** Her father's life is tied to the kingdom's fate; if she fails to claim it properly, he dies; if she claims it wrongly (through the Keeper), she becomes something inhuman

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Moana's ordinary life; the currents change; she follows them to the Rising Spire
- Ch 2 — The Pull: She enters the drowned kingdom, meets Grandmother Wave, and learns her birthright; her father collapses as the kingdom stirs
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Empire scouts arrive; Moana witnesses their violence; she realizes they intend destruction, not exploration

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Moana rallies the island clans, learns to use her breath-power as actual magic; Commander Yannick defects
- Ch 5 — The Push: The clans unite; they occupy the risen portions of the kingdom; Moana finds the Throne of Tides and begins unlocking its power
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The Admiral's armada arrives; first battle goes badly; the Keeper offers Moana full power if she'll become its vessel
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Moana's father dies; she nearly accepts the Keeper's offer in grief; Grandmother Wave reveals the Keeper was once good but has been corrupted
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Moana realizes she must neither reject the Keeper nor submit—she must heal it; she prepares a plan that risks everything

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The Battle of the Rising Kingdom—island canoes against empire ships, Moana diving to the Keeper's Grotto while battle rages above, the confrontation and healing
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The Keeper is redeemed, lending its power to drive off the armada; the kingdom fully rises; Moana takes the Throne not as conqueror but as healer; her people come home

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Reclaiming stolen heritage
**Deep theme:** Home is not a place—it is the people who remember who you were and help you become who you should be
**Thematic question:** When your ancestors' homeland rises from the sea, do you owe it loyalty—and what do you do with the anger of centuries?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The First Dive**: Moana swimming down through fathoms of water into the drowned palace, light filtering through coral windows, fish swimming through throne rooms
2. **The Battle of the Currents**: Island outrigger canoes against empire warships, Moana calling currents to capsize ships while canoes weave through cannon fire
3. **The Keeper's Grotto**: Deep underwater, the confrontation with a creature of madness and pain, Moana offering healing instead of combat

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Moana (Disney) meets Aquaman
**Book comparables:** Daughter of the Moon Goddess by Sue Lynn Tan, She Who Became the Sun by Shelley Parker-Chan

## Tone and Pacing Notes
This novel should feel oceanic—moments of calm alternating with surges of action. The underwater sequences need different pacing than surface scenes, slower and more dreamlike. The empire's presence brings urgency and violence. The prose should be lush when describing the sea, sharp when depicting conflict.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Moana's voice is warm and direct, with lyrical moments during underwater scenes. Tua speaks with the cadence of a storyteller. Admiral Kron is cold and clipped. The Keeper's voice is distorted, layered, sometimes plaintive. Polynesian-influenced names: "Moana" (moh-AH-na), "Tane" (TAH-nay), "Motunui" (MOH-too-NOO-ee). Underwater scenes need slowed, measured delivery.
