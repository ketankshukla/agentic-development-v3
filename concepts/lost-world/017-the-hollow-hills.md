# The Hollow Hills

## Genre
Lost World

## One-Line Premise
An Irish folklorist dismissed as a crackpot discovers a network of caves beneath the Burren where the Tuatha Dé Danann survived—not as gods but as refugees, nursing a millennia-old grudge against humanity.

## Expanded Premise
Dr. Siobhan Brennan has spent thirty years documenting fairy folklore while her colleagues mock her for believing the stories contain kernels of truth. When construction work for a coastal wind farm inadvertently opens a sealed passage beneath County Clare's Burren limestone, she's brought in to assess its archaeological significance. What she finds is Tir Tairngire—the Land of Promise—carved into the earth itself. The beings who live there are no gods, but they are old: descendants of a pre-Celtic civilization driven underground by wave after wave of invaders. They've watched Ireland change above them for three thousand years, nurturing resentment into ideology. And now that the passage is open, some of them want to reclaim what was taken.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Dr. Siobhan Brennan
**Age:** 58
**Occupation / Background:** Folklorist and ethnographer at University College Dublin, specializing in Irish fairy mythology. Born in Galway, raised on her grandmother's stories. Never married, entirely devoted to her work.
**Core skill:** Encyclopedic knowledge of Irish folklore, fluent in Old and Modern Irish, with a lifetime of practice sorting truth from embellishment in oral tradition.
**Fatal flaw:** Her vindication-seeking makes her reckless—she's spent so long being dismissed that she'll do anything to prove she was right, even if it endangers others.
**Character arc:** Begins desperate for academic redemption. Discovers that being right isn't worth what her discovery might cost. Ends choosing protection over proof.
**Voice and register:** Academic formality layered over West of Ireland warmth. She lectures by instinct, quotes poetry without thinking, and curses in Irish when frustrated.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Lord Bres and the Reclamation faction
**Goal:** Bres believes the time has come for the Tuatha Dé to reclaim Ireland's surface. Three thousand years of watching invaders was enough—the Celts, the Vikings, the Normans, the English. Now they're weak. Now is the time.
**Method:** Bres has been preparing for centuries: mapping surface infrastructure, identifying vulnerabilities, stockpiling weapons forged from iron that doesn't burn them (they've adapted).
**Connection to protagonist:** Bres sees Siobhan as both useful and dangerous—her knowledge of folklore means she understands them, which makes her a potential ally or a critical threat.

## Supporting Characters

**Queen Brigid** — The ruler of Tir Tairngire, ancient and weary, who believes coexistence is possible but struggles to control Bres's faction.
**Cormac** — A young Tuatha Dé who has never seen the surface, assigned to guard Siobhan, increasingly curious about the world above.
**Dr. Padraig Flynn** — Siobhan's former student, now a construction consultant, who accidentally opened the passage and feels responsible for what follows.

## Setting

**Primary location:** Tir Tairngire, an underground realm carved into the limestone caves beneath the Burren and extending deep beneath County Clare, Ireland.
**Time period:** Present day
**Atmosphere:** The underground world is beautiful in an alien way—bioluminescent fungi casting silver-green light, carved halls that echo with music older than written language, gardens of pale flowers that have never seen the sun.
**Key locations:** The Opened Gate (the breach in the construction site), The Hall of Names (where every invasion of Ireland is recorded), The Forges of Adaptation (where the Tuatha Dé learned to handle iron), The Pool of Seeing (surveillance point for monitoring the surface), The Queen's Court (where Brigid holds council).

## Central Conflict
Siobhan must prevent Bres's invasion while also protecting the Tuatha Dé from the modern world's response—because once humanity knows they exist, the response will be military.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The breach is sealed, the Tuatha Dé remain hidden, and Siobhan carries proof she can never share—vindication she can never claim.
**If the protagonist fails:** Either Bres invades and humanity responds with overwhelming force, or Siobhan's evidence triggers that response preemptively. Either way, a three-thousand-year civilization is destroyed.
**Personal stakes:** Siobhan has spent her life proving the stories were true. Now she must choose between her life's work and the survival of the people who inspired those stories.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Siobhan is called to County Clare to assess what the construction crew found. She descends into the passage, knowing she's about to prove everything.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: She finds Tir Tairngire—and is immediately captured by Tuatha Dé sentries. Her scholarly knowledge saves her life; she can speak their language.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Brought before Queen Brigid, she learns the truth: the Tuatha Dé are not gods but survivors. Bres demands she be killed; Brigid demands time.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Siobhan is given to Cormac as a charge. She explores Tir Tairngire, seeing three thousand years of parallel civilization—and three thousand years of resentment.
- Ch 5 — The Push: In the Hall of Names, she finds records that rewrite Irish history. The folklore she's studied her whole life is propaganda—stories the Tuatha Dé wanted told.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Bres stages a coup, imprisoning Brigid and seizing control. He accelerates invasion preparations. Siobhan realizes she's the only one who can warn the surface.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Siobhan escapes with Cormac's help, but the breach is now guarded by Bres's forces. She's trapped underground with a war about to begin.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Brigid, freed by loyalists, offers Siobhan a choice: help her stop Bres, and Siobhan will be returned to the surface with memory intact but no evidence. Or help Bres, and claim the vindication she's wanted for thirty years.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Siobhan and Brigid's forces confront Bres at the Opened Gate as his invasion begins. Siobhan must use her knowledge of the surface to help stop him—modern technology versus ancient magic.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Bres is defeated. The breach is sealed. Siobhan returns to the surface, her evidence destroyed, her memory intact. She publishes nothing—but she knows. And that, finally, is enough.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Irish mythology and hidden histories
**Deep theme:** Vindication is not worth destroying what you love
**Thematic question:** What do we owe to truths that would harm more than they heal?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The First Descent**: Siobhan entering the passage, her torch illuminating carved walls older than any Celtic art, the passage opening into impossible vastness.
2. **The Hall of Names**: Every invasion of Ireland recorded in stone, from the Milesians to the English, Siobhan finding her own ancestors named among the conquerors.
3. **The Gate Battle**: Tuatha Dé forces clashing at the breach, Siobhan caught between, modern construction equipment becoming weapons.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Pan's Labyrinth (2006), The Secret of Kells (2009)
**Book comparables:** The Tain translated by Thomas Kinsella, The Buried Giant by Kazuo Ishiguro

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Opens with the wistful melancholy of Irish academia—a woman mocked for believing. The descent shifts to wonder and dread. The underground sections balance beauty with political tension. The climax is violent but grounded in character—Siobhan choosing silence over glory.
