# The Idol

## Genre
supernatural-thriller

## One-Line Premise
A K-pop star whose career ended in scandal discovers that the entertainment company that made her is literally feeding on young performers — harvesting their years to keep a being older than Korea alive.

## Expanded Premise
Min Soo-yeon was the lead singer of NOVA until a manufactured scandal destroyed her career overnight. Now 28 and working as a vocal coach for trainees, she notices something wrong: the company's most successful idols are aging in reverse, while trainees keep having "accidents" that leave them decades older than their years. When she investigates, she uncovers Daehan Entertainment's true business model. The company was founded in 1952 by something wearing a human face — a kumiho, a nine-tailed fox demon — that has been harvesting youth from performers ever since. Soo-yeon's scandal wasn't an accident. She got too close. Now she must expose the truth before her replacement takes the stage.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Min Soo-yeon
**Age:** 28
**Occupation / Background:** Former K-pop idol, now vocal coach at a smaller agency. Spent seven years at the top before scandal brought her down. Lives in a small apartment in Seoul, avoiding the spotlight.
**Core skill:** Industry knowledge — Soo-yeon knows how entertainment companies work, where the bodies are buried, and how to navigate a system designed to chew people up.
**Fatal flaw:** Bitterness. She's convinced herself she doesn't care about the industry anymore, but she's really just wounded. This blinds her to allies who could help.
**Character arc:** Believes she was thrown away unfairly and wants justice only for herself. Discovers that thousands have been sacrificed, and justice requires protecting those still trapped in the system.
**Voice and register:** Sharp and observant, with the practiced presentation of someone trained to perform. Interior voice reveals someone exhausted by the constant performance of being okay.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Kim Dae-hong — CEO of Daehan Entertainment, appearing as a distinguished man in his sixties. In reality, the kumiho that founded the company seventy years ago, wearing human form, feeding on the youth of performers to sustain itself.
**Goal:** To continue indefinitely. The kumiho needs youth to survive; the entertainment industry provides an endless supply of willing victims who blame themselves when they "age out."
**Method:** It contracts with ambitious families, promising their children stardom. The contracts include clauses no one reads carefully — clauses that grant the company certain "energies." Failed trainees are drained openly; successful idols are drained slowly, their youth extracted over years.
**Connection to protagonist:** Soo-yeon was too strong-willed to drain easily. Her scandal was manufactured to remove her before she noticed the pattern. But she didn't leave the industry entirely, and now she's back.

## Supporting Characters

**Park Ji-hoon** — A current Daehan trainee, 17, talented and naive. Soo-yeon sees herself in him and tries to warn him.

**Choi Min-jun** — Former Daehan manager, now homeless and seemingly decades older than his forty years. He remembers everything the company did to him.

**Detective Kwon Eun-bi** — Seoul police detective investigating a trainee's death that was ruled suicide. She suspects foul play but not supernatural involvement.

## Setting

**Primary location:** Seoul, South Korea — the K-pop industry's heart
**Time period:** Present day, winter — comeback season, when new idols are launched
**Atmosphere:** The neon-lit surfaces of Seoul's entertainment district, glittering and artificial. Practice rooms where trainees work until they collapse. The contrast between glamorous stages and the exhausted faces behind them. A world where youth is currency and aging is failure.
**Key locations:** Daehan Entertainment's headquarters (a sleek tower hiding something ancient); practice rooms and dorms; Soo-yeon's small apartment; the rooftop where trainees go to cry; Kim Dae-hong's private floor, decorated in a style that predates the company by centuries.

## Central Conflict
Soo-yeon must expose Kim Dae-hong's true nature and free the current trainees before the next showcase — where successful debut means permanent binding to the company, and failure means accelerated drainage. But she has no proof that would be believed, no allies with power, and the company owns every platform that could spread her message.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The kumiho is destroyed or driven out, Daehan Entertainment collapses, and hundreds of young performers are freed from exploitative contracts.
**If the protagonist fails:** The kumiho continues indefinitely, the industry's dark practices spread, and Soo-yeon herself becomes the next victim — aged into unrecognizability.
**Personal stakes:** Soo-yeon's replacement, a seventeen-year-old named Yuna, will debut next week. If Soo-yeon doesn't act, the girl who took her place will pay the price.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Soo-yeon coaches trainees at a small agency, avoiding anything connected to Daehan. A former colleague visits — a woman who debuted at nineteen and now looks fifty. She says: "They're doing something. I saw it."
- Ch 2 — The Pull: The colleague is found dead — officially suicide. Soo-yeon attends the funeral and sees Kim Dae-hong paying respects. He recognizes her. She recognizes what's wrong with his face: it's exactly the same as seven years ago. Exactly.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Soo-yeon investigates the colleague's claims and finds Choi Min-jun, the former manager. He tells her the truth — about contracts, about draining, about the CEO's real nature. He's been drained so much he can barely speak.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Soo-yeon connects with Detective Kwon, sharing evidence without the supernatural elements. The detective investigates financial records showing impossible longevity in Daehan's leadership.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Park Ji-hoon, the trainee Soo-yeon warned, finds her. He's seen something: a late-night ritual in the CEO's office. He has partial phone footage. They begin planning exposure.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The kumiho discovers their investigation. Ji-hoon is "reassigned" — disappeared into an intensive training program. Detective Kwon is taken off the case. Soo-yeon receives a message: stop, or age.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Soo-yeon begins aging. Her hair greys; her joints ache. The kumiho is draining her remotely, using the contract she never fully exited. She has days before she's too weak to fight.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Choi Min-jun, dying, tells her the kumiho's weakness: its true form must be witnessed. If enough people see what it really is, it cannot maintain human disguise. The showcase is livestreamed to millions. That's their moment.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Soo-yeon infiltrates the showcase — now aged to apparent fifty, unrecognizable. She finds Ji-hoon, frees him, and together they reach the stage. Live on air, she confronts Kim Dae-hong, using talismans from a shaman Choi Min-jun trusted. The CEO's form wavers. The audience sees something wrong.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The kumiho flees, its human form unsustainable once witnessed. Daehan Entertainment collapses under investigation. Soo-yeon doesn't regain her youth — the years taken are gone — but she accepts it. She opens a small agency focused on protecting young performers, and the industry begins to change.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** A supernatural predator hiding within the K-pop industry.
**Deep theme:** Industries built on consuming youth will always find ways to consume more literally.
**Thematic question:** What sacrifices should never be demanded of the young, no matter the reward?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Colleague's Face**: Soo-yeon confronting her former friend, seeing the wrinkles and grey hair on a woman who should be twenty-eight, understanding that this wasn't stress — this was theft.
2. **The Ritual Footage**: Ji-hoon's phone recording, shaky and terrible, showing Kim Dae-hong standing before a bound trainee, his face peeling back to reveal something ancient underneath.
3. **The Showcase Confrontation**: Live on air, millions watching, Soo-yeon stepping onto the stage aged and broken, speaking truth as the CEO's form unravels and the audience begins to scream.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Handmaiden meets Black Swan
**Book comparables:** The Vegetarian by Han Kang; Mexican Gothic by Silvia Moreno-Garcia

## Tone and Pacing Notes
This book moves like a K-pop career — frantic work, manufactured crises, the pressure of constant exposure. Early chapters have the sleekness of the industry; later chapters descend into body horror as the supernatural becomes visible. The climax is public and performative — everything in this world happens in front of an audience.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Korean names: Soo-yeon (SOO-yawn), Ji-hoon (JEE-hoon), Dae-hong (DAY-hong), Eun-bi (EUN-bee). Industry terminology (trainee, comeback, showcase) should be spoken naturally. The kumiho's voice, when it speaks without disguise, should have an ancient, inhuman quality. Hangul-language songs or phrases should be pronounced authentically.
