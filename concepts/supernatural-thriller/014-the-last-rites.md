# The Last Rites

## Genre
supernatural-thriller

## One-Line Premise
A Haitian-American funeral director in New Orleans discovers his family has been performing secret rituals on every body they prepare — rituals that keep the dead from being used as weapons by something that has been building an army in the bayou for three hundred years.

## Expanded Premise
Marcus Beaumont is the fourth generation of his family to run Beaumont Funeral Home in New Orleans' Tremé district. He learned the craft from his father, who died two years ago — but his father never explained the small ritual he performed over every body, the prayers in Haitian Kreyol that Marcus dismissed as superstition. Now bodies are going missing from cemeteries across the city, and an old woman arrives at his door demanding Marcus perform the rites his father taught him. Because something in the bayou is raising the dead, and only the Beaumont ritual can keep a body down. Marcus's father died before teaching him the words.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Marcus Beaumont
**Age:** 38
**Occupation / Background:** Fourth-generation funeral director, owner of Beaumont Funeral Home. Born in New Orleans, educated at mortuary college in Baton Rouge. Has spent his career bringing dignity to death without examining the rituals beneath.
**Core skill:** Body preparation — Marcus can make the dead look more peaceful than they did alive. He understands death's physical processes intimately.
**Fatal flaw:** Avoidance. He rejected his Haitian heritage to fit into American success, dismissing his father's beliefs as embarrassing old-country superstition.
**Character arc:** Believes death is the end — his job is cosmetic and emotional, nothing more. Discovers that his family has been soldiers in a war against something that sees death as a beginning, and he must fight a battle he never trained for.
**Voice and register:** Professional and calming, a voice trained to comfort the grieving. Interior reveals someone who has never processed his own grief, who buried his father without performing the rites that would keep him safe.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Baron Kalfu — a dark loa, master of crossroads and black magic, who has been building an army of the dead in the Louisiana bayous since the slave ships brought him here. Not a god but something that feeds on the interface between life and death.
**Goal:** To complete his army — enough dead to overwhelm the living and establish a kingdom where death serves life, not the other way around. He's close. He just needs the Beaumonts to stop interfering.
**Method:** He raises the dead as zombi — not movie monsters but shells animated by his will. He's spent three centuries building his numbers, waiting for the moment when there are no more Beaumonts to seal the dead against him.
**Connection to protagonist:** The Beaumont family has served as Baron Samedi's priests for generations, performing the Baron's rites to keep the dead peaceful. Marcus's father was the last to know the full ritual. Marcus is the last Beaumont.

## Supporting Characters

**Mambo Claudette Etienne** — Elderly Vodou priestess who trained Marcus's father. She knows the rites but cannot perform them — only a Beaumont can. She becomes Marcus's teacher in a desperate crash course.

**Detective Janelle Broussard** — NOPD detective investigating the cemetery desecrations. She's Creole, knows the stories, doesn't want to believe them.

**Isaac Beaumont** — Marcus's father, appearing in dreams and visions. The ritual he didn't teach in life, he tries to communicate in death — but Baron Kalfu is blocking the messages.

## Setting

**Primary location:** New Orleans, Louisiana — Tremé district, cemeteries, and the surrounding bayous
**Time period:** Present day, October — the weeks leading to Halloween and All Saints' Day
**Atmosphere:** The heat and humidity that never quite leaves, jazz drifting from open doors, the unique smell of a city built on swamp. Above-ground tombs that look like small houses. The line between life and death thin everywhere, thinner in New Orleans than most.
**Key locations:** Beaumont Funeral Home (the basement holds secrets); St. Louis Cemetery No. 1; Mambo Claudette's temple in the Lower Ninth; the deep bayou where Baron Kalfu's army gathers; a crossroads outside the city where the final confrontation occurs.

## Central Conflict
Marcus must learn the complete Beaumont ritual before All Saints' Day, when the veil between worlds thins and Baron Kalfu plans to unleash his army. But his father is dead, the ritual was oral, and Baron Kalfu is hunting anyone who might teach Marcus. He must find fragments of the knowledge scattered across the community and assemble them in time.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Baron Kalfu's army is dispersed, the dead rest peacefully, and Marcus takes his place in a tradition that protects the city.
**If the protagonist fails:** An army of the dead rises on All Saints' Day, overwhelming New Orleans and spreading across the Gulf Coast.
**Personal stakes:** Marcus's father's body was never given the rites. If he fails, his father becomes one of Kalfu's soldiers — and Marcus will have to face him.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Marcus prepares a body for burial, performing truncated rites he doesn't understand. That night, the body digs itself out of the grave. Cemetery security footage shows a shadow leading it into the bayou.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: More bodies vanish. Detective Broussard investigates, connecting the missing dead to Beaumont Funeral Home's clients. Marcus realizes — only bodies he prepared without full rites are taken.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Mambo Claudette arrives, ancient and terrified. She tells Marcus the truth: his family has been at war for three hundred years. His father was killed by Baron Kalfu's servants. And Marcus is the last.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Marcus begins learning from Mambo Claudette, but she only knows parts of the Beaumont ritual. His father kept secrets even from her. Marcus must search his father's papers, his father's contacts.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Marcus finds partial instructions hidden in his father's funeral records — words, gestures, substances. He reconstructs enough to attempt a protective ritual on a new body. It works.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Baron Kalfu strikes directly. Mambo Claudette is killed, her knowledge incomplete. Detective Broussard witnesses the attack — a dead man walking, controlled by something in the shadows — and becomes a believer.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Marcus hides at the funeral home, surrounded by the dead he cannot protect. His father appears in a dream, trying to speak, but Kalfu's interference scrambles the message. Marcus has pieces but not the whole.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Marcus realizes the final piece is in his father's grave. Isaac Beaumont was buried with a notebook — the complete ritual. Marcus must dig up his father and face what Kalfu has done to him.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Marcus and Broussard travel to the crossroads where the army gathers. There, Marcus performs the rites on the entire assembly — a massive working that exhausts him. Baron Kalfu manifests, but Marcus speaks the words that bind him, words his father waited beyond death to deliver.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Baron Kalfu is not destroyed but contained — sent back to the crossroads, his army dispersed. Marcus buries his father properly at last, speaking the rites that let him rest. He reopens the funeral home, now fully understanding what he does. Every death he touches, he protects.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** A funeral director discovers his family's secret war against the dead.
**Deep theme:** We cannot protect our community by rejecting our heritage — we must embrace what we inherit.
**Thematic question:** What do we owe the traditions we inherit, especially those we don't understand?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Risen Body**: Security footage showing a fresh grave erupting, a body climbing out, and a shadow figure leading it away — all while the graveyard remains silent.
2. **Father's Grave**: Marcus digging up his father's coffin at night, opening it to find his father's corpse intact but with eyes that open and a mouth that speaks the words he needed to hear.
3. **The Crossroads Confrontation**: Marcus standing at the intersection of two bayou roads, an army of the dead surrounding him, speaking the binding words as Baron Kalfu manifests in fire and shadow.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Serpent and the Rainbow meets Angel Heart
**Book comparables:** A Lesson Before Dying by Ernest J. Gaines; The Good House by Tananarive Due

## Tone and Pacing Notes
This book moves to New Orleans rhythms — moments of languid tension punctuated by sudden supernatural violence. The funeral home scenes are quiet and dignified; the supernatural confrontations are visceral and terrifying. Dialogue should capture Creole and Haitian influences without becoming parody. The climax is ritual rather than combat — words have power.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Haitian Kreyol and New Orleans Creole terms throughout: mambo (mahm-BOH), loa (LOH-ah), Baron Kalfu (bah-ROHN kal-FOO), zombi (ZOHM-bee, not zombie). Marcus speaks standard American English; Mambo Claudette speaks with Haitian accent; dialogue includes New Orleans vernacular. The Baron's voice, when he speaks, should be deep and resonant with a hint of French colonial inflection.
