# The Hound's Bargain

## Genre
mythological-action

## One-Line Premise
An exiled druidess must resurrect the legendary hero Cú Chulainn before the Morrigan's army of the dead claims all of Ireland.

## Expanded Premise
Aoife was cast out from her grove for practicing forbidden resurrection magic. When the goddess Morrigan tears the veil between worlds and the ancient dead begin rising across Ireland, the druids who condemned her come begging. Only Aoife knows the ritual to summon Cú Chulainn, the Hound of Ulster, from his grave. But the hero's spirit demands a price: he will fight, but when the battle ends, Aoife must take his place in the lands of the dead forever.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Aoife Ní Dálaigh
**Age:** 34
**Occupation / Background:** Exiled druidess and death-talker; daughter of a chieftain who chose magic over her birthright
**Core skill:** Spirit-binding—she can anchor souls to physical forms, speak with the dead, and temporarily walk between the worlds of living and dead
**Fatal flaw:** Obsessive attachment to those she loves; her forbidden magic began as an attempt to save her dying daughter
**Character arc:** Believes death is an enemy to be conquered; learns that death is a threshold, not a wall; accepts that letting go is the final act of love
**Voice and register:** Quiet authority that carries the weight of grief. Speaks in rhythms that echo the old poetry, observing the world through the lens of what lies beneath and beyond it.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Morrigan, Goddess of War and Death
**Goal:** To end the cycle of mortal suffering by ending mortality itself; she believes forcing all souls into her realm is mercy
**Method:** Raising the legendary dead of Ireland as her generals, tearing holes in reality to allow her realm to flood into the living world
**Connection to protagonist:** The Morrigan took Aoife's daughter's soul as payment for Aoife's first forbidden ritual; their war is deeply personal

## Supporting Characters

**Cú Chulainn** — The Hound of Ulster, legendary hero pulled from honored death; exhausted by being summoned again, but honor demands he fight; becomes both ally and philosophical opponent
**Fergus mac Róich** — Another of the Morrigan's risen generals, but he fights against her control; provides inside knowledge of the goddess's forces
**Brother Colum** — A Christian monk who believes the old gods are demons; unlikely ally who provides sanctuary and challenges Aoife's assumptions

## Setting

**Primary location:** Ireland during the transition from paganism to Christianity—Ulster, Connacht, and the Otherworld
**Time period:** 560 CE, as the old ways fade and new faith rises
**Atmosphere:** Green hills shrouded in mist, standing stones that hum with dormant power, monasteries rising beside sacred groves. The air tastes of salt and rain, the nights are long and full of movement at the edge of vision, and the boundary between worlds is thin as breath.
**Key locations:** The standing stones of Tara; Cú Chulainn's grave at Knockbridge; the Morrigan's fortress in the Otherworld; the monastery at Clonmacnoise; the ford where the final battle occurs

## Central Conflict
Aoife must lead the resurrected Cú Chulainn across Ireland to close the three tears in reality before the Morrigan's forces become unstoppable. Each tear is guarded by one of Ireland's legendary dead, enslaved to the goddess. To close the tears, Aoife must release each enslaved soul—and each release weakens her ability to maintain Cú Chulainn's binding. She races to save Ireland while her only weapon slowly slips away.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The tears seal, the dead return to their rest, and Ireland survives to see both faiths coexist
**If the protagonist fails:** Ireland becomes an extension of the Morrigan's realm, a land of the eternal dead where no living thing endures
**Personal stakes:** Aoife can demand her daughter's return as part of victory—but only by condemning Cú Chulainn's soul to the goddess's service forever

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Dead Walk: Aoife witnesses the first tear opening; the dead rise; the druids who exiled her arrive begging for help
- Ch 2 — The Hound's Grave: Aoife performs the forbidden summoning; Cú Chulainn rises and names his price—her life for his service
- Ch 3 — The First Tear: They reach Tara where Ferdiad, Cú Chulainn's greatest enemy, guards the first tear; the old rivals fight while Aoife begins the sealing

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The March South: Moving through a transformed Ireland, gathering allies among both druids and monks who recognize the greater threat
- Ch 5 — The Second Tear: At Cruachan, Medb herself—the warrior queen—blocks their path; Fergus's betrayal of the Morrigan opens the way
- Ch 6 — The Goddess Speaks: The Morrigan confronts Aoife directly, offering her daughter's return in exchange for abandoning the quest; Aoife refuses but learns the truth about the third tear
- Ch 7 — The Binding Fails: Cú Chulainn begins fading as Aoife's power drains; Brother Colum's faith provides unexpected support; Aoife begins to understand death differently
- Ch 8 — The Final Choice: At her daughter's grave, Aoife must choose—use her remaining power to bring her daughter back, or keep Cú Chulainn bound for the final battle

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Ford: The final tear at the river ford where Cú Chulainn died long ago; the Morrigan manifests fully; the battle reshapes the landscape
- Ch 10 — The Letting Go: Victory comes when Aoife releases Cú Chulainn—and her daughter—willingly, making the choice that death refused to give her before

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The living must defeat the army of the dead
**Deep theme:** Grief is not love; holding on is not the same as caring
**Thematic question:** When does letting go become the greatest act of devotion?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Summoning**: In the rain at Knockbridge, the earth cracks open and the Hound of Ulster rises wreathed in battle-rage, his war-spasm transforming him into something barely human
2. **The Queen's Stand**: The undead Medb leads her chariot charge against the living; Fergus turns on his queen while ancient magic tears the sky
3. **The Ford of Stars**: The final battle where river and sky merge, Cú Chulainn fighting through his own legend while Aoife weaves spirit-magic against the manifested goddess

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** King Arthur: Legend of the Sword meets The Green Knight
**Book comparables:** The Táin translated by Thomas Kinsella; The Harp of Kings by Juliet Marillier

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Elegiac but propulsive. The prose has the rhythm of oral poetry—repeating epithets, triadic structures, moments of formal declamation—but the action is brutal and immediate. The pacing accelerates through each act, but pauses for moments of grief and beauty. Ireland itself is a character; the landscape should feel alive and ancient. The melancholy is earned but never suffocating.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Irish names require careful pronunciation: Aoife (EE-fah), Ní Dálaigh (nee DAHL-ee), Cú Chulainn (koo HULL-in), Morrigan (MORE-ih-gan), Cruachan (KROO-a-khan), Medb (MAYV). The narrator should indicate the hero's battle-transformation vocally. Brother Colum's Latin prayers provide contrast. The Morrigan's voice should be triple—maiden, mother, crone speaking in harmony. Battle sequences need weight; magical sequences need otherworldly pause.
