# The Fury's Daughter

## Genre
mythological-action

## One-Line Premise
A blind priestess of Apollo discovers she is the mortal daughter of a Fury, destined to hunt the god who murdered her mother.

## Expanded Premise
Clio has served in Apollo's temple on Delos since childhood, her blindness marking her as touched by the gods. When assassins destroy the temple and her dying mentor reveals the truth—Clio's mother was Alecto, eldest of the Furies, slain by Apollo himself—she inherits her mother's vengeance-sight. Now she can see the guilt that stains mortal souls, and the trail of divine murder leads to Olympus itself. But hunting a god means becoming monstrous, and Clio must decide if justice is worth losing her humanity.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Clio of Delos
**Age:** 24
**Occupation / Background:** Blind oracle-priestess who interprets Apollo's prophecies; orphan raised by the temple since infancy
**Core skill:** Vengeance-sight—she perceives guilt as visible wounds on the soul, allowing her to track murderers across any distance and sense lies as physical pain
**Fatal flaw:** Righteous fury that threatens to consume her identity; once she begins pursuing someone, she cannot stop
**Character arc:** Believes the gods are worthy of worship and her blindness is sacred; discovers the gods are capable of atrocity; becomes a force of justice that transcends divine hierarchy
**Voice and register:** Formal and precise from temple training, but increasingly raw as her fury awakens. Describes the world through sound, scent, and the texture of guilt she now perceives.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Apollo, God of Light and Truth
**Goal:** To erase all evidence of his crimes against the Furies during the primordial wars; he believes his actions were necessary to establish Olympian order
**Method:** Hunting down every being with Fury blood, corrupting oracles to prevent prophecy of his downfall, using mortal agents to maintain divine plausible deniability
**Connection to protagonist:** Apollo personally killed Alecto when she discovered he had murdered her sisters; he then hid Clio in his own temple to monitor her, never realizing she would inherit her mother's power

## Supporting Characters

**Theron of Argos** — A disgraced soldier of Athens cursed by the gods; offers to guide Clio across Greece in exchange for her help lifting his curse, becomes her protector and moral anchor
**Persephone, Queen of the Dead** — Sympathetic to the Furies who once served her husband; provides crucial information about Apollo's crimes and passage through the underworld
**Nestor the Witness** — An immortal satyr who saw Apollo's original murders; his testimony is required to bring formal accusation before the divine court

## Setting

**Primary location:** Ancient Greece—from the Cyclades to mainland Hellas to the slopes of Mount Olympus
**Time period:** 480 BCE, during the Persian Wars, when mortal attention is elsewhere
**Atmosphere:** Marble temples bake under Mediterranean sun while shadows hold whispers of older powers. The sea is wine-dark and hungry, the mountains hide caves that breathe, and every grove might shelter a god in disguise.
**Key locations:** The ruins of Apollo's temple on Delos; the Gates of Hades at Cape Taenarum; Delphi's corrupted oracle; the primordial cave where the Furies were born; the throne room of Olympus

## Central Conflict
Clio must gather evidence of Apollo's ancient murders and present them before the Olympian court—the only way to destroy a god without triggering divine war. But Apollo controls prophecy itself, meaning every oracle in Greece works against her. She must find the immortal witnesses to his crimes while evading his hunters, all while her awakening Fury nature threatens to transform her into an inhuman engine of vengeance.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Apollo faces divine judgment, the Furies are restored to their rightful place in cosmic order, and balance between justice and mercy is preserved
**If the protagonist fails:** Apollo erases all memory of the Furies, justice becomes whatever the powerful decree, and Clio dies as the last witness to the truth
**Personal stakes:** Clio must choose between completing her transformation into a full Fury—powerful but no longer human—or retaining her mortality and abandoning her mother's vengeance

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Temple Falls: Apollo's assassins destroy Delos; Clio's dying mentor reveals her true heritage as vengeance-sight awakens
- Ch 2 — The Cursed Soldier: Clio meets Theron, follows the trail of guilt across the Aegean, learns she must gather three witnesses to accuse a god
- Ch 3 — The Oracle's Lie: At Delphi, Clio discovers Apollo has corrupted his own oracle to hunt her; barely escapes with Nestor the satyr's location

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — The Satyr's Bargain: Clio finds Nestor hiding from divine wrath and must free him from a binding curse before he can testify
- Ch 5 — The Gates of Hades: Descent to the underworld where Persephone reveals the full scope of Apollo's crimes—he killed all three Furies
- Ch 6 — The Golden Arrows: Apollo himself confronts Clio at the Fury's birthplace cave; Theron dies shielding her, and she fully transforms in grief
- Ch 7 — The Monster's Face: Clio, now inhuman, terrorizes the countryside unable to control her vengeance-sight; sees herself becoming what she hunted
- Ch 8 — The Mortal Choice: Persephone offers a way to reclaim humanity, but only by surrendering most of her power; Clio chooses, preparing a different approach

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Olympian Court: Clio brings her accusation before Zeus; Apollo unleashes full divine power; Clio defeats him not through strength but by revealing his guilt to every god present
- Ch 10 — The New Balance: Apollo is bound to serve the restored Furies; Clio becomes their mortal voice in the world; Theron is returned from Hades as reward for his sacrifice

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Revenge against a god who committed murder
**Deep theme:** Justice requires restraint; vengeance without limit becomes tyranny
**Thematic question:** Can you pursue justice without becoming the evil you fight?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Delos Massacre**: Golden arrows rain from a cloudless sky as the temple burns, Clio's sight awakening as she holds her dying mentor amid shattered marble
2. **The Fury's Cave**: Deep beneath mountains where light has never reached, the birthplace of vengeance itself pulses with ancient power as Apollo and Clio clash between worlds
3. **The Olympian Accusation**: Before the assembled gods, Clio forces Apollo to see his own guilt reflected in their eyes as his facade of righteousness crumbles

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Clash of the Titans (2010) meets Kill Bill
**Book comparables:** Circe by Madeline Miller; The Silence of the Girls by Pat Barker

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Operatic tragedy played at action-movie speed. The prose should have the formal weight of epic poetry in the mythological sections but become urgent and visceral during combat. Clio's perception shifts from serene temple calm to violent sensory overload as her power grows. The middle act drags her through darkness before the cathartic confrontation. Emotional beats land between set pieces—this is a revenge story that questions revenge.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Clio's voice evolves from measured priestess to something with harmonics of fury—the narrator should indicate this shift. Greek names use standard English pronunciations: Delos (DEE-lohs), Alecto (ah-LEK-toh), Taenarum (TEE-nah-rum). Theron is gravelly and soldier-rough. Persephone should sound ancient and kind but with an edge of underworld chill. Divine voices need distinction—Apollo melodious and false, Zeus thunderous.
