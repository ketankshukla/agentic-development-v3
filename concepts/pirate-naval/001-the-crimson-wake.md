# The Crimson Wake

## Genre
pirate-naval

## One-Line Premise
An escaped slave steals a slaver's ship and builds a pirate fleet to burn the Caribbean slave trade to ash.

## Expanded Premise
Adaeze was a Dahomey warrior before chains brought her to Jamaica. When a hurricane wrecks the slave ship transporting her to auction, she leads a mutiny, captures the vessel, and rechristens it *The Crimson Wake*. Within two years, she commands a growing fleet of escaped slaves, indentured servants, and outcasts, systematically raiding slave ships and liberating their cargo. But the Royal African Company has dispatched its most ruthless captain to end her rebellion—a man who once commanded the very ship she now sails.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Adaeze Okonkwo
**Age:** 32
**Occupation / Background:** Former Dahomey warrior, captured in a rival kingdom's raid and sold to European slavers. Spent three years in bondage on a Jamaican sugar plantation before her transport to a new owner ended in shipwreck.
**Core skill:** Military tactics learned as a Dahomey warrior—she understands unit cohesion, ambush strategy, and how to forge disparate individuals into a disciplined fighting force.
**Fatal flaw:** Her hatred of slavers blinds her to necessary alliances. She cannot distinguish between pragmatic cooperation and moral compromise.
**Character arc:** Begins believing vengeance alone can heal her wounds. Discovers that building something—a free community, a family of outcasts—matters more than destroying her enemies. Ends having to choose between total victory and saving the people she's come to love.
**Voice and register:** Commands with quiet intensity rather than volume. Speaks multiple languages but uses silence as a weapon, letting others reveal themselves before she responds.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Captain Elias Whitmore, Royal African Company's chief enforcement officer
**Goal:** Destroy the pirate fleet to protect the Company's monopoly on the slave trade, and thereby secure his promised title and estate in England
**Method:** Patient, methodical pursuit. He turns captured pirates into informants, bribes port officials, and uses the Royal Navy as his personal hammer. He studies Adaeze's tactics and adapts.
**Connection to protagonist:** Whitmore commanded the slaver that brought Adaeze from Africa. He remembers her—she killed three of his men during her first attempted escape. This is personal for both of them.

## Supporting Characters

**Tomás Vega** — A Spanish deserter and master gunner who joins Adaeze after she liberates him from a prison hulk. Sardonic, haunted by his past actions, becomes her tactical advisor and reluctant conscience.

**Mercy Ashworth** — An English indentured servant who escaped a Barbados plantation. She handles the fleet's logistics, negotiates with smugglers, and builds the underground network that hides liberated slaves.

**Kofi** — A young boy Adaeze liberated from a slaver, now her cabin boy and surrogate son. His presence reminds her what she's fighting for—and what she has to lose.

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Caribbean Sea—Jamaica, Hispaniola, the Windward Passage, and the scattered cays of the Bahamas
**Time period:** 1715-1718, the Golden Age of Piracy
**Atmosphere:** Salt-crusted decks and the stench of gunpowder. Emerald islands ringed by shark-infested waters. Port Royal's rum-soaked taverns and moonlit coves where fugitives whisper of freedom. Heat that clings to the skin like a second shirt, broken only by the violence of sudden squalls.
**Key locations:** The hidden careening cove on Andros Island (fleet headquarters), Port Royal's black market docks, the Windward Passage hunting grounds, a fortified Company depot on the Mosquito Coast, the killing waters of the Crooked Island Passage

## Central Conflict
Adaeze must destroy the Royal African Company's Caribbean operations before Captain Whitmore's methodical pursuit traps her fleet against a superior naval force. Each raid liberates more enslaved people—but also draws more warships to the hunt. She must decide whether to keep fighting until her luck runs out or find a way to vanish with the community she's built.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The Royal African Company's Caribbean operations collapse, creating a window for hundreds of enslaved people to escape and establish a free settlement beyond colonial reach.
**If the protagonist fails:** Her fleet is destroyed, her people returned to bondage or hanged, and Whitmore's victory emboldens the slave trade for a generation.
**Personal stakes:** Kofi, Mercy, Tomás, and everyone who trusted her with their freedom. Her failure means their deaths or worse.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Adaeze labors on a Jamaican plantation, planning her third escape attempt—then learns she's being transported and sold to a new owner on Barbados.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: A hurricane devastates the slaver mid-voyage. Adaeze seizes the chaos, leads a mutiny, and takes the ship. She must decide: run for Spanish territory or strike back.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Her first raid on a slave ship succeeds, but a Company frigate nearly catches her. She loses a quarter of her crew and learns Whitmore has been assigned to hunt her.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Adaeze liberates Tomás and gains a gunner; intelligence reveals the Company's quarterly slave convoy—a target that could cripple their operations.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Three successful convoy raids. Her fleet grows to four ships. Liberated slaves flock to her banner. Whitmore seems always one step behind.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Whitmore springs his trap—he's been tracking her through a turned informant. Two ships burn, Mercy is captured, and Adaeze barely escapes with Kofi.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Down to one damaged ship, crew halved, hiding in a mangrove swamp. Tomás wants to flee to South America. Adaeze cannot abandon Mercy but has no means to save her.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Kofi's innocent question—"What would you teach me to do?"—breaks through her despair. She realizes her legacy isn't vengeance but the free community she's built. She devises a desperate rescue plan.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: A night assault on the fortified depot where Mercy awaits execution. Fire ships, infiltration, and a final confrontation with Whitmore aboard his flagship.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Whitmore dead, Mercy freed, the depot burning—but the Royal Navy is hours away. Adaeze leads her people not to another battle but to a hidden settlement on an uncharted cay, choosing to build rather than destroy.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Rebellion against slavery and empire
**Deep theme:** The difference between vengeance and liberation—destroying your enemies versus building something worth protecting
**Thematic question:** Can you fight a system built on dehumanization without becoming what you hate?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Hurricane Mutiny**: Adaeze leads the uprising below decks during the storm—hand-to-hand combat in the dark while the ship heaves, the hatch bursting open to reveal the slaver captain silhouetted by lightning.
2. **The Convoy Ambush**: Four pirate ships emerge from a fog bank to surround a slave convoy. Cannons roar, grappling hooks fly, and Adaeze herself leads the boarding action on the flagship.
3. **The Depot Inferno**: Fire ships crash into the harbor at midnight. Adaeze's team scales the walls while the garrison fights the flames. She faces Whitmore in the powder magazine, both knowing one spark ends them both.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *12 Years a Slave* meets *Master and Commander*; the visceral action of *Black Sails* with the moral weight of *Amistad*
**Book comparables:** *Black Leopard, Red Wolf* by Marlon James; *Washington Black* by Esi Edugyan

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Relentless forward momentum punctuated by moments of hard-won peace. The action sequences are brutal and chaotic—18th-century naval combat was hell—but the quiet scenes carry equal weight. This book moves like a shark: stop swimming, die. Adaeze's internal voice is spare and controlled, contrasting with the explosive violence around her.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Adaeze's name: ah-DAY-zay oh-KOHN-kwoh. The Dahomey references are historically grounded; pronounce them as spelled. Tomás uses Spanish-inflected English. Whitmore speaks in clipped, aristocratic tones. Sea battle scenes require careful pacing—chaos in the action, clarity in Adaeze's tactical observations. Kofi's voice should be youthful but not childish; he's seen too much.
