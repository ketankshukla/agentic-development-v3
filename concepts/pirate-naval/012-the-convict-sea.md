# The Convict Sea

## Genre
pirate-naval

## One-Line Premise
A transported Irish rebel leads a mutiny aboard a convict ship, turning prisoners into pirates and sailing into the unknown Pacific—with a sadistic captain hunting them.

## Expanded Premise
Siobhan Brennan was a United Irish rebel before the Crown transported her for sedition. Eight weeks into the voyage to Van Diemen's Land, she leads an uprising that takes the transport ship *Perseverance*. Now she commands two hundred former convicts—thieves, rebels, murderers, and the unjustly accused—with a stolen ship and nowhere to go. The British Navy controls every port. Australia means recapture. Home is forever closed. The only option is disappearing into the Pacific, finding an island beyond any empire's reach. But Captain Horace Blackwood of HMS *Retribution* doesn't just want them caught; he wants to make an example that will terrify future convicts into submission.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Siobhan Brennan
**Age:** 28
**Occupation / Background:** Former schoolteacher and United Irish organizer from County Wexford. She taught farmers to read; she also taught them to fight. The Crown considered her dangerous enough for transportation rather than hanging.
**Core skill:** Organization and persuasion—she can unify disparate groups around common purpose. She also learned navigation from her father (a coastal fisherman) before she learned revolution.
**Fatal flaw:** Her revolutionary idealism sometimes blinds her to practical reality. She believes in the goodness of people; some of her convicts are genuinely dangerous.
**Character arc:** Begins believing she can create a utopian community of the unjustly condemned. Discovers that freedom requires not just escape but governance, and that her people include wolves as well as sheep. Ends learning to lead imperfect humans toward something better than what they left.
**Voice and register:** Irish cadence beneath careful English, the voice of someone who learned to code-switch between cottage and courtroom. She speaks to inspire, and her voice carries.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Captain Horace Blackwood, Royal Navy, commanding HMS *Retribution*
**Goal:** Recapture the mutineers, publicly execute the ringleaders, and restore the terror that keeps the convict system functioning
**Method:** Patient, relentless pursuit. He follows every lead, tortures any captured convict for information, and never doubts his righteousness. To him, Siobhan isn't a person—she's an infection to be cauterized.
**Connection to protagonist:** Blackwood commanded the escort that suppressed Siobhan's village after the '98 rebellion. They've met—he doesn't remember; she'll never forget.

## Supporting Characters

**Thomas Crane** — A transported highwayman with genuine sailing experience. Pragmatic, amoral, dangerous—but skilled. Siobhan needs him; she doesn't trust him.

**Mary Flynn** — A young convict transported for theft, who becomes Siobhan's most devoted lieutenant. Her loyalty is absolute, but she's never killed anyone. That will change.

**Dr. Abernathy** — The transport ship's surgeon, who sided with the convicts during the mutiny. An abolitionist and secret radical who provides medical skill and educated counsel.

## Setting

**Primary location:** The southern seas—from the waters off New South Wales to the scattered islands of the Pacific, the coasts of New Zealand, and the vast emptiness between
**Time period:** 1802, during the early days of Australian colonization
**Atmosphere:** The crushing monotony of the open ocean broken by sudden violence. Unknown islands appearing from the mist, some paradise, some death. The constant presence of the sea trying to kill everyone equally. The stench of the ship and the hard-won clean air of freedom above deck.
**Key locations:** The convict deck of the *Perseverance*, Sydney harbor (glimpsed but never entered), a volcanic island where they careen the ship, the killing waters of the Tasman Sea, a Polynesian island that offers unexpected hospitality

## Central Conflict
Siobhan must keep her fractious community from destroying itself, find a place beyond any empire's reach, and evade Blackwood's pursuit—while confronting the reality that some of her "victims of injustice" are violent predators who threaten everyone.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Two hundred people find a place to live free, proof that another world is possible—a new society built by the empire's discards.
**If the protagonist fails:** Mass execution, transportation of survivors to hellish penal settlements, and the demonstration effect that Blackwood craves.
**Personal stakes:** Mary, Thomas, Dr. Abernathy—the people who've become her family. And her own conscience: can she justify leading people into the unknown when many will die?

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Below deck on the *Perseverance*, Siobhan organizes. The conditions are killing them slowly; what they're sailing toward is worse. She decides the only chance is mutiny.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: The uprising. Siobhan's plan unfolds in chaos—keys stolen, guards overwhelmed, the captain locked in his cabin. By dawn, the convicts control the ship. Now what?
- Ch 3 — First Contact: They can't land in Australia—that's recapture. They sail east into the Pacific. A storm nearly kills them; Siobhan proves her seamanship. But Blackwood's *Retribution* is already on their trail.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: The community fractures. Thomas leads a faction that wants to become proper pirates; Siobhan insists they must find land and build something. A fight breaks out that almost becomes a second mutiny.
- Ch 5 — The Push: They find an uninhabited island. Landing, fortifying, beginning to build. Hope grows. Dr. Abernathy helps establish medical care and sanitation. It feels like it might work.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Blackwood finds them. The *Retribution* bombards their settlement. They escape in the ship, but half their people are captured—including Mary.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Running on damaged ship with reduced crew. Thomas wants to abandon Mary; Siobhan refuses. Internal conflict threatens to tear them apart. Blackwood is close behind.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: They reach a Polynesian island where the chief offers sanctuary—for a price. Siobhan must negotiate, using everything she learned organizing back home. She secures alliance and a plan to rescue Mary.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: An ambush using outrigger canoes and the *Perseverance*. Siobhan's force boards the *Retribution* at night. She faces Blackwood in his cabin—and kills him, slowly understanding that mercy is not always kindness.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Mary rescued. The *Retribution* scuttled. The community settles on the Polynesian island, not as conquerors but as guests becoming family. Siobhan knows they'll never go home—but home was always just a word for where people treat you like a human being.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Escape from tyranny
**Deep theme:** Building community among broken people—freedom is not just escape but the creation of something worth living for
**Thematic question:** What do we owe the people who've suffered alongside us, and can justice be rebuilt from the wreckage of injustice?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Midnight Mutiny**: Below deck chaos. Prisoners fighting with chains and improvised weapons. The captain's marines overwhelmed by sheer numbers. Fire below deck, managed to keep the ship from burning.
2. **The Bombardment**: Blackwood's cannons destroying the settlement. Convicts running for the ship, some cut down. Siobhan the last aboard, pulling the wounded after her.
3. **The Final Boarding**: Outriggers alongside the *Retribution* in darkness. Siobhan leading the climbers over the rail. Hand-to-hand combat on the quarterdeck. Justice served with a pistol.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *Mutiny on the Bounty* meets *The Shawshank Redemption*; the escape narrative of *Papillon* with the community-building of *The Village*
**Book comparables:** *The Secret River* by Kate Grenville; *True History of the Kelly Gang* by Peter Carey

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Claustrophobic below deck, vast and terrifying once free. The convict ship sequences are dark and tense; the Pacific sequences alternate between beauty and danger. Siobhan's voice carries Irish defiance even when she's terrified. The violence is earned—every death matters. The ending is bittersweet: victory, but not the victory anyone expected.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Irish names use standard anglicized pronunciation: Siobhan (shih-VAWN), Brennan (BREN-an). Maintain subtle Irish cadence in Siobhan's speech—particular word choices and phrasing rather than phonetic spelling. Blackwood speaks with clipped aristocratic precision. The convict characters have varied accents—Irish, English regional, one or two Australian-born.
