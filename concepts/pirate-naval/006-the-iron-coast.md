# The Iron Coast

## Genre
pirate-naval

## One-Line Premise
A Swedish privateer must hunt down his own mutinous crew before they deliver stolen naval secrets to the Russian tsar—secrets that include proof of his noble family's treason.

## Expanded Premise
Erik Lindqvist commanded the *Stormvinge* under a royal letter of marque, raiding Russian merchantmen in service to King Karl XII. Then his first mate led a mutiny, stole coded documents from a captured Russian courier, and sailed east—to sell Swedish naval codes to Peter the Great. The codes alone could doom the Swedish fleet. But the courier also carried evidence that Erik's father, Admiral Lindqvist, has been selling secrets to Russia for a decade. Erik must chase his former crew across the frozen Baltic, recover the documents, and decide whether to save his country or protect his family's name—knowing he cannot do both.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Erik Lindqvist
**Age:** 31
**Occupation / Background:** Swedish privateer captain from a distinguished naval family. Third generation to command warships for the crown. Trained at Karlskrona, blooded in a dozen Baltic actions.
**Core skill:** Ship handling in ice conditions—he can navigate the treacherous winter Baltic when other captains refuse to leave port. He knows every rock, shoal, and ice shelf from Stockholm to St. Petersburg.
**Fatal flaw:** Loyalty to his family name. He has spent his life trying to be worthy of his father's legacy, never questioning whether that legacy was worth defending.
**Character arc:** Begins believing family honor is inseparable from personal honor—what his father did reflects on him. Discovers that true honor lies in his own choices, not his bloodline. Ends building a new legacy on the ashes of the old.
**Voice and register:** Clipped, military, precise. He speaks like someone accustomed to giving orders that will be obeyed. Emotion bleeds through in what he doesn't say, in the pauses between commands.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Henrik Moller, former first mate of the *Stormvinge*, leader of the mutiny
**Goal:** Sell the stolen documents to Russia and retire wealthy in a land where Swedish law cannot reach him
**Method:** Ruthless pragmatism. He has no ideology—just a desire to escape the brutal life of Baltic privateering before it kills him. He will kill anyone between him and his retirement.
**Connection to protagonist:** Erik and Henrik sailed together for five years. Erik saved Henrik's life at Narva. The betrayal cuts to bone—and Henrik's knowledge of Erik's tactics makes him a dangerous enemy.

## Supporting Characters

**Lieutenant Sofia Berggren** — A naval intelligence officer assigned to Erik's pursuit. She knows about Admiral Lindqvist's treason before Erik does and watches to see if the son shares the father's corruption.

**Jorgen the Finn** — Erik's bosun, one of the few crewmen who didn't join the mutiny. Ancient, scarred, absolutely loyal—and possibly the best small-boat sailor on the Baltic.

**Tsarevich Alexei's Agent** — A Russian spymaster code-named "The Bishop" who waits in St. Petersburg to receive the documents. He will ensure Henrik never lives to enjoy his payment.

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Baltic Sea—from the Swedish archipelago to the Gulf of Finland, the frozen waters around Åland, and the approaches to St. Petersburg
**Time period:** 1714, during the Great Northern War
**Atmosphere:** Ice floes grinding against oak hulls. Pale winter light that barely distinguishes day from night. Freezing spray that coats rigging in crystal armor. Fortress islands with ancient stone walls. The creak of ships straining against frozen harbors. A sea that kills the careless in minutes.
**Key locations:** Stockholm's Skeppsholmen naval base, the treacherous channels of the Åland archipelago, the ice-locked harbor at Viborg, a smuggler's cove on the Estonian coast, the siege lines around St. Petersburg

## Central Conflict
Erik must catch the mutineers before they reach St. Petersburg while simultaneously uncovering the truth about his father's treason—and deciding whether to bury that truth or let it destroy his family.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The naval codes are recovered, the fleet is saved, and Erik can choose what to do with his father's secret.
**If the protagonist fails:** Sweden loses the war, thousands die, and Erik's family is destroyed anyway—this time publicly, shamefully.
**Personal stakes:** Erik must decide: protect his father or serve his country. Protecting his father makes him complicit. Exposing his father destroys his mother, his sisters, everything he knew as home.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Erik's crew mutinies at night, mid-voyage. He's left on a ice floe with Jorgen and two wounded men. He watches his ship sail east toward the enemy.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Rescued by a passing patrol, Erik reaches Stockholm and learns the true stakes—the stolen documents are catastrophic. Lieutenant Berggren joins him with a fast sloop and orders: recover or destroy at any cost.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Erik catches up with the *Stormvinge* near Åland, but Henrik has set an ambush in the ice channels. Erik's new command is crippled. They survive, barely.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Repairing at a Finnish harbor, Erik interrogates a captured mutineer and learns about the second document—the evidence against his father. His world cracks.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Berggren reveals she already knew. Stockholm sent her to watch Erik as much as to help him. Erik proves his loyalty by sharing intelligence that lets them track Henrik to an Estonian smuggler's cove.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: They attack the cove but Henrik escapes, burning his temporary ship and crossing the ice on foot with a small party. The documents are still in play—and now Erik must pursue on land.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Stranded on the frozen Estonian coast, his ship gone, most of his crew dead. Berggren wounded. Russian patrols everywhere. Erik finally reads his father's letters—the full scope of the treason.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Erik buries the evidence of his father's guilt in the ice and makes peace with the decision. He's not protecting his father—he's refusing to let Henrik profit from his father's crimes. He marshals his remaining people for a final pursuit.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: A running battle across the frozen Gulf of Finland. Sleds, skirmishing, the desperate race to reach Henrik before he reaches Russian lines. Erik faces Henrik on the ice—two men who know each other too well.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Henrik dead, the naval codes recovered, the evidence of his father's treason destroyed. Berggren knows what Erik did—and chooses not to report it. Erik returns to Stockholm a hero, carrying a secret that will shape the rest of his life.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Pursuit and revenge across a frozen sea
**Deep theme:** The weight of inherited identity—are we responsible for our fathers' sins, and can we build honor on a foundation of shame?
**Thematic question:** What do we owe the families that made us, and when does loyalty become complicity?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Ice Floe Survival**: Erik and Jorgen adrift on breaking ice, rigging a sail from frozen canvas, navigating between crushing floes toward a distant light that may be salvation or Russian guns.
2. **The Smuggler's Cove Assault**: Erik's ship bombards the hidden cove while landing parties fight through snow-drifted pines. Henrik's ship burns—but Henrik isn't aboard.
3. **The Frozen Gulf Duel**: Erik and Henrik, exhausted, wounded, facing each other on ice that groans beneath their feet. The fight is brutal and intimate, ending not with swords but with one man's hand around the other's throat.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** *The Revenant* meets *Master and Commander*; the frozen survival of *The Terror* with the pursuit narrative of *The Fugitive*
**Book comparables:** *Midwinter* by Matthew Sturges; *The Long Ships* by Frans G. Bengtsson (for Baltic maritime adventure)

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Cold seeps through every page. The environment is as much an antagonist as Henrik. Pacing is relentless—Erik cannot rest, cannot recover, must keep moving or die. The emotional core, the revelation about his father, hits at the exact moment Erik is most physically broken. Sparse prose, action described with military precision, emotional devastation delivered in silence.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Swedish names use standard pronunciation: Erik (AIR-ik), Lindqvist (LIND-kvist), Berggren (BERG-gren). Henrik Moller (HEN-rik MOHL-ler). The Swedish umlauts in Åland are acceptable—pronounce as AW-lahnd. Jorgen (YOR-gen). Russian terms should be spoken clearly. The cold should be audible in the descriptions—short, clipped sentences that feel frozen.
