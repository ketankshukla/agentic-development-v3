# Black Water

## Genre
Survival-Thriller

## One-Line Premise
When a witness protection bus crashes in the Everglades, a US Marshal must keep her protectee alive while evading both the assassins sent to finish the job and the swamp that's killing them by the hour.

## Expanded Premise
Deputy Marshal Vera Santos was transporting Dominic Garza to testify against the Gulf Cartel when their convoy was ambushed and the bus crashed into the Everglades. Now Vera and Dominic are alone in 40 miles of swamp with no communications, cartel sicarios hunting them, and an environment where everything — alligators, pythons, sawgrass, sinkholes — wants them dead. Dominic's testimony could dismantle a cartel. Vera's job is to deliver him alive. But the swamp doesn't care about justice, and neither do the killers closing in.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Deputy Marshal Vera Santos
**Age:** 36
**Occupation / Background:** Cuban-American US Marshal, daughter of a refugee who testified against drug runners, 10 years in witness protection
**Core skill:** Expert in witness security protocols; combat training; ability to improvise protection scenarios
**Fatal flaw:** Views protectees as assets, not people; emotional distance that prevents real connection
**Character arc:** Believes her job is to deliver bodies, not save people; learns that protecting someone means understanding them
**Voice and register:** By-the-book professional, military precision in speech. Cuban phrases emerge under stress. Gradual warmth.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Everglades — and El Cirujano ("The Surgeon"), cartel hitman known for making bodies impossible to identify
**Goal:** El Cirujano wants Dominic dead before he testifies; failure means his own death
**Method:** Small team of professional killers with swamp experience; willing to wait and track
**Connection to protagonist:** El Cirujano was the one who killed Vera's father's handler — the case is personal but Vera doesn't know it yet

## Supporting Characters

**Dominic Garza** — Former cartel accountant, soft and scared but knows cartel operations intimately; more depth than initial impression
**Agent Miguel Reyes** — Wounded fellow Marshal who survives the ambush but dies buying Vera time
**Old Man Osceola** — Elderly Seminole they encounter deep in the swamp; helps them despite his distrust of government

## Setting

**Primary location:** Everglades National Park, Florida — from the Tamiami Trail deep into the backcountry
**Time period:** Present day, wet season (June-November), over approximately 4 days
**Atmosphere:** Water everywhere but none of it safe to drink. Cypress trees draped with moss, blocking the sun. Sawgrass that cuts skin like razors. Alligators that float like logs until they move. The air so humid it's nearly liquid. Mosquitoes thick as smoke. At night, the eyes — hundreds of them — reflecting back in the beam of a flashlight.
**Key locations:** The crashed bus, the alligator nesting area, the abandoned fishing camp, the Seminole encampment, the ranger station

## Central Conflict
Vera must traverse 40 miles of deadly swamp with a man she dislikes, while professional killers track them and the environment strips away her every advantage.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Dominic testifies, the cartel is crippled, Vera's father's legacy is honored
**If the protagonist fails:** Dominic dies, the cartel operation continues, justice is denied for hundreds of victims
**Personal stakes:** Completing the mission her father died for — protecting those who testify against evil

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: The convoy; the ambush; the crash into the swamp; Agent Reyes's sacrifice
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Vera and Dominic alone; assessing the situation; beginning to move before the cartel regroups
- Ch 3 — First Contact: First encounter with El Cirujano's team; narrow escape deeper into the swamp

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: The swamp begins to take its toll — dehydration, cuts, fever; Vera learns who El Cirujano really is
- Ch 5 — The Push: They find the abandoned fishing camp; rest, hydrate, rearm from what's there
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: El Cirujano catches up; Dominic is wounded; they lose the supplies and barely escape
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Dominic delirious from infection; lost in the sawgrass; Vera questions if either of them will survive
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Old Man Osceola finds them; provides treatment and guidance; Vera opens up about her father

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Final confrontation near the ranger station; Vera faces El Cirujano; the swamp itself becomes a weapon
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Dominic testifies; cartel leadership arrested; Vera visits her father's grave for the first time in years

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Survival in the Everglades with killers hunting you
**Deep theme:** The difference between protecting people and treating them as cargo
**Thematic question:** What is a life worth — just its testimony, or something more?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Ambush**: RPG striking the convoy, bus rolling into the swamp, gunfire in the steam and chaos
2. **The Alligator Nest**: Passing through an alligator nesting area at night, knowing any wrong step triggers an attack
3. **The Sawgrass Trap**: Final fight in chest-deep water and sawgrass, El Cirujano and Vera circling each other

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Rundown (2003), Hanna (2011)
**Book comparables:** Swamplandia! by Karen Russell, Florida by Lauren Groff

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Humid, threatening, claustrophobic despite the open water. The swamp presses in constantly. Vera's professionalism cracks under pressure, revealing the person beneath. Dominic's transformation from liability to partner is gradual. The cartel pursuit provides human threat; the swamp provides environmental menace. Pacing alternates between trudging movement and explosive violence.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Spanish terms and Cuban pronunciation should be correct (Vera with Spanish 'v', El Cirujano, sicarios). The Everglades has its own vocabulary (hammock, slough, sawgrass). Dominic's fear should be audible in his speech patterns. Old Man Osceola speaks with measured wisdom. The swamp sounds — insects, birds, splashes — should be implied throughout. Night scenes need the sense of creatures watching.
