# Green Hell

## Genre
Survival-Thriller

## One-Line Premise
A disgraced trauma surgeon must lead five plane crash survivors through 200 miles of uncontacted Amazon territory while a cartel hunting party closes in.

## Expanded Premise
Dr. Marcus Chen was flying to São Paulo to face a malpractice tribunal when his charter plane goes down in the Javari Valley — one of Earth's last uncontacted indigenous territories. With the pilot dead and five other survivors depending on him, Marcus must use his medical skills to keep them alive while navigating terrain no outsider has mapped. But the plane was carrying evidence against a Brazilian narco-trafficking network, and a cartel cleanup crew is already tracking the wreckage. Marcus must choose between saving lives and outrunning killers in a jungle that wants them all dead.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Dr. Marcus Chen
**Age:** 42
**Occupation / Background:** American trauma surgeon, former Army combat medic, suspended pending malpractice investigation
**Core skill:** Emergency medicine under impossible conditions; ability to improvise surgical solutions from available materials
**Fatal flaw:** Paralyzing self-doubt after a patient death he believes was his fault; freezes at critical moments
**Character arc:** Believes he no longer deserves to hold lives in his hands; learns that perfection isn't the standard — showing up is
**Voice and register:** Calm under pressure but internally spiraling. Clinical language masks deep emotion. Dark humor as defense mechanism.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** "O Caçador" (The Hunter) — Renato Dias, cartel enforcer and former BOPE special forces operator
**Goal:** Recover the evidence (encrypted drives) from the crash and eliminate all witnesses before they reach civilization
**Method:** Small team of ex-military trackers using satellite phones, jungle expertise, and brutal efficiency
**Connection to protagonist:** Renato's brother died in Marcus's operating room — the malpractice case — making this hunt personal vengeance

## Supporting Characters

**Sofia Reyes** — Mexican journalist who was transporting the evidence; provides context on the cartel and crucial survival knowledge from covering jungle conflicts
**Gabriel Ferreira** — Brazilian environmental lawyer, badly injured in crash, becomes moral compass despite his condition
**Tomás and Elena Vega** — Argentinian honeymooners, initially useless but Elena reveals hidden competence as a former park ranger

## Setting

**Primary location:** Javari Valley, Brazil — deep Amazon rainforest along the Peru border
**Time period:** Present day, wet season (December-May)
**Atmosphere:** Suffocating humidity and perpetual green twilight beneath the canopy. The sound never stops: insects, birds, rain, things moving in the undergrowth. Everything rots, everything bites, everything grows over you if you stop moving. The jungle is indifferent in the most terrifying way possible.
**Key locations:** The crash site in a river gorge, abandoned rubber tapper camp, indigenous warning territory (carved markers), flooded forest (igapó), the Javari River

## Central Conflict
Marcus must keep five injured civilians alive across 200 miles of trackless jungle while evading professional killers, all while battling his own conviction that he's not fit to be responsible for human lives.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The evidence reaches authorities, the cartel network is exposed, and the survivors testify — dismantling a major trafficking operation
**If the protagonist fails:** Everyone dies in the jungle, the evidence is destroyed, and the cartel continues operations that fund violence across three countries
**Personal stakes:** Marcus's soul — whether he can forgive himself and practice medicine again, or surrender to despair

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Marcus on the flight, reflecting on his upcoming tribunal; the plane goes down in the jungle canopy
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Survivors regroup, Marcus performs emergency surgery on Gabriel; Sofia reveals what they were carrying and who will come for it
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Cartel scouts find the crash site within hours; Marcus leads a desperate night escape into the jungle

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: The group realizes they're being actively hunted; Marcus must treat infections, injuries, and psychological breakdown while moving
- Ch 5 — The Push: They find the rubber tapper camp and gain supplies, distance from pursuers, and a map fragment showing a river route
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Tomás is killed by Renato's team; Gabriel's condition worsens; the group enters indigenous warning territory and must choose between human hunters and unknown dangers
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Lost in flooded forest, Gabriel near death, Elena in shock, Marcus unable to save anyone — he sits in rising water considering surrender
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Sofia snaps him out of it with the truth about her own guilt; Marcus realizes forward is the only direction and operates on Gabriel with a knife and fishing line

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Final confrontation at the river; Marcus faces Renato, learns the connection to his patient, and must fight despite the guilt
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Three survivors reach a river patrol; evidence delivered; Marcus returns to medicine in a jungle clinic, having found purpose in the place that nearly killed him

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Survival in hostile wilderness against human predators
**Deep theme:** The weight of responsibility for others and the courage to keep trying after failure
**Thematic question:** When you've failed to save one life, do you still have the right to try to save others?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Canopy Crash**: The plane tears through trees in slow motion, passengers tumbling as the fuselage disintegrates
2. **The Night Crossing**: Swimming across a caiman-infested river at midnight, flashlight beams searching from both shores
3. **The Flooded Surgery**: Marcus performing intestinal surgery on Gabriel in waist-deep water as floodwaters rise around them

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Ruins (2008), Jungle (2017)
**Book comparables:** The River by Peter Heller, Annihilation by Jeff VanderMeer

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Oppressive, claustrophobic, relentless. The jungle should feel like a character — always pressing in, always dangerous. Short chapters, constant forward movement. Medical procedures described with visceral precision. Action sequences are chaotic and muddy, not choreographed. The humidity should feel palpable on every page.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Marcus's internal monologue should sound exhausted but controlled. Portuguese and Spanish words should be pronounced correctly (Javari, caçador, igapó). Indigenous territory should be described with respect, not exoticism. Sound design opportunities: constant background of jungle noise should be implied through descriptive passages. Renato's voice is calm and professional, making him more frightening.
