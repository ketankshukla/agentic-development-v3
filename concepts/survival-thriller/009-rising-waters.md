# Rising Waters

## Genre
Survival-Thriller

## One-Line Premise
When a catastrophic dam failure floods Bangkok, a trauma nurse must navigate submerged streets and collapsing buildings to reach her daughter's school before the second surge hits.

## Expanded Premise
Somchai "Som" Prasert is working the night shift when every phone in the ER starts screaming the same alert: the Bhumibol Dam has collapsed. Bangkok has two hours before 65 feet of water drowns the city. Som's daughter is in a school 15 miles across the city — and every route is flooding fast. With no vehicles, no communications, and water rising by the minute, Som must swim, climb, and fight through a drowning megacity while the second reservoir prepares to release. Sixteen million people are in the water with her, and not all of them are friendly.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Somchai "Som" Prasert
**Age:** 39
**Occupation / Background:** Thai trauma nurse, former competitive swimmer, single mother, 15 years in emergency medicine
**Core skill:** Triage under pressure; ability to assess and prioritize when everything is a crisis; exceptional swimming ability
**Fatal flaw:** Tunnel vision on her goal that blinds her to others' needs; willing to abandon strangers to reach her daughter
**Character arc:** Believes her only obligation is to her daughter; learns that saving others along the way is how she saves herself
**Voice and register:** Practical, rapid-fire assessment, maternal protectiveness underneath professional calm. Thinks in medical terms.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The flood — and looters exploiting the chaos, led by a gang boss named "Nok"
**Goal:** The flood has no goal; Nok wants to control remaining high ground and resources
**Method:** Water rising relentlessly; gangs controlling boats and dry ground
**Connection to protagonist:** Som's hospital treated Nok's brother after a police shooting; the brother died; Nok blames hospital staff

## Supporting Characters

**Officer Chai Rattanakorn** — Police officer trying to maintain order; provides protection and local knowledge
**Pitchaya "Pitch" Chaiwat** — Teenage boy Som rescues; knows the rooftop paths across the city
**Dr. Arun Suthep** — Elderly doctor Som carries from the hospital; provides medical supplies and moral grounding

## Setting

**Primary location:** Bangkok, Thailand — from the hospital district to the international school zone
**Time period:** Present day, over approximately 12 hours
**Atmosphere:** Brown water swallowing a modern city. Cars float like toys. Towers become islands. The silence between streets is broken by screams and helicopter rotors. The water tastes of sewage and diesel. Bodies float past. The sun rises on a drowned world.
**Key locations:** Siriraj Hospital, the Chao Phraya River (now everywhere), Victory Monument (an island of refugees), the shopping mall rooftop, Patana School

## Central Conflict
Som must cross 15 miles of flooded city in 12 hours, making impossible choices about who to save and who to leave behind, to reach her daughter before the second surge drowns everything.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Her daughter survives; the people she saved along the way live; the photos she took document the disaster
**If the protagonist fails:** Her daughter drowns in the school; everyone Som left behind dies knowing she abandoned them
**Personal stakes:** Being the mother she promised to be — and the person she can live with being

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Night shift at the hospital; the alert; the water beginning to rise
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Hospital floods; Som learns her daughter's school is cut off; she leaves against orders
- Ch 3 — First Contact: First miles through the flood; she rescues Pitch from a rooftop; learns the city's new geography

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Nok's gang controls the Victory Monument zone; Som trades medical skills for passage
- Ch 5 — The Push: Rooftop path across flooded district; they reach the mall; halfway there
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Dr. Arun dies helping them; second surge warning comes — they have 4 hours; Pitch is injured
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Trapped in the mall with water rising; Nok's men find them; Som faces choice between treating an injured gang member or fleeing
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: She treats the injured man; Nok lets them go with a boat; she pushes toward the school

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Second surge hits as she reaches the school; fighting through debris and currents; finding her daughter on the roof
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Helicopter rescue; Som's photos lead to better dam safety legislation; she adopts Pitch after his family is confirmed dead

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** A mother crossing a flooded city to save her daughter
**Deep theme:** The impossible ethics of triage — who we save when we cannot save everyone
**Thematic question:** What do we owe strangers when our own loved ones are in danger?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Hospital Evacuation**: Water breaching the ground floor, patients floating, Som carrying babies to upper floors before leaving
2. **The Rooftop Run**: Jumping between buildings on a path of debris, water churning below, Pitch leading the way
3. **The Second Surge**: Wall of water visible between towers, Som swimming against the current with her daughter on her back

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Impossible (2012), Hard Rain (1998)
**Book comparables:** The Water Knife by Paolo Bacigalupi, Into the Drowning Deep by Mira Grant

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Relentless forward movement with moral weight accumulating. The water never stops rising. Every choice has consequences that catch up later. The city itself is a character — familiar landmarks transformed into alien landscape. Moral calculus is constant: how much time spent saving others vs. reaching the daughter? Thai cultural elements woven throughout without exoticization.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Thai names should be pronounced correctly (Somchai, Prasert, Rattanakorn). Som's nickname is pronounced "Sohm." The chaos of flood scenes should be reflected in description density. Tender moments with her daughter in flashbacks contrast with present urgency. Water sounds — lapping, rushing, dripping — should be implied throughout. The rooftop running scenes need kinetic energy.
