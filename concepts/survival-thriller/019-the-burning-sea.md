# The Burning Sea

## Genre
Survival-Thriller

## One-Line Premise
When an oil tanker explodes in the Mediterranean, a Greek coast guard captain must rescue survivors from burning water while human traffickers use the chaos to eliminate witnesses to their operation.

## Expanded Premise
Captain Eleni Karas was running a routine patrol when the tanker Prometheus detonated 30 miles off Crete. Racing to the disaster, she finds survivors in water that's literally on fire — and refugees from a smuggler boat that was scheduled to pass unseen during the night. Now Eleni must pluck people from burning seas while the smugglers' mother ship closes in to kill everyone who could testify about their cargo. With her patrol boat damaged and Greek Navy support hours away, she must navigate between fire, water, and murder to save as many lives as possible.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Captain Eleni Karas
**Age:** 38
**Occupation / Background:** Greek Coast Guard captain, 15 years of service, rescued thousands of refugees during the migration crisis, haunted by those she couldn't save
**Core skill:** Small-boat handling in dangerous conditions; crisis command under pressure; intimate knowledge of Mediterranean currents and weather
**Fatal flaw:** Compulsive savior complex that puts her crew at risk; cannot accept that she cannot save everyone
**Character arc:** Believes every death is her personal failure; learns that trying to save everyone means saving no one
**Voice and register:** Naval authority with Greek warmth beneath. Gives orders expecting obedience. Softer with civilians. Prays in crisis.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The burning oil slick — and Captain Nikolaos Diamantis, Greek businessman running human trafficking through "legitimate" shipping
**Goal:** Diamantis needs all witnesses to the refugee boat eliminated; the burning slick makes perfect cover
**Method:** Armed crew on his vessel disguised as rescue; willingness to let people burn
**Connection to protagonist:** Diamantis was Eleni's superior before his discharge; he knows how she thinks

## Supporting Characters

**Lieutenant Stavros Christou** — Eleni's second in command, injured in the explosion, provides critical support despite wounds
**Amara Osei** — Ghanaian refugee, witness to the smugglers' operation, becomes Eleni's responsibility
**Petty Officer Maria Papadimitriou** — Young crew member who must step up when others fall

## Setting

**Primary location:** Mediterranean Sea, between Crete and Libya, at night
**Time period:** Present day, summer, over approximately 8 hours (one night)
**Atmosphere:** Black water lit by orange fire. Oil burning on the surface, flames three meters high. The stench of burning petroleum and worse. Screams cutting through the roar. Silhouettes of people thrashing in the water. The stars invisible behind smoke. Ships appearing from the darkness — you can't tell who is rescue and who is death.
**Key locations:** The explosion site, the refugee boat wreckage, the patrol boat, Diamantis's ship, the oil slick maze, the open sea beyond the fire

## Central Conflict
Eleni must rescue survivors from burning water while fighting off traffickers who are hunting the same people, with a damaged boat and injured crew.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Maximum survivors rescued; trafficking operation exposed; Amara's testimony breaks the network
**If the protagonist fails:** Witnesses murdered; trafficking continues; Eleni dies knowing she couldn't protect the people she found
**Personal stakes:** Every refugee she's lost over the years — this night is her chance to finally save enough

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Night patrol; the explosion on the horizon; racing toward the disaster
- Ch 2 — The Pull: First survivors in the water; discovering the refugee boat wreckage; realizing this is two disasters in one
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Diamantis's ship appears, claiming rescue mission; first shots fired; the truth becomes clear

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Combat while pulling people from the water; Stavros wounded; patrol boat damaged
- Ch 5 — The Push: Eleni finds a path through the oil slick; reaches a concentration of survivors; begins systematic rescue
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Diamantis blocks their escape; oil slick closes in around them; they're trapped between fire and guns
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Trapped, burning, ammunition low; Eleni prepares to sacrifice herself to hold off Diamantis
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Amara reveals crucial information about Diamantis's ship; Eleni sees a way to turn the fire against him

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Eleni drives through the burning oil; uses the fire itself as weapon; final confrontation with Diamantis
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Dawn rescue by Greek Navy; survivors counted; trafficking network dismantled; Eleni decorated but changed

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Rescue operation in burning waters
**Deep theme:** The weight of choosing who to save when you cannot save everyone
**Thematic question:** How many strangers' lives are worth your own?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Tanker Explosion**: The Prometheus erupting in the night, a mushroom cloud of fire, debris raining for miles
2. **The Fire Maze**: Navigating through gaps in the burning oil, flames on both sides, reaching toward survivors
3. **The Final Run**: Driving the patrol boat through solid fire to ram Diamantis's ship, the sea itself their weapon

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Finest Hours (2016), Deepwater Horizon (2016)
**Book comparables:** Fire at Sea by Marco Ferreri, Sea State by Tabitha Lasley

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Frantic, urgent, burning. The night setting keeps everything close and chaotic. Fire provides constant danger and illumination. The refugee crisis is handled with dignity — these are people, not statistics. Naval combat is close-quarters and desperate. Eleni's Greek Orthodox faith is present without being heavy-handed. The pace never lets up — this is one night of constant crisis.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Greek names should be pronounced correctly (Eleni, Karas, Christou, Diamantis). Amara speaks English with Ghanaian accent. Naval terminology (bow, stern, helm, bearing) should be clear. The fire and water sounds should be constant in description. Eleni's prayers in Greek (Kyrie eleison) provide moments of quiet. Radio chatter is fragmented and urgent. The dawn, when it comes, needs the relief to be audible.
