# The Green Corridor

## Genre
Survival-Thriller

## One-Line Premise
When military aid workers are captured by a rebel group in Myanmar, a former child soldier turned relief coordinator must lead them through 150 miles of jungle to Thailand while evading the army that raised her.

## Expanded Premise
Aye Myint Thein escaped the Karen rebel army at sixteen. Now thirty-two, she runs humanitarian operations for a Danish aid organization — work that returns her to the borders of the jungle she fled. When the military junta's forces raid a refugee camp and capture her team, Aye must become what she once was to survive. Leading six aid workers through guerrilla-contested jungle, she must navigate between junta forces, rebel groups, and her own traumatic past. Some of the rebels hunting her were her childhood friends. Some remember her as a deserter.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Aye Myint Thein
**Age:** 32
**Occupation / Background:** Myanmar-born humanitarian coordinator, former Karen child soldier, escaped at 16, educated in Denmark, returned to help
**Core skill:** Jungle warfare training from childhood; knowledge of rebel tactics and territory; ability to disappear in terrain
**Fatal flaw:** Suppressed violence that she fears will resurface; guilt about leaving her unit behind
**Character arc:** Believes she left that soldier behind; learns that the skills she hates are what saves people she loves
**Voice and register:** Professional, measured, carefully controlled. When the old training returns, she becomes clipped, tactical. Internal struggle constant.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Colonel Min Aung — junta military officer — and Captain Saw Kaw — Karen rebel leader who was Aye's childhood friend
**Goal:** Min Aung wants to capture foreign aid workers for propaganda; Saw Kaw wants Aye to pay for her desertion
**Method:** Min Aung uses military resources; Saw Kaw uses jungle knowledge and personal vendetta
**Connection to protagonist:** Aye was once part of Saw Kaw's unit; she escaped while he stayed; he sees her survival as betrayal

## Supporting Characters

**Dr. Anna Larsen** — Danish team leader, older, represents the person Aye wants to be
**Private Zaw Htun** — Young junta soldier who defects during an attack, becomes unlikely ally
**Naw Paw** — Karen woman they encounter, provides shelter and forces Aye to confront what she left behind

## Setting

**Primary location:** Karen State, Myanmar, from the Thai border region through jungle to Thailand
**Time period:** Present day, monsoon season, over approximately 2 weeks
**Atmosphere:** Triple-canopy jungle so dense the sun never reaches the floor. Rain that falls continuously for days. Leeches on every surface. Paths that disappear overnight. Villages hidden so well you can pass within fifty meters and never know. The sounds of war that never stopped — distant mortars, helicopters, gunfire. The jungle holds decades of conflict in its roots.
**Key locations:** The refugee camp, the junta base, the hidden Karen village, the mountain pass, the Thai border crossing

## Central Conflict
Aye must cross 150 miles of war zone using skills she swore never to use again, while evading a childhood friend who will never forgive her for leaving.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Six lives saved; her testimony brings international attention to the conflict; she reconciles her two identities
**If the protagonist fails:** Her team dies or is captured for propaganda; Saw Kaw's vendetta is satisfied; decades of suppressed trauma consume her
**Personal stakes:** Facing the person she was and integrating her with the person she became

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Aid work at the border; the raid; the capture; brief captivity
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Aye enables escape during a rebel attack; the old skills coming back; the team free but deep in the jungle
- Ch 3 — First Contact: First encounter with Saw Kaw's scouts; Aye recognized; the hunt begins

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Aye reveals her past to the team; Zaw Htun defects and joins them; three-way pursuit
- Ch 5 — The Push: They reach the hidden Karen village; Naw Paw provides shelter; Aye confronts her abandoned identity
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Village is attacked; Dr. Larsen is captured; Aye must choose between rescue and escape
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Rescue attempt fails; Dr. Larsen is taken by junta forces; Aye faces the child soldier she was
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Aye embraces her training fully; plans an assault on the junta convoy; team becomes her unit

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Ambush of the convoy; confrontation with both Saw Kaw and Colonel Min Aung; rescue succeeds
- Ch 10 — Resolution: They cross into Thailand; international response; Aye begins work to help other child soldiers reintegrate

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Escaping through guerrilla-contested jungle
**Deep theme:** The integration of trauma into identity — accepting the past without being consumed by it
**Thematic question:** Can you use the weapons of your trauma to save others without losing yourself?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Refugee Camp Raid**: Soldiers swarming the camp at dawn, chaos and gunfire, Aye's training taking over
2. **The Hidden Village**: Entering a Karen village invisible until you're inside it, encountering her people's resistance
3. **The Convoy Ambush**: Classic guerrilla tactics used against the military, Aye becoming the soldier she feared

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** First They Killed My Father (2017), Rambo (2008)
**Book comparables:** The Burma Effect by Michael E. Rose, A Thousand Splendid Suns by Khaled Hosseini

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Lush, green, threatening. The jungle is both prison and sanctuary. Aye's internal struggle — civilian vs. soldier — drives emotional tension. Danish aid workers provide contrast and moral perspective. The politics are present but not preachy. Action sequences are brutal and guerrilla-authentic. The pacing alternates between exhausting marches and explosive violence.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Burmese and Karen names should be pronounced correctly (Aye, Myint, Thein, Naw Paw, Saw Kaw). Danish names for the aid workers. The jungle sounds should be constant in description. Aye's voice shifts between identities — professional when controlled, terse when soldiering. Monsoon rain is ambient throughout. The horror of child soldier experience should be present but not exploitative.
