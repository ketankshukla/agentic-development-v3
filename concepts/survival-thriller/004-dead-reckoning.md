# Dead Reckoning

## Genre
Survival-Thriller

## One-Line Premise
When a cargo ship is hijacked and scuttled in the Indian Ocean, the only officer left alive must keep twelve trapped survivors breathing in an air pocket until rescue that may never come.

## Expanded Premise
Chief Officer Nadia Petrov was below decks when Somali pirates seized the MV Astoria. When the hijacking went wrong and the crew fought back, the pirates scuttled the ship and fled. Now the Astoria lies on the seafloor in 180 feet of water, and Nadia is trapped with twelve survivors in a slowly shrinking air pocket. With no way to signal for help, limited oxygen, injuries among the survivors, and the psychological pressure of darkness and certain death, Nadia must keep everyone alive long enough for a rescue that depends entirely on chance. Every breath brings them closer to suffocation.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Nadia Petrov
**Age:** 36
**Occupation / Background:** Ukrainian Chief Officer, 15 years in merchant marine, one of few women officers in cargo shipping
**Core skill:** Ship systems expertise and crisis management; knows the Astoria's layout and emergency systems better than anyone alive
**Fatal flaw:** Control obsession; cannot delegate or trust others to perform under pressure
**Character arc:** Believes she must handle everything herself; learns that leadership means empowering others, not controlling them
**Voice and register:** Authoritative, clipped, naval-formal. Softens only in private moments of doubt. Thinks in procedures and contingencies.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The ship itself — the flooding compartments, dwindling oxygen, and the mathematics of survival
**Goal:** Physics and time work against survival: air depletes, pressure builds, metal groans
**Method:** Slow suffocation, hypothermia from cold water, psychological collapse from darkness and hopelessness
**Connection to protagonist:** Nadia loved this ship; now it's killing everyone she's responsible for

## Supporting Characters

**Engineer Kwame Asante** — Ghanaian second engineer, badly injured but provides crucial technical knowledge about hull integrity
**Dr. Priya Sharma** — Ship's medical officer, Indian, manages injuries and psychological states while hiding her own claustrophobic terror
**Dmitri "Dima" Volkov** — Ukrainian AB seaman, young and panicking, becomes either the group's weakest link or unexpected hero

## Setting

**Primary location:** The sunken MV Astoria, 180 feet below the Indian Ocean surface, 200 miles off the Somali coast
**Time period:** Present day, over approximately 72 hours
**Atmosphere:** Total darkness except for emergency lights running on battery. Cold water seeping through bulkheads. The constant groan of metal under pressure. The air growing thicker, harder to breathe. Claustrophobic spaces shrinking as water rises. The absolute silence of the deep broken only by the ship dying around them.
**Key locations:** The air pocket in the forward hold, the flooded engine room, the tilted bridge (partially accessible), the emergency equipment locker

## Central Conflict
Twelve people have approximately 72 hours of air, assuming no physical exertion. Every action Nadia takes to signal for rescue or improve their situation costs oxygen. She must balance action against survival, hope against mathematics.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Survivors are rescued; Nadia's testimony exposes the piracy network and shipping company negligence
**If the protagonist fails:** All twelve die slowly in the dark; their bodies are never found; the piracy continues
**Personal stakes:** Nadia's promise to her dying mother to "come home" — and whether she can keep twelve other people's promises too

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Normal operations on the Astoria; the pirate attack; the scuttling as Nadia is trapped below
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Survivors find each other in darkness; Nadia takes command; they assess their situation and realize how dire it is
- Ch 3 — First Contact: First attempt to signal fails; water breaches a compartment; they lose two people to flooding

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Full inventory reveals they have less time than thought; injuries worsen; Nadia forms a plan to reach the emergency beacon
- Ch 5 — The Push: Nadia and Kwame dive through flooded sections to reach equipment; they activate a beacon and believe rescue is coming
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The beacon's battery dies after one transmission; a survivor panics and wastes oxygen; Dr. Sharma reveals they have 20 hours left
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Another compartment floods; they're crowded into final space; Dmitri breaks down; Nadia sits in the dark and records final messages
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Kwame suggests a desperate plan to release air bubbles on a schedule; Nadia agrees to try; they organize for one final effort

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The air bubble signal is spotted by a search plane; divers arrive; final rescue through flooded sections as the air pocket collapses
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Eight survive, including Nadia; she testifies against the shipping company; she returns to sea but now trains other officers in survival

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Survival in a sinking ship with dwindling air
**Deep theme:** Leadership under impossible conditions and the weight of being responsible for others' lives
**Thematic question:** When you cannot save everyone, how do you decide what to do?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Scuttling**: The ship rolling as explosions tear through the hull; Nadia tumbling through flooding compartments as lights fail
2. **The Dive Through**: Nadia swimming through pitch-black flooded corridors, feeling her way toward equipment that might save them
3. **The Final Rescue**: Divers cutting through hull as the last air pocket collapses, pulling survivors through water in total darkness

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Poseidon Adventure (1972), Kursk (2018)
**Book comparables:** Deep Storm by Lincoln Child, The Wreck of the Medusa by Jonathan Miles

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Claustrophobic and relentless, with the walls literally closing in. Time is constantly referenced — hours of air remaining, battery life, distance to surface. The darkness is a character. Chapters are short, often ending on new crises. Flashbacks to surface life provide breathing room and emotional stakes. The pace tightens as oxygen depletes.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
The underwater acoustics should be reflected in description — sounds distorted, muffled. Ukrainian and Ghanaian accents for Nadia and Kwame respectively. Breathing should be referenced often, with narrative rhythm slowing as oxygen depletes. Emergency announcements and ship sounds provide audio variety. Claustrophobic sections benefit from slightly faster, tighter delivery.
