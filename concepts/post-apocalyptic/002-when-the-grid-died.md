# When the Grid Died

## Genre
Post-Apocalyptic

## One-Line Premise
A blind radio operator becomes the only person who can locate survivors after an EMP destroys civilization, but the warlord hunting her frequency wants silence forever.

## Expanded Premise
Elena Vasquez lost her sight in childhood but built a life as a ham radio enthusiast in rural Montana. When a coordinated EMP attack collapses the electrical grid across North America, her battery-powered equipment and encyclopedic knowledge of radio frequencies make her the last point of contact for scattered survivors. As she guides refugees to safe zones using nothing but sound and memory, a militia commander named Holt realizes that whoever controls the frequencies controls the new world — and he's coming to either recruit or silence her. Elena must broadcast from constantly moving positions while leading a growing band of survivors she has never seen toward a rumored government refuge in Wyoming.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Elena Vasquez
**Age:** 32
**Occupation / Background:** Former FCC compliance officer and amateur radio championship competitor; blind since age 8 due to a car accident
**Core skill:** Echolocation and audio mapping — she navigates the world through sound, can identify approaching threats by footstep patterns, and operates radio equipment entirely by touch and memory
**Fatal flaw:** Isolation — she built her pre-collapse life around avoiding dependence on others, which makes her resist the very community she's creating
**Character arc:** Believes her blindness makes her a burden who must compensate with usefulness; discovers that her different way of perceiving the world is exactly what makes her irreplaceable; learns to accept help without shame
**Voice and register:** Precise and technical in her broadcasts, awkward and self-deprecating in personal conversation. Her internal monologue is rich with sound-textures — she experiences the world as a symphony of information.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Commander Silas Holt, leader of the Blackout Militia
**Goal:** Establish total territorial control over Montana and Wyoming by eliminating all competing communication networks, forcing survivors to depend solely on his organization
**Method:** Triangulates radio signals to locate and destroy transmitters, executes operators publicly as "hoarders of vital resources," and spreads propaganda that independent broadcasts attract government drone strikes
**Connection to protagonist:** Holt was once a legitimate National Guard officer who responded to Elena's first emergency broadcast; when she refused to join his command structure, he declared her a threat to public order

## Supporting Characters

**Marcus Chen** — A 16-year-old refugee who becomes Elena's "eyes," describing the visual world with increasing poetry; he lost his entire family and attaches to Elena as a surrogate
**Dr. Rebecca Frost** — A trauma surgeon traveling with a field hospital's worth of supplies; pragmatic, morally flexible, and essential to the group's survival
**Crow** — A deaf former Army Ranger who communicates with Elena through tactile sign language; he handles security and shares her experience of navigating a world that doesn't accommodate difference

## Setting

**Primary location:** Rural Montana, moving toward the Wind River Range refuge
**Time period:** Present day plus six weeks (collapse began late autumn)
**Atmosphere:** A world gone suddenly silent except for wind, animals, and human voices. Vehicles sit abandoned where they died mid-road. Cities are dark and dangerous, but rural areas have adapted faster. Snow is coming, adding environmental urgency. Without electronics, every journey is measured in daylight and foot-miles.
**Key locations:** Elena's cabin near Flathead Lake, the dead city of Missoula, the survivalist compound at Salmon River, Yellowstone's geothermal zone, the Wind River military bunker

## Central Conflict
Elena must guide an ever-growing convoy of survivors across 400 miles of militia-controlled territory using only radio signals, while evading a commander who can track her every broadcast — all before winter makes the mountain passes impassable.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Hundreds of survivors reach the Wind River refuge, a new communication network links isolated communities, and Holt's authoritarian movement collapses without its monopoly on information
**If the protagonist fails:** The survivors freeze or are captured, Holt controls the region's only communication infrastructure, and the post-collapse West becomes a feudal dictatorship
**Personal stakes:** Elena has finally found people who need her specific abilities — losing them would prove her deepest fear that she is, ultimately, helpless

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Elena broadcasts her nightly weather report to three listeners when the EMP hits; she hears transformers explode for miles and realizes everything has changed
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Marcus stumbles into her cabin, the sole survivor of his family's car crash; she agrees to help him reach Missoula, breaking her isolation
- Ch 3 — First Contact: In Missoula, they encounter Holt's militia executing a man for "unauthorized transmission"; Elena broadcasts a warning and makes herself a target

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Survivors following Elena's broadcasts begin converging on her position; she realizes she's created a community she never intended to lead
- Ch 5 — The Push: The convoy crosses the Salmon River with help from a survivalist compound; Elena negotiates safe passage by sharing her frequency codes
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Holt captures Dr. Frost and broadcasts a demand: Elena surrenders or he executes one prisoner per day
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Elena attempts a solo rescue, fails, and is wounded; Crow has to carry her back while Marcus broadcasts in her place
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Feverish from infection, Elena hears Marcus's amateur broadcast and realizes he's learned her techniques; she doesn't have to carry everything alone

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The convoy attacks Holt's compound from multiple angles, using coordinated radio signals to synchronize movements Elena directs entirely by sound
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Holt is killed by his own men when they realize the Wind River refuge is real; Elena broadcasts the first inter-community network, then rests her voice for the first time in weeks

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Communication is survival — whoever controls information controls the future
**Deep theme:** Disability is not inability; different ways of experiencing the world offer unique insights
**Thematic question:** Is leadership about giving orders or about helping people find their own voices?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Missoula Escape**: Elena navigates the dead city using echoes and Marcus's whispered descriptions while militia patrols search with flashlights, their footsteps betraying their positions.
2. **The Geothermal Crossing**: The convoy moves through Yellowstone's steam vents at night, using the natural heat to mask their thermal signatures while Elena guides them by the distinct sounds of different geysers.
3. **The Compound Assault**: A synchronized attack coordinated entirely through Morse code clicks, with Elena in a hilltop position hearing the battle unfold as overlapping radio transmissions and distant gunfire.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** A Quiet Place, Children of Men
**Book comparables:** One Second After by William R. Forstchen, The Dog Stars by Peter Heller

## Tone and Pacing Notes
The book alternates between tense movement chapters and quieter broadcast interludes where Elena connects with distant survivors. Her perspective creates unusual narrative texture — descriptions focus on sound, temperature, air pressure, and vibration rather than visual detail. Action sequences are disorienting by design, putting the reader in Elena's experience. The pace accelerates as winter closes in.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Elena's internal monologue should be reflective and detailed. Her broadcasts are crisp and professional, a distinct "radio voice." Marcus speaks quickly with teenage informality. Crow's presence is conveyed through tactile descriptions since he doesn't speak. Use pauses effectively during tense sequences where Elena is listening — the silence itself becomes narrative. Avoid over-emphasizing her blindness; it's present but normalized.
