# The Quarantine Line

## Genre
Post-Apocalyptic

## One-Line Premise
A Cuban doctor must cross the U.S. military's plague wall to retrieve her daughter before the infected zone is sterilized by fire.

## Expanded Premise
When the hemorrhagic plague erupted in Miami, the U.S. government drew a line across Florida and shot anyone who crossed it. Dr. Isabel Delgado was at a medical conference in Atlanta when the quarantine descended; her twelve-year-old daughter Sofia was with relatives in Havana visiting Miami for vacation. Now, three months later, the government has announced Operation Cleanse — a firebombing campaign to eliminate the infection zone. Isabel has seventy-two hours to cross the quarantine line, navigate plague-ravaged South Florida, find her daughter in a city of corpses, and escape before the bombs fall.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Dr. Isabel Delgado
**Age:** 44
**Occupation / Background:** Infectious disease specialist, former head of the Cuban national disease surveillance program who defected for research opportunities; now working at Emory University
**Core skill:** Disease expertise and biological threat assessment — she can evaluate exposure risk, create improvised protective equipment, and navigate plague zones with minimal contamination
**Fatal flaw:** Guilt — she's in Atlanta because she wanted her career more than she wanted to be with her daughter; every decision is filtered through that self-blame
**Character arc:** Believes she failed her daughter by prioritizing work; discovers that her skills are exactly what's needed to save Sofia; learns that being the right person in the right place isn't failure — it's preparation
**Voice and register:** Clinical precision mixed with maternal terror. She speaks both Spanish and English fluently, mixing them under stress. Her internal voice is disciplined but cracking.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Plague itself and General Marcus Rhodes, commander of the Southern Quarantine Zone
**Goal:** The plague has no goal — it spreads, mutates, kills. Rhodes's goal is clean containment; he believes sacrificing the quarantine zone is the only way to save the continent.
**Method:** Military cordon, shoot-on-sight orders, and countdown to firebombing; Rhodes is not evil, just terrible in his calculus
**Connection to protagonist:** Isabel's research was meant to create the vaccine that would make the quarantine unnecessary; she failed, and Rhodes knows it

## Supporting Characters

**Major Javier Santos** — A Marine officer who lost his family to the plague and now guards the quarantine line; he helps Isabel cross because he believes no child should die for strategy
**Tommy Chen** — A teenage survivor inside the zone, immune to the plague, who knows Miami's streets and guides Isabel in exchange for extraction
**Dr. Benjamin Hale** — Isabel's former colleague, now Rhodes's scientific advisor; he believes Isabel is already dead and is shocked when she calls from inside the zone

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Florida Quarantine Zone, from the Jacksonville Line to Miami
**Time period:** Three months after the outbreak
**Atmosphere:** A plague landscape. Bodies in cars, bodies in houses, bodies in streets. The living hide from the dead and from each other. The disease presents as hemorrhaging — the visual is red and terrible. Military drones circle. The approaching deadline is audible in distant bomber squadrons.
**Key locations:** The Jacksonville Quarantine Wall, the Orlando ruins (drone zone), the Everglades crossing, Miami's plague districts, the extraction point at Key Biscayne

## Central Conflict
Isabel must cover 400 miles through the most dangerous terrain in America, find one child among millions of corpses, and reach an extraction point before the bombers arrive — all while her disease expertise makes her aware of exactly how close to death she is every moment.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Sofia lives, Isabel's expertise proves evacuation was possible, and political pressure prevents future "cleanses"
**If the protagonist fails:** Sofia dies in fire, Isabel dies in fire, and the precedent for plague response becomes extermination rather than treatment
**Personal stakes:** Isabel has spent her career fighting disease; crossing the quarantine line means potentially bringing the plague back with her — even if she saves Sofia, she might become the vector that kills millions more

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Isabel watches the Operation Cleanse announcement in her Atlanta apartment; she has seventy-two hours; she begins planning
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Contact with Major Santos, who lost his wife and son to the plague; he agrees to create a gap in the line
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Crossing into the quarantine zone, Isabel immediately encounters survivors who have gone feral; Tommy saves her life and demands extraction as payment

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: The Orlando drone zone — an automated kill box around the theme parks; Isabel and Tommy navigate through the tunnels beneath the Magic Kingdom
- Ch 5 — The Push: Reaching Miami ahead of schedule; Isabel makes contact with Sofia's relatives' neighbors; Sofia is alive, hiding three miles away
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The neighborhood where Sofia is hiding is in active plague outbreak; Isabel must enter a hot zone and expose herself to find her daughter
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Isabel finds Sofia — but Sofia is showing early symptoms; evacuation might be impossible; Isabel considers staying with her daughter to die
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Isabel is a disease expert. She examines Sofia's symptoms. It's not the plague — it's dengue fever, treatable, survivable. Her expertise saves Sofia before the first bomb falls.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Racing to the Key Biscayne extraction point as the first bombers arrive; Dr. Hale has arranged a boat but it's on the wrong side of a plague camp
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Isabel, Tommy, and Sofia escape by sea as Miami burns behind them. Quarantine holds. Isabel spends the rest of her life working to ensure no one ever has to make Rhodes's choice again.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Pandemics reveal what we're willing to sacrifice for safety
**Deep theme:** Expertise is a form of love — knowing how to save someone is as important as wanting to
**Thematic question:** If saving one child risks restarting a plague, should you do it anyway — and who decides?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Orlando Tunnels**: Moving through service corridors beneath Disney World while drones patrol above, emerging through Cinderella Castle's maintenance access.
2. **The Hot Zone Entry**: Isabel constructing an improvised hazmat suit from plastic and tape, entering a building full of the dying, searching room by room.
3. **The Miami Burn**: The horizon on fire as helicopters fly overhead, Isabel carrying Sofia through streets that will be ash in minutes.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Contagion, Children of Men
**Book comparables:** Station Eleven by Emily St. John Mandel, The Last of Us novelization

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Medical thriller meets maternal nightmare. The plague details are clinical and specific — Isabel's expertise filters everything through virology. The deadline structure provides relentless momentum. Quiet moments happen during transit when Isabel and Tommy develop their relationship. The reunion with Sofia is understated — survival first, emotion later. The ending is relief rather than triumph.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Spanish names: Isabel (ee-sah-BEL), Delgado (del-GAH-doh), Javier (hah-vee-AIR). Cuban Spanish phrases should be delivered naturally. Medical terminology is a normal part of Isabel's voice. Tommy is a Florida teenager — casual, quick, profane. Sofia is twelve and terrified but brave. The plague descriptions benefit from clinical distance. The emotional breakthrough scenes should slow down.
