# Blood Tide

## Genre
Post-Apocalyptic

## One-Line Premise
A Filipino marine biologist must convince warring island factions to evacuate before the toxic algal bloom that killed the oceans reaches their last clean water.

## Expanded Premise
The Red Tide began as an ecological nuisance; it became an extinction event. Toxic algal blooms spread across every ocean, killing fish stocks, poisoning coastal water, and starving billions. Dr. Carmen Reyes is a marine biologist who predicted the collapse and was ignored. Now, eight years later, she monitors the remaining clean zones from a research station in the Sulu Sea. When her instruments detect the bloom advancing toward the last viable fishing grounds — waters controlled by three factions who would rather fight than cooperate — Carmen must broker an impossible peace before the tide arrives and everyone dies.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Dr. Carmen Reyes
**Age:** 48
**Occupation / Background:** Marine biologist, former University of the Philippines professor, now director of the last oceanic research station; she's spent eight years watching the seas die
**Core skill:** Oceanic expertise and predictive modeling — she can read the water, track bloom movements, and understand marine ecosystems at a level that borders on intuition
**Fatal flaw:** Cassandra syndrome — she's spent so long being right and ignored that she's stopped trying to be diplomatic
**Character arc:** Believes scientific truth should be enough to convince people; discovers that people need hope and trust, not just data; learns to lead instead of lecture
**Voice and register:** Academically precise with growing frustration. She explains things as a teacher would, then gets angry when people don't listen. Her internal voice mourns constantly for the world she studied.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Red Tide itself and Captain Salvador "Sal" Ocampo, leader of the Tawi-Tawi fishing fleet
**Goal:** The bloom has no goal; it's ecology, not malice. Ocampo's goal is to maintain his fleet's dominance of the remaining fishing grounds — admitting the bloom is coming means admitting his power base is dying
**Method:** Ocampo controls the largest armed fishing fleet in the region; he's kept his people alive through combat, not cooperation, and sees Carmen's warnings as enemy propaganda
**Connection to protagonist:** Carmen's research in the old world classified Ocampo's fishing practices as destructive; she publicly called for restrictions; he hasn't forgotten

## Supporting Characters

**Father Mateo Cruz** — A Catholic priest who serves as neutral mediator between factions; he's dying of bloom exposure from a contaminated water supply and has months to live
**Datu Karim Hashim** — Leader of the Sama Bajau sea nomads, who want peace but have been attacked by all sides; he trusts Carmen because she's the only outsider who learned to speak their language
**Elena Reyes** — Carmen's daughter, a young woman who commands the research station's defense team; she's hardened where Carmen is academic

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Sulu Archipelago between the Philippines, Malaysia, and Indonesia
**Time period:** Eight years after the Red Tide began
**Atmosphere:** A world where the sea, once the source of life, has become poison. The water itself is often red-orange, choked with algae. Fish stocks are gone; seabirds are gone; coastal villages are abandoned. The remaining fishing grounds are fought over with the desperation of the starving. The beauty of the islands remains, making the death more terrible.
**Key locations:** The research station at Cagayan de Tawi-Tawi, Ocampo's fleet anchorage, the Sama Bajau floating village, the Jolo neutral zone, the approaching bloom front

## Central Conflict
Carmen must unite three factions who have spent years killing each other, convince them to abandon the only viable fishing grounds left, and lead an evacuation to a rumored clean zone in Indonesian waters — all within the seventeen days before the bloom arrives.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Three thousand people survive; they establish a new settlement in clean waters; humanity maintains a foothold on the ocean
**If the protagonist fails:** The factions stay and fight until the bloom arrives; everyone dies of starvation or toxin exposure; the Sulu Sea becomes a dead zone
**Personal stakes:** Carmen's daughter Elena wants to stay and fight for the research station; Carmen must convince her that some things can't be defended, only escaped

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Carmen monitors the bloom front when her instruments show a catastrophic acceleration; seventeen days until it reaches their waters
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Father Mateo proposes a peace summit; Carmen attends with data; Ocampo storms out when she presents the timeline
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Attempting to reach Datu Karim, Carmen's boat is attacked by Ocampo's patrols; she escapes using her knowledge of the currents

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Carmen allies with the Sama Bajau; they know the bloom is real because they've seen it; Karim agrees to evacuate if the others will
- Ch 5 — The Push: Father Mateo reveals Ocampo is dying of bloom exposure too — he's denying the threat because admitting it means admitting he's already dead
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Ocampo attacks the Sama Bajau village; Carmen's research station is caught in the crossfire; Elena is wounded
- Ch 7 — The Descent: With Elena in critical condition, Carmen considers abandoning the mission to save her daughter; the bloom advances to day ten
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Elena recovers enough to tell Carmen to finish what she started; Carmen confronts Ocampo alone, appealing not to his reason but to his legacy

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The evacuation fleet assembles as the bloom becomes visible on the horizon; Ocampo's hardliners attack; Carmen coordinates the defense while leading the evacuation
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The fleet escapes. Ocampo dies holding the rear against his own mutineers. They reach Indonesian clean waters. Carmen begins the work of building a community that doesn't need her to be Cassandra anymore.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Environmental collapse requires cooperation, not competition
**Deep theme:** Expertise is worthless without the ability to communicate; being right doesn't make you a leader
**Thematic question:** If you can see catastrophe coming and no one listens, is it still your responsibility to keep warning them?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Red Horizon**: Carmen on a boat as the bloom appears on the horizon, a wall of discolored water stretching as far as the eye can see, inexorable, patient, deadly.
2. **The Floating Village Attack**: Small boats weaving between stilt houses in a firefight, Carmen navigating by instinct while bullets perforate the wood around her.
3. **The Evacuation Fleet**: Hundreds of vessels moving in convoy, leaving their homes forever, as the water behind them turns red and everything left behind dies.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** The Impossible, Captain Phillips
**Book comparables:** The Water Will Come by Jeff Goodell (nonfiction), New York 2140 by Kim Stanley Robinson

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Ecological thriller with human focus. The science is specific enough to feel authentic but accessible. Carmen's internal monologue provides context for the disaster without becoming a lecture. The faction politics are complicated but clear. The pacing accelerates as the bloom approaches — each chapter timestamp increases urgency. The ending is bittersweet survival, not triumph.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Filipino names: Carmen (kar-MEN), Reyes (RAY-ess), Salvador (sal-vah-DOR), Ocampo (oh-KAHM-poh), Mateo (mah-TAY-oh), Karim (kah-REEM). Place names: Tawi-Tawi (TAH-wee TAH-wee), Cagayan (kah-gah-YAN), Jolo (HO-lo). Sama Bajau (SAH-mah BAH-zhow) sea nomad terms may appear. The water itself should feel present in the prose — lapping, current, tide. Carmen's frustration should build audibly. The evacuation sequence benefits from measured, solemn delivery.
