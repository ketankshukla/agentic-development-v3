# The Drowned Kingdom

## Genre
Post-Apocalyptic

## One-Line Premise
A Bangladeshi river pilot must navigate her flotilla through a monsoon-transformed megacity to rescue her children before the water reclaims everything.

## Expanded Premise
When accelerated climate collapse raised sea levels faster than anyone predicted, coastal Bangladesh became an archipelago overnight. Rashida Begum captains a flotilla of refugee boats, trading salvage and water purification for protection. When she learns her two children — sent to Dhaka for safety before the floods — are trapped in a skyscraper surrounded by rising water, she must navigate through drowned streets patrolled by pirate fleets and the corporate security forces that now rule the submerged districts from armored barges. She has three days before the next storm surge submerges their refuge permanently.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Rashida Begum
**Age:** 38
**Occupation / Background:** Former cargo ship pilot for the Dhaka port authority, now captain of a flotilla of seven boats carrying ninety-six refugees
**Core skill:** Hydrological intuition — she reads currents, tides, and weather patterns the way others read text, and can navigate drowned streets by memory and water behavior
**Fatal flaw:** Guilt — she sent her children away to save them, and that decision haunts every choice she makes
**Character arc:** Believes leadership means suppressing maternal instinct for the collective good; discovers that her fierce love for her children is what makes her fight for everyone's children; forgives herself for impossible choices
**Voice and register:** Commands with quiet authority, speaks Bengali with working-class directness. Her internal voice is poetic in unexpected moments, finding beauty in the drowned world even as she grieves it.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** The Dhaka Maritime Authority — a corporate consortium that claimed emergency powers and never relinquished them, now running the dry zones as authoritarian enclaves
**Goal:** Control all water purification and high-ground real estate, forcing refugees to work as salvage divers in exchange for drinking water
**Method:** Armed patrol boats, drone surveillance, and water taxation — they don't need to kill refugees, just control what they need to survive
**Connection to protagonist:** Rashida's husband was conscripted as a salvage diver and died when the Authority collapsed a building to prevent "unauthorized" salvage operations

## Supporting Characters

**Tariq Hassan** — A former university professor who serves as the flotilla's navigator and historian; he's mapping the drowned city and believes knowledge will outlast the Authority
**Rina Das** — A teenage girl who grew up on the water and serves as Rashida's most trusted pilot; she's never known dry land and doesn't mourn it
**Colonel Bashar** — A defected Authority officer who knows their patrol patterns; he's seeking redemption for atrocities he committed and will probably die earning it

## Setting

**Primary location:** Drowned Dhaka and the flooded Ganges Delta
**Time period:** 2055, eight years after the Acceleration
**Atmosphere:** A vertical world where the first three floors of every building are underwater and people live on rooftops connected by rope bridges. Monsoon rain is constant. The water is brown with silt and human waste, punctuated by floating debris and the occasional corpse. At night, cooking fires glow on rooftops like constellations. The sound is water — always water.
**Key locations:** The flotilla anchorage at Keraniganj (flooded industrial zone), the Authority's Motijheel fortress (former financial district), the Buriganga river rapids (new formation), the Uttara dry towers (refugee skyscraper complex), the Turag marshlands (pirate territory)

## Central Conflict
Rashida must infiltrate Authority-controlled waters, reach a flooded skyscraper, rescue her children, and escape before a predicted storm surge — all while protecting a flotilla that cannot survive three days without her leadership.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Her children survive, the flotilla gains Authority navigation charts that open new salvage territories, and Rashida's rescue proves the Authority can be defied
**If the protagonist fails:** Her children drown, the flotilla disperses without her leadership, and the Authority completes its monopoly on Delta territory
**Personal stakes:** Rashida's identity as a mother has been suspended for eight years — if her children die believing she abandoned them, she cannot survive the grief

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Rashida negotiates a tense trade with a pirate fleet when a smuggled radio message arrives — her children are alive but trapped on the 40th floor of a sinking tower
- Ch 2 — The Pull: The flotilla council votes against a rescue; Rashida leaves command to Tariq and takes a single boat with volunteer crew
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Entering Authority waters, Rashida's boat is stopped at a checkpoint; Colonel Bashar appears, offering passage in exchange for taking him along

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Rashida learns the Authority is deliberately flooding the Uttara towers to drive refugees into their labor camps; her children's building is next
- Ch 5 — The Push: Using Bashar's codes, they penetrate the inner cordon; Rashida climbs thirty floors of flooded stairwells to find her children alive
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Authority forces arrive during evacuation; Rina is captured, and Rashida must choose between escaping with her children or going back for her crew
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Rashida goes back. She reaches Rina but the boat is destroyed. They're trapped in the flood zone with the storm approaching.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Floating in wreckage, watching her children drift away on debris, Rashida makes peace with death — then sees Tariq's flotilla arriving against her direct orders

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The flotilla attacks the Authority patrol boats during the storm surge confusion; Rashida coordinates the assault from the water using salvaged radio
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The Authority cordon is broken. Rashida reunites with her children on the flotilla deck. But the towers are gone, and a thousand new refugees need boats that don't exist.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Climate catastrophe creates new worlds with new rules
**Deep theme:** Motherhood is not about protection but about teaching children to survive without you
**Thematic question:** When you can't save everyone, is choosing your own children heroism or betrayal?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Checkpoint Crossing**: Rashida's boat approaches an Authority gunship in driving rain, tension building as searchlights sweep the deck — resolved through Bashar's credentials and her perfect composure.
2. **The Tower Climb**: Rashida ascends forty floors through chest-deep water, collapsed stairwells, and occupied refugee levels, each floor a new negotiation or threat.
3. **The Storm Surge Battle**: Flotilla boats ramming Authority patrol craft as waves throw all vessels into chaos, with Rashida coordinating from floating wreckage using hand signals and whistle codes.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Waterworld, The Impossible
**Book comparables:** The Water Knife by Paolo Bacigalupi, The Windup Girl by Paolo Bacigalupi

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Urgent and visceral. The water is a constant presence — every scene involves navigating, fighting, or escaping it. Bengali cultural details are woven naturally into action. The maternal emotional core never becomes sentimental; Rashida is too practical for that. The pace is relentless, driven by the storm deadline, with emotional beats compressed into brief moments of stillness.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Bengali names require care: Rashida (rah-SHEE-dah), Begum (BAY-gum), Keraniganj (keh-RAH-nee-gonj), Buriganga (BOO-ree-gon-gah), Uttara (OOT-tah-rah), Turag (TOO-rahg). The water environment creates acoustic variety — echoes in flooded buildings, muffled sound underwater, rain overwhelming dialogue. Rashida's narrative voice should be warm but commanding. Storm sequences should feel overwhelming.
