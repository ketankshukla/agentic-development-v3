# 008 - The Jericho Frequency

## Genre
Techno-Thriller

## One-Line Premise
A deaf acoustic engineer discovers that a tech company's revolutionary hearing aid is actually a mind-control device already implanted in 12 million people.

## Expanded Premise
Maya Okafor lost her hearing at seven due to meningitis, and spent her life turning that loss into expertise. Now she's the world's leading researcher in auditory prosthetics. When she's invited to consult for Sonance Technologies, whose neural hearing implant has been hailed as miraculous, she expects to find revolutionary science. Instead, she finds a device that does far more than restore hearing—it can transmit subliminal commands directly to the auditory cortex. 12 million people already have the implant. In 72 hours, Sonance will broadcast the Jericho frequency, transforming users into unwitting agents. Maya is immune because she's deaf—and she's the only one who can stop it.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Maya Okafor
**Age:** 37
**Occupation / Background:** Nigerian-American acoustic engineer and researcher, professor at MIT; lost hearing to meningitis as a child in Lagos, immigrated to the U.S. at 10; developed cochlear implant improvements that restored partial hearing to thousands
**Core skill:** She understands sound at a level hearing people never can—she reads vibrations, analyzes waveforms, and thinks in frequencies
**Fatal flaw:** Her deafness-related isolation has made her fiercely independent to the point of refusing help even when she desperately needs it
**Character arc:** Begins believing her disability makes her an outsider who must prove herself alone; learns that her unique perspective is exactly what makes her capable, and that accepting help is not weakness
**Voice and register:** Precise, visual, and tactile. Her internal monologue describes the world in terms of what she can see and feel—lip-reading, vibrations through floors, the body language that hearing people never notice.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Dr. Leonard Cross, CEO and founder of Sonance Technologies
**Goal:** Cross believes humanity is on a path to self-destruction through polarization and conflict. The Jericho frequency won't control minds—it will synchronize them. He intends to create a unified humanity by eliminating disagreement.
**Method:** The neural implant includes hidden circuitry that, when activated, overrides the brain's resistance to suggestion. The Jericho broadcast will make 12 million people receptive to a unified ideology—Cross's ideology.
**Connection to protagonist:** Cross's wife was deaf and died waiting for an implant that could help her. He built Sonance in her memory—and his grief curdled into messianic delusion. Maya represents everything he couldn't save, and everything he's trying to transcend.

## Supporting Characters

**Dr. Benjamin Okonkwo** — Maya's cousin, a neurosurgeon who helped develop the implant's surgical procedure; discovers the hidden circuitry and becomes Maya's inside man
**Agent Sarah Chen** — FBI cybercrime specialist who investigates Sonance for corporate fraud and becomes Maya's reluctant ally
**Thomas Vance** — Sonance's head of security, who genuinely believes he's protecting a humanitarian project; physical antagonist to Maya

## Setting

**Primary location:** Boston, Massachusetts and San Jose, California
**Time period:** Present day
**Atmosphere:** The sterile campuses of hearing aid manufacturers where silence is treated as a disease. Research labs full of waveforms and frequencies. The vibrant Deaf community in Boston that Maya straddles but never fully joins. A world where sound is weaponized, and the only immune person is someone who can't hear it.
**Key locations:** MIT's auditory research center; Sonance Technologies headquarters in San Jose; the server farm in Nevada where the Jericho broadcast will originate; Maya's apartment in Cambridge, adapted for a deaf occupant; the Deaf community center where she finds unexpected allies.

## Central Conflict
Maya must expose Sonance's true purpose and disable the Jericho broadcast before it activates—while being unable to hear the very frequencies she's fighting against.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The Jericho broadcast is stopped, Sonance is exposed, and regulations are established to prevent neural implants from being used for control
**If the protagonist fails:** 12 million people become vectors for Cross's ideology, spreading it to others through ordinary social influence. Within a year, the number doubles. Within a decade, humanity is "unified" under Cross's vision—free will chemically suppressed.
**Personal stakes:** Maya's cousin Benjamin has the implant. Half her colleagues do. If she fails, everyone she knows becomes something other than themselves.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Maya is recruited by Sonance to review their implant's acoustic processing. Everything seems legitimate—until she notices the implant is drawing power from brain activity, not just its battery. That shouldn't be possible.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Maya secretly examines a removed implant and discovers the hidden circuitry. Before she can report it, her MIT lab is ransacked and her research destroyed. She contacts Benjamin, who confirms the implant does more than restore hearing.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Maya is confronted by Cross, who offers her a position as his chief scientist. She pretends to accept while secretly copying encrypted files. Vance's security team detects her intrusion. She escapes, but now she's being hunted.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Maya decrypts the files and discovers the Jericho frequency specifications. She contacts Agent Chen, who is investigating Sonance for financial crimes. Chen is skeptical of mind control but provides protection.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Benjamin sabotages Sonance's manufacturing facility, delaying the broadcast by 48 hours. Maya reaches the Nevada server farm with Chen's help. They begin preparing an electromagnetic pulse to destroy the broadcast equipment.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Vance captures Benjamin and threatens to activate his implant manually, turning him against Maya. Cross broadcasts a test signal—thousands of users in San Jose suddenly attend an impromptu rally, unaware of why they came. The demonstration proves Maya right—but also proves she can't stop it conventionally.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Maya's EMP plan fails—the server farm has hardened shielding. Benjamin is being transported to Cross's location. Chen is pulled off the case by superiors who've received "donations" from Sonance. Maya is alone, 24 hours from the broadcast.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Maya contacts the Deaf community center she's avoided for years. She explains everything. They don't hesitate—they're immune too. Together, they plan a direct assault on the broadcast facility using skills hearing people underestimate: visual communication, vibration detection, lip-reading at a distance.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Maya leads a team of deaf infiltrators into the server farm. They use sign language to coordinate silently. They feel the vibrations of approaching guards. They reach the broadcast room as the Jericho frequency begins transmitting—but Maya has modified the signal. Instead of mind control, it broadcasts white noise, overloading and destroying every implant's hidden circuitry.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Cross is arrested. Benjamin recovers from the overload with his mind intact. The destroyed circuitry leaves 12 million people with ordinary hearing implants—still able to hear, no longer vulnerable. Maya returns to MIT as a hero of the Deaf community she'd distanced herself from, finally accepting that her difference was always her strength.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Technology designed to help can be turned to harm
**Deep theme:** Disability is not deficiency—it is difference, and difference is strength
**Thematic question:** What do we lose when we try to eliminate human variation?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Rally Test**: Thousands of people walking zombie-like toward a park in San Jose, no one knowing why, while Maya watches from a rooftop, feeling the vibrations of their footsteps but hearing nothing.
2. **The Silent Infiltration**: The deaf assault team moving through the server farm in perfect coordination, communicating in sign language that guards can't intercept, feeling enemy positions through floor vibrations.
3. **The Frequency Override**: Maya in the broadcast room, surrounded by screens showing waveforms she can't hear, manually adjusting the signal while Cross pleads with her to understand his vision—a conversation in sign language between two people who process the world completely differently.

**Film comparables:** Sound of Metal (2019), Eagle Eye (2008)

## Tone and Pacing Notes
The prose emphasizes non-auditory senses: what Maya sees, feels, and intuits. Dialogue scenes include visual description of lip-reading and environmental vibration. The thriller pacing is standard, but the perspective is unique—the reader experiences the world as a deaf person does. The Deaf community is portrayed with respect and specificity; their eventual alliance is earned, not token.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Maya's internal monologue requires the narrator to convey visual and tactile focus. Sign language scenes should describe the signing rather than translate it instantly. Technical acoustic terminology—frequency, waveform, cochlear—must be clear. Cross's persuasive madness should be delivered with conviction, not camp. The silent infiltration sequence should emphasize the absence of sound cues the listener might expect.
