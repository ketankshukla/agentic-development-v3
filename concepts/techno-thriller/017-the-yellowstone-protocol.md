# 017 - The Yellowstone Protocol

## Genre
Techno-Thriller

## One-Line Premise
A USGS volcanologist discovers that a tech billionaire is attempting to trigger a controlled Yellowstone eruption to "reset" civilization, and she has three days to stop him without triggering the eruption herself.

## Expanded Premise
Dr. Maya Redhawk has spent her career warning that Yellowstone's supervolcano could erupt at any time. No one listened—until the seismometers started showing patterns that shouldn't exist. Someone is drilling into the magma chamber, inserting charges at precise intervals. An investigation reveals Alexander Novak, a tech billionaire convinced that climate change will end civilization within 30 years. His solution: trigger Yellowstone first, kill 90% of humanity quickly, and let survivors rebuild on a cooled planet. Maya must find and stop the drilling operation—but any intervention could set off the eruption Novak planned or an even worse uncontrolled one.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Dr. Maya Redhawk
**Age:** 47
**Occupation / Background:** Lakota-American volcanologist, lead scientist at USGS Yellowstone Volcano Observatory; raised on the Pine Ridge Reservation, first in her family to attend college; world authority on supervolcanic systems
**Core skill:** Understanding volcanic behavior—she can read seismic data the way musicians read sheet music
**Fatal flaw:** She's spent her career being ignored and dismissed; she's become so used to not being heard that she sometimes doesn't fight hard enough for what she knows
**Character arc:** Begins resigned to being Cassandra, always warning, never believed; learns to fight for her knowledge with everything she has, becoming the leader she always had the potential to be
**Voice and register:** Grounded, patient, with the cadence of someone who thinks in geological time. She explains complex phenomena simply because she's had to. Anger surfaces rarely but devastatingly.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Alexander Novak, tech billionaire and founder of Project Genesis
**Goal:** Novak has concluded, based on his own modeling, that climate change will cause civilization to collapse by 2055. He's decided to accelerate the collapse, triggering Yellowstone to kill most of humanity now and spare survivors the slow death of environmental degradation.
**Method:** Novak's engineers have drilled to the magma chamber through a private geothermal project. Shaped charges are being placed along the chamber's weakest points. In 72 hours, he'll trigger a controlled eruption—or what he believes will be controlled.
**Connection to protagonist:** Novak funded Maya's research for years. He used her data to plan the eruption points. She made his genocide possible without knowing it.

## Supporting Characters

**Agent James Okonkwo** — FBI geologist specialist who believes Maya's claims when no one else does; becomes her primary ally
**Dr. Chen Wei** — Chinese volcanologist collaborating with Maya; provides international pressure when American authorities hesitate
**Chief Thomas Crow Dog** — Maya's uncle, traditional spiritual leader on Pine Ridge; provides refuge and perspective when Maya must reconnect with what she's fighting for

## Setting

**Primary location:** Yellowstone National Park, Wyoming; Novak's compound in Montana; Pine Ridge Reservation, South Dakota
**Time period:** Present day
**Atmosphere:** The magnificent beauty of Yellowstone—geysers, hot springs, herds of bison—underlaid by the knowledge that this entire landscape is the surface of a bomb. The contrast between natural wonder and technological horror. Steam rising from the earth like breath.
**Key locations:** Yellowstone Volcano Observatory at Mammoth Hot Springs; Novak's private geothermal facility disguised as a green energy project; the caldera's subsurface drilling sites; a traditional sweat lodge on Pine Ridge where Maya finds clarity.

## Central Conflict
Maya must locate and disable Novak's charges without triggering the eruption—a process so delicate that any mistake means extinction-level disaster.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The charges are disabled, Novak is stopped, and Yellowstone returns to its normal dormancy; the near-miss drives international cooperation on supervolcano monitoring
**If the protagonist fails:** Yellowstone erupts. The initial blast kills everyone within 500 miles. Ash blankets North America, destroying agriculture. Global temperatures drop. Billions starve. Civilization, as Novak predicted, ends—just not the way he wanted.
**Personal stakes:** Maya's data made this possible. Her research was weaponized. If Yellowstone erupts, she will have contributed to the deaths of billions while trying to save them.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Maya detects anomalous seismic patterns beneath Yellowstone—too regular to be natural, too deep to be surface activity. Her requests for investigation are denied by supervisors who consider her alarmist.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Maya investigates independently, discovering drilling equipment where there shouldn't be any. She's caught by Novak's security and brought before him. He explains his plan, expecting her to understand. She doesn't.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Maya escapes Novak's compound and contacts Agent Okonkwo. The FBI believes her, but Novak has political connections—an immediate raid would tip him off. They have 72 hours to gather evidence and plan a surgical intervention.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Dr. Chen Wei arrives with international seismic data confirming Maya's readings. The evidence is undeniable. Maya and Okonkwo plan an infiltration of the drilling sites to disable the charges.
- Ch 5 — The Push: The team reaches the first drilling site and successfully disables several charges. But Novak has prepared for this—redundant triggers activate, and the timeline accelerates. 36 hours remain.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Novak captures Chen Wei and threatens to detonate immediately unless Maya surrenders. She complies to buy time. In captivity, she learns the full scope: 47 charges across the caldera, each capable of triggering cascading eruption.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Maya is imprisoned in Novak's facility as the final preparations begin. Okonkwo's team can't assault without risking detonation. Chen Wei is injured. Maya watches monitors showing magma pressure rising—her caldera, her life's work, weaponized against everything she loves.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Maya escapes using her knowledge of volcanic gases—venting hydrogen sulfide to knock out guards. She reaches Novak's control room and discovers the charges can be disabled, but only from the central hub. To reach it, she must cross the caldera floor during a seismic event that Novak is about to trigger.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Maya crosses the erupting caldera floor—steam vents exploding, the ground cracking beneath her—to reach the central hub. Novak pursues her, trying to trigger the full eruption manually. They confront each other at the hub. Maya disables the system as Novak argues that she's dooming humanity to a slower death. Her response: "That's not your choice to make."
- Ch 10 — Resolution: The caldera stabilizes. Novak is arrested. International protocols for supervolcano monitoring are established. Maya returns to Pine Ridge for a ceremony with her uncle, reconnecting with the land she's spent her life trying to understand and protect.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** The danger of geoengineering and technological hubris
**Deep theme:** No one has the right to decide extinction—that choice belongs to no individual, no matter how certain they are
**Thematic question:** If you could save humanity by killing most of it, should you? (The answer is no.)

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Caldera Discovery**: Maya descending into an unauthorized drilling site, finding charges positioned precisely where her own research indicated maximum vulnerability—the horror of seeing her work weaponized.
2. **The Caldera Crossing**: Maya traversing Yellowstone's erupting surface—steam geysers, collapsing ground, sulfurous air—to reach the control hub as the supervolcano awakens around her.
3. **The Control Room Confrontation**: Maya and Novak's final argument at the hub, monitors showing magma pressure, timer counting down, two visions of human survival in direct conflict.

## Comparable Titles

**Film comparables:** Dante's Peak (1997), 2012 (2009)
**Book comparables:** Supervolcano: Eruption by Harry Turtledove, The Swarm by Frank Schatzing

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Epic scale combined with personal stakes. The science is accurate—Yellowstone really is a supervolcano, and the described eruption consequences are realistic. Action sequences are visceral and grounded in physical reality. Maya's Indigenous background informs her relationship with the land without reducing her to stereotype. The theological debate with Novak should feel genuine—his logic is horrifying but internally consistent.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Lakota terms and names require respectful pronunciation. Scientific terminology—magma chamber, seismic activity, volcanic winter—should be delivered clearly. Novak's persuasive confidence should make his villainy more disturbing. The caldera crossing sequence requires intensity without losing geographic clarity.
