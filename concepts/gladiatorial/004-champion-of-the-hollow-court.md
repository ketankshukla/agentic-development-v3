# Champion of the Hollow Court

## Genre
Gladiatorial Dark Fantasy

## One-Line Premise
A mortal blacksmith must fight as the Fae Queen's champion in a tournament where gods gamble kingdoms and the losers become the winners' slaves.

## Expanded Premise
Cormac Blackthorn forged the finest blades in Ulster until the Fae took his daughter as a changeling. He followed through a fairy mound and demanded her back—and the Queen of the Hollow Court offered a bargain: become her champion in the Sevenfold Tournament, the once-in-a-century contest where the Fae courts gamble their territories and powers, and if he wins, his daughter goes free. But Cormac is mortal, fighting against immortal champions empowered by ancient magics. Each round is fought in a different realm with different rules. And the Queen has her own reasons for choosing a mortal champion—reasons that might doom Cormac regardless of whether he wins or loses.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Cormac Blackthorn
**Age:** 38
**Occupation / Background:** Master blacksmith of Armagh, widower, known for forging ceremonial blades for kings. His bloodline carries old magic—his grandmother was half-Fae, a truth his family buried.
**Core skill:** Metal intuition. He reads weapons the way others read faces. Any blade or armor reveals its maker's intentions, its hidden strengths and flaws. In a realm of magical weapons, he sees what even Fae smiths overlooked.
**Fatal flaw:** His love is a chain. His daughter is leverage. He will not sacrifice her for anything, and his enemies know it.
**Character arc:** Begins believing he can navigate Fae politics without becoming part of them. Discovers his heritage means he was never truly mortal. Ends having to choose between his daughter's freedom and his own humanity.
**Voice and register:** Rough-hewn, practical, with the patience of a man who shapes hot iron. He swears by old gods and thinks in forge-fire metaphors. His courtesy to Fae is careful and thin.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Prince Morvran of the Winter Court, champion of the rival Fae faction
**Goal:** To win the tournament for the Winter Court, which would grant them dominion over the Hollow Court's territories—including the mortal borderlands where Cormac's daughter lives.
**Method:** Ancient combat magic, political manipulation, and assassins who strike between tournament rounds. He considers mortals vermin and intends to prove it by crushing the Queen's "pet human."
**Connection to protagonist:** Morvran murdered Cormac's grandmother a century ago, ending the blacksmith bloodline's Fae ties. He doesn't know Cormac carries that blood—when he discovers it, the fight becomes personal.

## Supporting Characters

**Aisling Blackthorn** — Cormac's daughter, age 12, held in the Hollow Court as leverage and changeling. She has been in Fae realms for two years (two days mortal time). She is beginning to forget her mortal life.

**Brigid the Red** — A Fae warrior bound to serve as Cormac's trainer. She despises mortals but owes the Queen a debt. Her grudging respect for Cormac's determination becomes genuine loyalty.

**The Queen of the Hollow Court** — Never named, she is beautiful and ancient and utterly alien. Her motives are her own. She chose a mortal champion because mortality is the one thing Fae magic cannot account for.

## Setting

**Primary location:** The Hollow Court beneath Ireland, and the tournament grounds that exist between realms
**Time period:** 9th century CE, during the Viking Age, when mortal Ireland is contested and Fae power begins to wane
**Atmosphere:** The Fae realms are beautiful and wrong—colors too saturated, angles that don't add up, time flowing like honey. The courts are full of lethal courtesy, ancient grudges, and hungers that mortals cannot understand. The tournament grounds shift with each round—frozen wastes, burning mountains, underwater palaces.
**Key locations:** The Hollow Court's twilight halls, the Tournament Circle (a floating arena between worlds), the Winter Court's ice fortress, the Forge of First Fire (where Cormac must seek a weapon), the Changeling Garden where stolen children play forever.

## Central Conflict
Cormac must defeat six Fae champions in six different realms, each with their own rules and magics, while navigating court politics that could kill him before he ever enters the arena. The Queen protects him because she needs him to win—but her protection has limits, and her enemies are creative.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Aisling goes free, the Hollow Court maintains its power, and Cormac earns a place in Fae society—whether he wants it or not.
**If the protagonist fails:** Aisling remains Fae forever, forgetting her mortal life entirely. The Winter Court claims the borderlands. And Cormac's soul belongs to whoever struck the killing blow.
**Personal stakes:** His humanity. Every round in Fae realms changes him. He is becoming something other than mortal. If he wins, he may not be human enough to return to mortal lands.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Cormac discovers Aisling has been taken, follows the signs his grandmother taught him, enters the Hollow Court and demands his daughter. The Queen offers the bargain.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Cormac accepts, begins training with Brigid, learns the tournament rules—and learns that no mortal has ever survived past the third round.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: The first bout, against a minor court's champion. The fight is in a realm of perpetual storm. Cormac barely survives, winning through craftsmanship—he identifies the flaw in his opponent's enchanted armor and exploits it.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Morvran takes notice. Assassins come for Cormac between rounds. The Queen cannot protect him outside the arena. Brigid saves his life and begins teaching him Fae magic.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Rounds two and three. Cormac wins through cleverness and his unique ability to read weapons. He visits Aisling; she barely remembers him. He realizes he's running out of time.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Round four, against a nightmare champion from the Deep Courts. Cormac wins but is gravely wounded—and the Queen reveals that to heal him, she had to awaken his Fae blood fully. He will never be entirely mortal again.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Recovering, Cormac discovers the Queen's true plan: she needs him to kill Morvran specifically, to settle an ancient debt that has nothing to do with Aisling. He is a weapon, not a champion.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Cormac confronts the Queen. She admits everything—but the bargain stands. Win, and Aisling goes free. It doesn't matter why she chose him. He forges his own weapon in the Forge of First Fire, investing his mortality into the blade.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: The final round, against Morvran in the heart of Winter. A battle that reshapes the landscape. Cormac, now half-Fae, wielding a blade forged of his own mortal essence, fighting an immortal prince who remembers murdering his grandmother.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Cormac wins. Aisling is freed. But Cormac cannot return to mortal lands for more than a day at a time—his blood is too Fae now. He becomes a wanderer between worlds, seeing his daughter grow up in glimpses, belonging to neither realm.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** A father's fight for his child.
**Deep theme:** What we sacrifice of ourselves to save those we love—and whether they would want us to.
**Thematic question:** If saving your child means becoming someone she can no longer know, is it still salvation?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Storm Arena**: Lightning-forked combat on floating stones, rain like needles, thunder deafening every clash of blades.
2. **The Forge of First Fire**: Cormac forges his final weapon in primordial flames, hammering his mortality into steel, the last purely human thing he will ever do.
3. **Winter's Heart**: The finale in a frozen realm where breath freezes and shadows hunt, Cormac and Morvran circling each other in the cold, both wounded, both committed.

## Tone and Pacing Notes
The book moves between brutal arena combat and uncanny Fae court intrigue. Fight scenes are visceral and elemental—fire, ice, storm, stone. Court scenes are treacherous conversations where every word has double meaning. The prose should feel like a myth being told: cadenced, deliberate, with occasional brutal simplicity. Time in Fae realms is tricky; the narrative should occasionally skip or loop.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Cormac's voice should be rough and tired—a working man's voice. Fae names follow Irish pronunciation: "Aisling" is ASH-ling, "Morvran" is mor-VRAHN. The Queen should sound like music that's almost a voice. Gaelic terms should be kept minimal. Fight scenes need heavy, slow pacing—these are heavyweight bouts, not fencing.
