# Drowned Borough Games

## Genre
Gladiatorial Dystopian Fiction

## One-Line Premise
A former climate refugee must survive Manhattan's flooded death games to buy clean water rights for her starving commune—but the games are secretly engineered to eliminate the people most likely to lead a revolution.

## Expanded Premise
Forty years after the floods, Manhattan's skyscrapers rise from the water like tombstones. The wealthy live in the upper floors; the poor fight for scraps in the lower levels that flood with every tide. Once a year, the Drowned Borough Games draw millions of viewers: contestants race, swim, and fight through flooded subway tunnels and collapsed streets for corporate sponsorship prizes. Kira Ndungu's commune has three months of clean water left. She enters the Games planning to win the purification contract that will save her people. But as the Games progress, she discovers the real purpose: contestants are chosen not for their entertainment value but for their leadership potential. The corporations aren't giving away prizes—they're identifying and eliminating future threats.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Kira Ndungu
**Age:** 25
**Occupation / Background:** Climate refugee from drought-destroyed East Africa, now a scavenger and informal leader of a Staten Island commune. She was nine when her family drowned crossing the Mediterranean; she alone survived.
**Core skill:** Water sense. She's survived floods, tides, and drowning more times than she can count. She reads currents like others read faces, predicts when structures will flood, and moves through water like she belongs there.
**Fatal flaw:** Her guilt. She survived when others died, and every decision she makes is haunted by those she couldn't save. She overprotects people who should protect themselves.
**Character arc:** Begins believing the system can be navigated—win the game, save her commune, disappear. Discovers the system is designed to destroy people like her. Ends realizing the only way to save anyone is to expose the Games entirely.
**Voice and register:** Practical, observant, with the quiet watchfulness of someone who has learned to see threats early. She narrates in short, declarative sentences. She plans obsessively. She doesn't hope anymore—she calculates.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Clearwater Corporation, specifically their Games Director, Marcus Fontaine
**Goal:** To maintain control of the water supply by eliminating potential leaders among the refugee population. The Games are population control disguised as entertainment.
**Method:** Arena design that identifies and eliminates the strongest, most charismatic, most strategically minded contestants. Survivors receive prizes—but they're flagged for "accidents" later. No one who wins the Games lives more than a year.
**Connection to protagonist:** Clearwater was the corporation that rejected Kira's family's refugee application twenty years ago, consigning them to the Mediterranean crossing where most of them died.

## Supporting Characters

**Deshi** — A young programmer from the commune who uses scavenged tech to monitor the Games from outside. He's Kira's eyes and ears—and the first to notice the pattern of "accidents" among former winners.

**Tomas Reyes** — A former corporate security officer, now a contestant who claims he's competing for his sick daughter. He's either Kira's best ally or a Clearwater plant sent to identify her weaknesses. His combat skills suggest his cover story is a lie.

**The Archivist** — An anonymous figure who broadcasts pirate signals into the Games zones, providing contestants with information Clearwater wants hidden. They claim to be a former Games designer who wants revenge.

## Setting

**Primary location:** Flooded Manhattan, from Times Square (now a lake) to the subway tunnels (now aquatic death traps)
**Time period:** 2094 CE
**Atmosphere:** The water is everywhere—lapping at thirtieth-floor windows, surging through elevator shafts, carrying debris and worse. The upper floors glitter with corporate opulence; the lower levels are dark, drowned warrens where scavengers compete for scraps. The Games zones are lit with floating lights, turning the flood into a twisted spectacle.
**Key locations:** The Starting Grid (a floating platform in Central Park), the Subway Labyrinth (flooded tunnels with collapsing sections), the Midtown Killing Floor (an office building where contestants fight in flooding rooms), the Staten Island Commune, Clearwater's gleaming tower above the waterline.

## Central Conflict
Kira must survive ten days of Games while piecing together the truth about what Clearwater is really doing. But exposing the conspiracy means trusting other contestants—any of whom might be Clearwater plants—and the closer she gets to the truth, the harder Clearwater works to kill her specifically.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** Kira exposes the Games, Clearwater loses control, and the refugee communes gain leverage to demand fair water access.
**If the protagonist fails:** Her commune runs out of water and disperses, the Games continue harvesting leaders, and the refugee population remains permanently controlled.
**Personal stakes:** Kira's survivor guilt. If she exposes the Games but dies doing it, she becomes another martyr. She wants to live—and she's not sure she deserves to.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Kira leads a scavenging run through flooded buildings. The commune's water filter fails—irreparable. She returns to find the Games registration open.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Kira registers, passes the "qualification" tests (which are secretly personality evaluations). She meets Tomas, whose daughter supposedly needs treatment only a Games prize can buy.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Day one of the Games. A swimming race through flooded streets. Kira sees other contestants sabotaged by the "neutral" environment. She survives; others don't.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Days two and three. Combat rounds in the Subway Labyrinth. Kira forms a reluctant alliance with Tomas. Deshi messages her: the Archivist wants to meet.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Kira contacts the Archivist, receives evidence that previous winners died shortly after their victories. The pattern is undeniable. She keeps competing, gathering proof.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: The Archivist is captured during a broadcast. Their final message: the Games' real purpose is encoded in the arena design itself. Kira must reach the central control station—inside the Games zone—to access the files.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Kira tries to reach control. Tomas is revealed as a Clearwater asset—but he hesitates to stop her. His daughter is real; his crisis of loyalty is real. The Games become a hunt for Kira specifically.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: Trapped in the flooded lower levels, Kira broadcasts to Deshi what she's found. He can release it, but she needs to survive long enough for the upload to complete. She makes peace with possibly dying.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Kira fights her way through Clearwater's security contractors in the flooding tunnels. Tomas switches sides. The arena collapses around them as the broadcast goes out, reaching millions.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Kira survives. The Games are cancelled. Clearwater is weakened but not destroyed. The commune receives their water—for now. But Kira knows she's on a list. She begins organizing for the real fight to come.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** Survival against corporate oppression.
**Deep theme:** The systems that exploit the desperate make resistance feel impossible—and that feeling is the system's greatest weapon.
**Thematic question:** When the game is designed to kill you, is survival itself an act of revolution?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The Subway Surge**: Racing through flooded tunnels as a tidal wave pursues, the tunnel collapsing behind them.
2. **The Floating Combat**: Fighting on debris in a flooded skyscraper lobby, platforms sinking as combatants struggle.
3. **The Broadcast Fight**: Kira holding position while the upload completes, water rising, enemies closing, clock ticking.

**Book comparables:** *The Hunger Games* by Suzanne Collins, *Station Eleven* by Emily St. John Mandel

## Tone and Pacing Notes
Relentless and claustrophobic. The water is always present—rising, falling, carrying threat. Action sequences are physical and desperate, survival rather than spectacle. The prose should feel compressed, like breath held underwater. Moments of rest are brief and tense. The political conspiracy unfolds through fragments and implications.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Kira's voice should be calm and controlled—someone who has learned not to panic. "Ndungu" is en-DOON-goo. Water sounds should be evoked through description, not onomatopoeia. The Archivist's broadcasts can be processed/distorted in narration pacing. Tomas should sound like corporate security—clipped and professional—with cracks appearing as his loyalties fracture.
