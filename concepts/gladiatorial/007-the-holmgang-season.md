# The Holmgang Season

## Genre
Gladiatorial Historical Fiction

## One-Line Premise
A shieldmaiden must fight nine holmgang duels in nine days to prevent a blood feud from destroying her clan—while carrying the secret that her husband was murdered by her own brother.

## Expanded Premise
When Sigrid Haraldsdottir's husband is found dead, his family demands wergild—blood payment—from her clan. By law, her family must pay or face endless feud. But the price demanded is impossible: nine horses, nine slaves, nine pounds of silver. The only alternative is holmgang: ritual combat. If Sigrid can defeat nine champions chosen by the dead man's kin in nine consecutive days, the debt is cancelled. She is the greatest warrior in her clan. But she knows what no one else does: her brother Bjorn killed her husband, and if the truth emerges, her entire family will be outlawed and hunted. She must win impossible odds while protecting an unforgivable crime.

## Protagonist

**Name:** Sigrid Haraldsdottir
**Age:** 28
**Occupation / Background:** Shieldmaiden who fought in the Danelaw campaigns, now managing her family's farm in Iceland after returning home to marry. She was supposed to settle down. The sword had other plans.
**Core skill:** Battlefield endurance. She fights like a war, not a duel—designed to exhaust opponents, absorb punishment, and strike decisively when they falter. Nine duels in nine days is impossible; she's trained for worse.
**Fatal flaw:** Her loyalty. She will die before betraying her brother, even though she knows he deserves to answer for his crime.
**Character arc:** Begins protecting her brother out of family obligation. Discovers he killed her husband because she wouldn't leave with Bjorn to raid again—he wanted to control her. Ends having to choose between her family's survival and her brother's life.
**Voice and register:** Terse, practical, with gallows humour. She speaks like a soldier—short sentences, dark jokes, sudden poetry when the moment demands it. She prays to the old gods but doesn't expect them to answer.

## Antagonist or Antagonistic Force

**Name / Description:** Thorstein Eriksson, father of Sigrid's dead husband, the one demanding the holmgang
**Goal:** To destroy Sigrid's family through legal means—either through impossible payment or her death in the holmgang. He suspects the truth about Bjorn and will settle for nothing less than total ruin.
**Method:** Selecting the nine finest warriors in Iceland as his champions, manipulating the Thing (assembly) to ensure fair combat laws that favour his side, and investigating Bjorn's movements on the night of the murder.
**Connection to protagonist:** Thorstein arranged her marriage to his son specifically to bind her fighting skills to his family. Her husband's death robbed him of his investment. This is business as much as vengeance.

## Supporting Characters

**Bjorn Haraldsson** — Sigrid's younger brother, a berserker who returned from raiding with something broken inside him. He killed his brother-in-law in jealousy and rage, and Sigrid's protection of him is destroying her.

**Gunhild the Skald** — An elderly poet who wanders between farms, collecting saga material. She becomes Sigrid's witness and chronicler, and her presence protects Sigrid from assassination between duels. But Gunhild is composing the true saga—and will tell it eventually.

**Orm Ivarsson** — The seventh champion, a man who once fought beside Sigrid in the Danelaw. He does not want this fight. He knows her weaknesses—and she knows his.

## Setting

**Primary location:** Iceland, during the Althing (annual assembly) when legal disputes are settled
**Time period:** 985 CE, during the Icelandic Commonwealth's peak
**Atmosphere:** High summer in Iceland means endless daylight—there is no night to rest, no darkness to hide wounds. The Thing Plain is crowded with tents, disputes, trade, and entertainment. The holmgang ground is a rectangle of hide pegged to a hilltop where everyone can watch.
**Key locations:** The holmgang ground (the "island"), the family booth where Sigrid's clan camps, Thorstein's encampment, the Law Rock where judgments are pronounced, the hot spring where Sigrid treats her wounds between fights.

## Central Conflict
Sigrid must defeat nine warriors in nine days—any loss ends her family—while preventing Thorstein from proving Bjorn's guilt. But each day of fighting weakens her, and Bjorn is not staying hidden as she commanded.

## Stakes

**If the protagonist succeeds:** The blood debt is cleared, her family survives, and Bjorn's secret remains buried—unless she chooses to reveal it herself.
**If the protagonist fails:** Her family is destroyed by feud, Bjorn is certainly killed, and Sigrid dies with her reputation as her husband's protector intact—a lie she cannot live with but might die for.
**Personal stakes:** Sigrid's soul. Every duel she wins protects a murderer. If she dies, she dies for a lie. If she lives, she lives with the lie forever.

## Plot Skeleton

**Act One (Chapters 1-3):**
- Ch 1 — The Inciting World: Sigrid tends her farm, troubled by her loveless marriage. She finds her husband dead. Bjorn confesses to her privately—"He was going to sell you back to Thorstein." She sends him to hide.
- Ch 2 — The Pull: Thorstein arrives with his accusation. The Thing judges the wergild impossible to pay. Sigrid claims holmgang right—she will fight in her family's name. Nine duels against nine chosen champions.
- Ch 3 — First Contact: Day one. Her opponent is a young warrior seeking fame. She kills him in nine strokes, but she's been away from serious combat for years. Her body remembers—but nine days will test its memory.

**Act Two (Chapters 4-8):**
- Ch 4 — Escalation: Days two and three. Opponents increase in skill. Sigrid wins but takes wounds. Thorstein's men are seen near the mountains where Bjorn hides.
- Ch 5 — The Push: Days four and five. Sigrid's reputation spreads; Gunhild begins composing her saga. Victory seems possible. But Bjorn defies her orders and returns to the Thing—he cannot stay away from the attention.
- Ch 6 — The Reversal: Day six. Her opponent nearly kills her. She wins but is badly injured. That night, Thorstein confronts her: he has witnesses who saw Bjorn near the farm on the murder night. He will bring charges at the next Thing—unless she forfeits.
- Ch 7 — The Descent: Day seven. She can barely lift her shield. Her opponent (Orm, her old comrade) fights with reluctance. The duel is brutal, personal, full of what neither says. She wins but knows she cannot survive two more days.
- Ch 8 — The Reckoning: The night before day eight. Sigrid confronts Bjorn. He admits the truth: he didn't kill to save her—he killed because she chose farming over him. She owes him nothing. But if she reveals him, their parents die of shame. She prepares for the final duels.

**Act Three (Chapters 9-10):**
- Ch 9 — The Assault: Days eight and nine. Two duels, back to back, against Thorstein's finest champions. She fights beyond exhaustion, beyond reason, on pure will. The last fight ends with both combatants collapsed.
- Ch 10 — Resolution: Sigrid wins. The blood debt is cancelled. But in victory, she proclaims the truth: Bjorn killed her husband. She surrenders him to Thorstein. The family is shattered but not destroyed—she has paid a different debt. Gunhild's saga will tell it true: the woman who fought for her family and then betrayed them for justice.

## Thematic Core

**Surface theme:** A warrior's duty to family.
**Deep theme:** The lies we protect and the truths we can't survive.
**Thematic question:** Is loyalty to blood worth more than loyalty to truth?

## Cinematic Set Pieces

1. **The First Blood**: Day one's duel, establishing the holmgang rules and ritual—the hide boundaries, the three shields, the stakes.
2. **The Old Comrade**: The Orm duel, fought in exhausted silence, two people who should have been on the same side.
3. **The Final Stand**: Day nine, barely conscious, Sigrid blocks a killing blow with a shield-arm she can no longer feel.

## Tone and Pacing Notes
The book moves at the rhythm of combat and recovery—explosive duels followed by tense interludes of politics, healing, and investigation. The prose should feel sagas: spare, matter-of-fact about violence, with occasional kenning-like poetry. The nine-day structure creates relentless forward momentum. Pain is constant and cumulative.

## Edge TTS Narration Notes
Norse names follow Icelandic pronunciation: "Sigrid" is SIG-rith, "Bjorn" is BYORN. Keep the prose rhythm terse—short sentences land harder. Holmgang combat should be heavy and slow, the sound of wood and iron. The narrator's voice should be weary but unyielding, like Sigrid herself.
